Maratha confederacy — Scindia, Holkar, Gaikwad, Bhosale PREMAINSMH

Overview

The Maratha Confederacy was the decentralised polity that emerged in the 18th century as individual Maratha chiefs — Scindia (Gwalior), Holkar (Indore), Gaikwad (Baroda), Bhosale (Nagpur), and Peshwa (Pune) — developed semi-autonomous kingdoms under nominal Peshwa and Chhatrapati leadership. Together they controlled the largest territory in India before the British, yet their internal rivalries made them vulnerable. MPSC MH tag tests the five houses and the Anglo-Maratha wars context.

In-Depth

Formation of the Confederacy

The Maratha empire expanded rapidly under Peshwa Bajirao I (1720–1740) through a policy of delegating conquered territories to trusted Maratha chiefs. These chiefs maintained autonomous armies and governed their regions while owing nominal allegiance to the Peshwa at Pune:

Key Chiefs — Individual Contributions

Mahadji Scindia (1761–1794): The most powerful chief after Third Panipat:

  • Revived Scindia power after Panipat (where he was wounded but escaped).
  • Controlled Delhi and the nominal Mughal Emperor Shah Alam II (1784–1803) — the Emperor was effectively under Scindia protection.
  • Employed Benoit de Boigne (French military officer) to build a modernised infantry corps (Compoo) of 30,000 soldiers in European discipline.
  • Key figure in the Second Anglo-Maratha War (1803) origins — British General Lake defeated Scindia and captured Delhi (1803).

Ahilyabai Holkar (1767–1795): Regent of Indore after husband Malerao's death:

  • One of the most revered rulers in Indian history — known for justice, temple construction, and administration.
  • Built/restored temples across India including Kashi Vishwanath (Varanasi), Somnath (Gujarat), Gaya; constructed dharamshalas on pilgrimage routes.
  • Ruled Indore with great efficiency; promoted trade; known as "Lokamata" (Mother of the People).

Yashwantrao Holkar (1797–1811): Most militarily capable Holkar chief:

  • Defeated Scindia and Peshwa's combined forces at Battle of Poona (Pune) 1802 — forcing Peshwa Baji Rao II to flee.
  • This led Peshwa Baji Rao II to sign the Treaty of Bassein (1802) with the British — the proximate cause of the Second Anglo-Maratha War and ultimate British control over the Marathas.

Raghuji Bhosale (Nagpur): Extended Maratha power into Orissa and Bengal (1740s) — collected chauth from Bengal nawabs.

Internal Rivalries — The Fatal Flaw

The confederacy's weakness was constant inter-chief rivalry:

  • Scindia vs Holkar for control of north India (battles at Lakheri 1793; Pune 1802).
  • Peshwa intrigues against individual chiefs and vice versa.
  • Nana Fadnavis (the wily minister, 1741–1800) kept the confederacy together through diplomacy for decades but after his death (1800) it rapidly disintegrated.
  • Each chief made separate treaties with the British; no unified front.

Anglo-Maratha Wars Context

Three Anglo-Maratha wars ended the confederacy:

  • First (1775–1782): British supported Raghunathrao's claim; ended inconclusively.
  • Second (1803–1805): British defeated Scindia and Nagpur Bhosale; took Delhi; Treaty of Surji-Anjangaon (Scindia) and Deogaon (Bhosale).
  • Third (1817–1818): British crushed Peshwa, Holkar, Nagpur Bhosale; Peshwa abolished; Pune annexed; Nagpur fell; end of Maratha confederacy.

Maharashtra Connection

The confederacy map is largely Maharashtra + surrounding territories:

  • Pune = Peshwa capital (historical centre of Maharashtra).
  • Nagpur = Bhosale capital — today's largest city in Vidarbha, Maharashtra; Nagpur's Bhosale dynasty history is a major MPSC topic.
  • Ahilyabai Holkar — born at Chondi, Ahmednagar (Maharashtra) — her Holkar lineage was Maratha from Maharashtra even though they ruled Indore.
  • The Third Anglo-Maratha War ended at battles mostly in Maharashtra — Koregaon (near Pune), Kirkee (Pune), Nagpur.

Key Facts

  • Five houses: Peshwa (Pune), Scindia (Gwalior), Holkar (Indore), Gaikwad (Baroda), Bhosale (Nagpur).
  • Mahadji Scindia: controlled Mughal Emperor Shah Alam II (1784); employed French officer Benoit de Boigne.
  • Ahilyabai Holkar: ruled 1767–1795; born at Chondi (Ahmednagar); restored Kashi Vishwanath temple.
  • Yashwantrao Holkar: defeated Peshwa at Pune (1802) → Peshwa signed Treaty of Bassein (1802) with British.
  • Nana Fadnavis: key diplomat who held the confederacy together till 1800.
  • First Anglo-Maratha War: 1775–1782; Second: 1803–1805; Third: 1817–1818.
  • Confederacy ended: 1818 with British victory.

Exam Traps

  • Scindia capital = Gwalior (shifted from Ujjain); don't say Ujjain for Scindia's main capital.
  • Ahilyabai Holkar = Indore (Holkar), NOT Gaikwad — confusion between the two female rulers (another female ruler: Tarabai of Bhosale).
  • Nana Fadnavis ≠ Nana Phadnavis the Peshwa — Nana Fadnavis was a minister/diplomat, not a Peshwa; he never held that title.
  • Treaty of Bassein (1802) is between Peshwa Baji Rao II and British EIC — not between Scindia and British.
