Rajarshi Shahu Maharaj — reservation, reforms PREMAINSINTMH

Overview

Rajarshi Chhatrapati Shahu Maharaj (1874–1922) was the Maharaja of Kolhapur Princely State and a towering social reformer who introduced the first official reservation policy in India in 1902 — 50% reservation for non-Brahmin and backward classes in Kolhapur state services. He patronised Dalit education, supported Jyotiba Phule's movement and young Dr. Ambedkar, and is celebrated as the "Father of Social Democracy" in Maharashtra. MPSC tests his name, the 1902 reservation, his support for Phule and Ambedkar, and his full name.

The 1902 Reservation Order

  • 26 July 1902: Shahu Maharaj issued an order providing 50% reservation for non-Brahmin and backward class communities in all government posts and education in the Kolhapur state.
  • This was 26 years before the Indian Constitution was even drafted — and well before any national-level reservation discussion.
  • Rationale: Shahu Maharaj observed that ~99% of government posts in Kolhapur were held by Brahmins while OBCs and untouchables were entirely excluded. He wanted to break this monopoly.
  • He also opened hostel and boarding schools for backward-class students; personally funded many students' education.
  • Key hostel: Opened separate boarding houses for different backward communities (Mali, Kumbhar, Nhavi, Shimpi, Dhor etc.) in Kolhapur town.

Connection with Phule and Ambedkar

  • Jyotiba Phule: Shahu was influenced by Phule's Satyashodhak Samaj and identified as a follower of Phule's mission of social equality.
  • Dr. B.R. Ambedkar: Shahu Maharaj met the young Ambedkar (then a student in London) and personally funded Ambedkar's higher education in London — a critical act of patronage that enabled India's Constitution-maker to complete his studies. He also supported Ambedkar's early work.
  • The Phule-Shahu-Ambedkar triad is the official social reform pantheon of Maharashtra — their portraits are placed together in government offices, court halls, and schools across Maharashtra.

Legacy in Maharashtra

  • Kolhapur: Shahu's capital; Kolhapur is famous for wrestling (kushti), which Shahu popularised without caste restrictions — wrestlers of all castes could compete equally.
  • Shahu Chhatrapati Sugar Mill and various institutions in Kolhapur carry his name.
  • Rajarshi Shahu Jayanti: 26 June — observed across Maharashtra.
  • OBC reservation: Maharashtra's and India's OBC reservation frameworks draw intellectual lineage from Shahu's 1902 experiment.

Maharashtra Connection

The Phule-Shahu-Ambedkar trinity is the founding intellectual trio of Maharashtra's social democracy — enshrined in the state's constitutional imagination. Kolhapur's identity as a progressive, wrestling-loving city with egalitarian traditions is directly Shahu's legacy. The 50% reservation for OBCs in Kolhapur (1902) predated India's Constitution by 48 years — a favourite MPSC fact.
