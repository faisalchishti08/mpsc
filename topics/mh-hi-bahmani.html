Bahmani and Deccan sultanates PREMAINSINTMH

Overview

The Bahmani Sultanate (1347–1527) was the first independent Muslim kingdom of the Deccan, with capital at Gulbarga (Bidar) in present-day Karnataka — but its territorial control extended deep into Maharashtra (Bidar, parts of Marathwada). Its breakup gave rise to the Five Deccan Sultanates: Bijapur (Adil Shahi), Ahmadnagar (Nizam Shahi), Golconda (Qutb Shahi), Bidar (Barid Shahi), and Berar (Imad Shahi) — all of which fought amongst themselves and then against the Mughals and Marathas. This period is critical for MPSC Maharashtra medieval history.

Ahmadnagar (Nizam Shahi) — Most Maharashtra-Relevant

  • Founded: 1490 by Malik Ahmad Nizam Shah (built Ahmadnagar city from scratch).
  • Chand Bibi (Chand Sultana): Regent-queen of Ahmadnagar; defended Ahmadnagar Fort against Mughal forces under Prince Murad (Akbar's son) in 1595 — became a symbol of Deccan resistance. She was later killed by her own courtiers (1599).
  • Malik Ambar (d. 1626): Ethiopian (Habshi) slave-turned-Prime Minister of Ahmadnagar; brilliant administrator and military tactician; repeatedly frustrated Mughal campaigns into the Deccan under Jahangir; introduced ryotwari revenue assessment system (precursor to later British reforms); builder of Khirki (Aurangabad).
  • Ahmadnagar Sultanate was finally absorbed by the Mughals in 1636 (under Shah Jahan) — its territory became the Mughal Deccan Subedar's domain.

Battle of Talikota (1565)

  • The combined forces of Bijapur (Adil Shahi), Bidar, Golconda, and Ahmadnagar (the "Alliance of Deccan Sultanates") defeated the Vijayanagara Empire at Talikota (Rakhasitangdi) on 23 January 1565 — effectively ending the Vijayanagara Empire.
  • Aftermath: Vijayanagara capital Hampi was looted and destroyed; Deccan sultanates became the undisputed power in the south.

Bijapur (Adil Shahi) and Afzal Khan

  • Afzal Khan: General of Bijapur (Adil Shahi) sent against Shivaji; killed by Shivaji at Pratapgad in 1659 — this battle is covered in mh-hi-shivaji.
  • Gol Gumbaz (Bijapur): Mausoleum of Sultan Muhammad Adil Shah; famous for the "Whispering Gallery" — dome creates circular echo; one of the world's largest domes.

Maharashtra Connection

Ahmadnagar district is named after the Nizam Shahi sultanate capital. Malik Ambar's revenue reforms influenced later Maratha revenue practice. The Berar Sultanate capital — Ellichpur (Achalpur, Amravati district) — is important for Vidarbha history. The Deccan sultanates' conflicts created the political vacuum and the experience of coalition warfare that Shivaji later exploited. Chand Bibi is one of the few medieval women leaders tested in MPSC.

Key Facts

  • Bahmani founded: 1347 by Hasan Gangu (Alauddin Bahman Shah); capital: Gulbarga → Bidar
  • Five successor states after Bahmani break-up: Bijapur (Adil Shahi), Ahmadnagar (Nizam Shahi), Golconda (Qutb Shahi), Bidar (Barid Shahi), Berar (Imad Shahi)
  • Ahmadnagar (Maharashtra): Founded 1490 by Malik Ahmad Nizam Shah
  • Chand Bibi: Defended Ahmadnagar vs. Mughals, 1595
  • Malik Ambar: Habshi PM of Ahmadnagar; defeated Mughals under Jahangir; ryotwari system
  • Battle of Talikota: 23 January 1565 — Deccan sultans defeated Vijayanagara
  • Berar capital: Ellichpur (Achalpur, Amravati); absorbed by Ahmadnagar 1574

Exam Traps

  • Bahmani capital was Gulbarga first, then Bidar (moved 1430s) — not Bijapur or Ahmadnagar.
  • Chand Bibi was regent of Ahmadnagar (Nizam Shahi), NOT Bijapur (Adil Shahi).
  • Battle of Talikota is also called Battle of Rakhasitangdi / Bannihatti — all three names refer to the same 1565 battle.
