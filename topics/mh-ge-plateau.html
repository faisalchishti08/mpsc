Deccan plateau and Maharashtra plateau PREMAINSINTMH

Overview

The Deccan Plateau covers over 75% of Maharashtra's area, lying east of the Sahyadri. Within Maharashtra, it is often called the Maharashtra Plateau — a vast, gently undulating lava-derived landscape of black cotton soil (regur) formed by ancient Deccan Trap basalt flows. It is the agricultural and demographic heartland of the state and the setting for most of Maharashtra's major rivers, cities, and historical events.

In-Depth

Formation — Deccan Trap Basalt

  • The Maharashtra Plateau is underlain by Deccan Trap volcanic rocks (flood basalts), formed by massive lava flows approximately 65–66 million years ago (at the Cretaceous–Paleogene boundary, possibly triggered by the Chicxulub impact and the Réunion mantle plume).
  • These basalt flows cover an area of ~500,000 sq km across the Deccan, including most of Maharashtra, northwest Karnataka, and parts of MP and Gujarat.
  • Weathering of basalt produces black cotton soil (regur) — Maharashtra's most important agricultural soil (discussed separately in mh-ge-soils).

Drainage Pattern

  • The Sahyadri acts as the main watershed: rivers west of it (Konkan rivers) drain into the Arabian Sea; rivers east flow across the Deccan Plateau toward the Bay of Bengal (via Godavari) or the Arabian Sea (via Krishna–Gulf of Khambhat route, though Krishna ends in Bay of Bengal).
  • Major Deccan rivers: Godavari, Bhima, Krishna, Tapi, Wardha, Wainganga, Penganga.
  • The plateau has a dendritic drainage pattern east of the Sahyadri.

Economy of the Plateau

  • Agriculture: Sugarcane (Pune, Kolhapur, Satara — cooperative sugar mills); Cotton (Vidarbha, Marathwada); Jowar/Bajra (dryland); Grapes (Nashik — India's wine capital); Oranges (Nagpur, Vidarbha); Onion (Nashik — Maharashtra is India's top onion producer).
  • Industry: Pune (automobiles — Tata Motors, Bajaj, Mercedes-Benz India, Volkswagen India); Nashik (wine, grapes, CIDCO town); Nagpur (logistics, MIHAN — Multi-modal International Cargo Hub); Aurangabad (Bajaj Auto, Aurangabad Industrial area).
  • Water scarcity: Unlike the Konkan, the plateau receives lower rainfall (600–1,000 mm/year) and is prone to drought, especially in Marathwada and northern Vidarbha.

Significant Geographical Features

  • Lonar Lake (Buldhana district): A meteor impact crater lake (~52,000 years old), one of only four hyper-saline impact craters in the world; declared a Wildlife Sanctuary (2000) and a Ramsar site (2020); unique saline-alkaline ecosystem.
  • Ajanta and Ellora Caves (UNESCO WH): Carved into the Deccan basalt of the Sahyadri foothills (Aurangabad area); Buddhist, Jain, Hindu cave temples.

Maharashtra Connection

The Deccan Plateau's black cotton soil gave Maharashtra its "cotton kingdom" identity in the British era — Mumbai (then Bombay) emerged as the cotton trade hub, and the plateau's cooperative sugar economy (post-Independence) built political power in western Maharashtra. Nashik's wine industry (Sula Vineyards, since 1999) has made the Maharashtra Plateau internationally visible in a new way. MIHAN (Nagpur): Multi-modal International Cargo Hub and Airport — a flagship SEZ/logistics park targeting Nagpur's central location on the plateau.

Key Facts

  • Plateau covers: >75% of Maharashtra's area
  • Lonar Lake: meteor impact crater, Buldhana district; Ramsar site (2020); saline-alkaline
  • Maharashtra plateau elevation: 200–900 m (rises toward Sahyadri)
  • Rain shadow effect: Deccan gets 600–1,000 mm/year vs. Konkan's 2,000–5,000 mm
  • Nashik: India's wine capital (Sula Vineyards); also top onion producer district
  • MIHAN: Nagpur — Multi-modal International Cargo Hub + SEZ
