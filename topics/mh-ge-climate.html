Climate and rainfall regions PREMAINSINTMH

Overview

Maharashtra's climate is shaped by its position in the tropical monsoon zone and the strong influence of the Sahyadri (Western Ghats), which creates a dramatic rainfall contrast between the wet Konkan coast and the drier Deccan plateau. Understanding Maharashtra's four climate zones, monsoon patterns, temperature ranges, and drought-prone areas is essential for MPSC Geography and also for environmental and policy questions.

Monsoon in Maharashtra

  • SW Monsoon (June–September): Maharashtra receives rainfall from the Arabian Sea branch of the SW monsoon.
  • Onset: Maharashtra receives monsoon around 5–10 June (Kerala onset ~1 June; reaches Maharashtra coast 7–10 June; Vidarbha gets it by ~15–20 June).
  • Orographic rainfall: The Sahyadri forces moist air upward, causing heavy rainfall on the western (windward) slope — up to 6,000 mm at Mahabaleshwar. The eastern (leeward) side is a rain shadow.
  • Rain shadow districts: Solapur, Osmanabad (Dharashiv), Latur, Beed, Jalna — typically 400–600 mm/year; most drought-prone.
  • Withdrawal: SW monsoon withdraws from Maharashtra by October.
  • NE Monsoon: Does not significantly affect Maharashtra (impacts Tamil Nadu, southern AP more).

Temperature Patterns

  • Hottest zone: Vidarbha — Chandrapur, Akola, Yavatmal regularly record 44–48°C in May; known as the "hot zone" of India; Akola has recorded ~47°C.
  • Coldest: Mahabaleshwar in winter can go below 5°C; Nashik, Pune have cold waves below 10°C.
  • Mumbai: Moderate temperature year-round (22–35°C); humidity the discomfort factor, not temperature extremes.
  • Nagpur: Continental character — hot summer (42–45°C), cool winter (8–12°C min); highest diurnal range in Maharashtra.

Drought in Maharashtra

  • Drought-prone districts (officially notified): Nashik, Dhule, Nandurbar, Aurangabad, Jalna, Beed, Osmanabad, Latur, Nanded, Parbhani, Hingoli, Solapur — primarily Marathwada and parts of Nashik division.
  • Maharashtra declared drought annually in some districts during deficient monsoon years.
  • Maharashtra Remote Sensing Application Centre (MRSAC) and IMD Pune office monitor drought.
  • NDVI (Normalized Difference Vegetation Index) from satellite data is used to assess drought severity.

Cyclones

  • Maharashtra's Konkan coast is occasionally affected by Arabian Sea cyclones — more frequent in October–November (post-monsoon season). In recent years: Tauktae (May 2021) caused severe damage to Ratnagiri, Sindhudurg, Raigad coasts; a rare pre-monsoon super-cyclone.

Rainfall Anomalies and Climate Change

  • Mahabaleshwar (~6,000 mm): One of the highest rainfall stations in India outside the North-East; driven by orographic uplift.
  • Least rainfall in MH: Solapur district regularly records below 600 mm — among the driest in the state.
  • Microclimate of Pune: The Pune Basin (enclosed by Sahyadri on three sides) receives only ~700 mm but is insulated from extreme temperatures — historically called a "pleasant climate city."

Maharashtra Connection

Cyclone Tauktae (2021) made landfall near Una (Gujarat) but caused catastrophic pre-landfall damage on Maharashtra's Konkan coast — a current-affairs topic. IMD Pune (Indian Meteorological Department, Pune) is the nodal weather forecasting centre for Maharashtra and is HQ for SW monsoon prediction for the country. Marathwada's chronic drought feeds farmer distress, debt waiver politics, and water-tanker supply chains — all MPSC policy topics.

Key Facts

  • Highest rainfall: Mahabaleshwar (~6,000 mm) — one of India's top rainfall stations
  • Rain shadow (driest): Solapur and Marathwada (<600 mm)
  • Hottest zone: Vidarbha (Chandrapur, Akola — ~47–48°C max)
  • SW monsoon arrives: ~7–10 June (coast); ~15–20 June (Vidarbha)
  • Drought-prone districts: Marathwada (8) + Nashik division (3+) + Solapur
  • IMD Pune: National SW monsoon forecasting HQ
  • Cyclone Tauktae: May 2021 — severe Konkan coast damage

Exam Traps

  • Mahabaleshwar gets most rain due to orographic uplift (Sahyadri), NOT because it's coastal.
  • NE monsoon does NOT significantly affect Maharashtra — Marathwada's October rains are still SW monsoon withdrawing, not NE monsoon.
  • Solapur is the driest district in western MH, but Marathwada districts (Osmanabad, Latur) may be drier in some years — rainfall varies annually.
