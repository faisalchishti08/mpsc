Buddhist councils and schools (Hinayana/Mahayana/Vajrayana) PREMAINS

Overview

The Buddhist councils were key assemblies of monks that compiled, standardised, and debated the Buddha's teachings (Dhamma) and monastic rules (Vinaya). Four major councils are recognised; the first two are widely accepted, the third is associated with Ashoka, and the fourth is contested (different traditions recognise different events). The councils also mark the splitting of Buddhism into its major schools — Hinayana (Theravada), Mahayana, and Vajrayana. MPSC tests on the councils' locations, dates, presiding monks, and the philosophical differences between the major schools.

The Major Schism — Hinayana vs Mahayana

Vajrayana (Tantric Buddhism)

  • Third major school; emerged ~4th–5th c. CE; fully developed by 7th c. CE.
  • Also called Mantrayana or Tantrayana.
  • Incorporated tantric ritual, mantras, mandalas, and esoteric practices for faster liberation.
  • Dominated in Tibet (Tibetan Buddhism), also spread to Nepal, Bhutan, Mongolia.
  • Key in Nalanda and Vikramashila (Bihar) — late period.
  • Influential in Pala dynasty patronage (~8th–12th c. CE).

Key Philosophical Schools

  • Madhyamaka (Sunyavada): Founded by Nagarjuna (~2nd c. CE); core concept = Sunyata (emptiness/voidness) — all phenomena are empty of inherent existence; the Middle Way between existence and non-existence. Nagarjuna's key text: Mulamadhyamakakarika.
  • Vijnanavada (Yogachara): Founded by Asanga and Vasubandhu (~4th–5th c. CE); "Mind-only" school — only consciousness exists; external reality is a projection of mind.

Nalanda University — Buddhist Learning Centre

  • Founded ~5th c. CE (during Gupta period); flourished ~450–1200 CE.
  • Burned/destroyed by Bakhtiyar Khilji (~1193 CE) — major blow to Buddhism in India.
  • Over 10,000 students from India, China, Korea, Tibet, Java; subjects: Mahayana Buddhism, Vedas, logic, grammar, medicine.
  • Famous teachers: Nagarjuna, Dinnaga, Dharmakirti, Silabhadra; famous alumni: Xuanzang (Chinese pilgrim).

Maharashtra Connection

  • Maharashtra's Buddhist caves (Ajanta, Karla, Kanheri, Nashik/Pandu Lena) reflect Hinayana phase primarily (~2nd c. BCE–4th c. CE) — rock-cut chaityas and viharas for monks, funded by Satavahana merchants and kings.
  • Ajanta later phases (5th–6th c. CE, Vakataka patronage) show Mahayana influence — bodhisattva images, elaborate devotional art.
  • Ambedkar's Navayana Buddhism (1956, Nagpur) is distinct from all three traditional schools — a socially engaged, casteism-fighting interpretation emphasising the Buddha's rational and egalitarian teachings.

Key Facts

  • 1st Council: Rajgriha (~483 BCE), Ajatashatru patron, Mahakassapa presides.
  • 2nd Council: Vaishali (~383 BCE), Kalasoka patron; Sthavira-Mahasanghika split.
  • 3rd Council: Pataliputra (~250 BCE), Ashoka patron, Moggaliputta Tissa presides; Tripitaka complete.
  • 4th Council (Mahayana): Kashmir (~72 CE), Kanishka patron, Vasumitra presides.
  • Hinayana/Theravada: Arhat ideal; Pali; South/SE Asia.
  • Mahayana: Bodhisattva ideal; Sanskrit; Central/East Asia.
  • Nagarjuna: Madhyamaka/Sunyata, ~2nd c. CE.
  • Nalanda destroyed: Bakhtiyar Khilji, ~1193 CE.

Memory Aids

  • Councils: "R-V-P-K" = Rajgriha, Vaishali, Pataliputra, Kashmir.
  • "Ajatashatru, Kalasoka, Ashoka, Kanishka" — the four royal patrons.
  • Hinayana = "A for Arhat"; Mahayana = "B for Bodhisattva."
  • Nagarjuna = "Naga-rjuna = emptiness snake" (Sunyata).

Exam Traps

  • "Hinayana" is a Mahayana-coined derogatory term; Theravada prefer "Theravada" — MCQs may use either.
  • The 4th Council has two versions — Kanishka's (~72 CE) is Mahayana; the Ceylon council (~29 BCE) is Theravada. MPSC may specify which.
  • Nalanda was destroyed by Bakhtiyar Khilji (~1193 CE), NOT by Mahmud of Ghazni (common confusion).
  • Nagarjuna is a 2nd c. CE Buddhist philosopher, NOT the 1st c. BCE Satavahana king Nagarjuna.

Questions

Prelims (MCQ-style):

  1. [Asked] The Third Buddhist Council was held at Pataliputra under the patronage of? — Ashoka (Maurya Emperor)
  2. [Expected] Which school of Buddhism upholds the Bodhisattva ideal — the postponement of one's own Nirvana to help all beings? — Mahayana
  3. [Expected] Nagarjuna's philosophical doctrine of 'Sunyata' (emptiness) belongs to which Buddhist school? — Madhyamaka (Sunyavada)
  4. [Expected] The First Buddhist Council was held at Rajgriha under the patronage of which king? — Ajatashatru (Haryanka dynasty, Magadha)

Mains (descriptive):

  1. [Expected] Trace the development of Buddhism from the Hinayana to Mahayana and Vajrayana phases, noting the key doctrinal differences.
  2. [Expected] Describe the Buddhist councils and their role in preserving and standardising Buddhist teachings.
  3. [Expected] Discuss the philosophical contributions of Nagarjuna to Buddhist thought.
