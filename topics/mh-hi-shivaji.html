Shivaji Maharaj — administration, forts, navy PREMAINSINTMH

Overview

This is the Maharashtra-specific entry for Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj (1630–1680) — complementing the general history entry at hist-mar-shivaji. It focuses on his administration, forts, navy, and lasting imprint on Maharashtra's identity. Shivaji is Maharashtra's defining historical figure — MPSC consistently tests administrative details (Ashtapradhan, revenues), naval achievements (Sindhudurg), fort heritage, and his legacy in modern Maharashtra.

In-Depth

Administrative System

  • Ashtapradhan (Council of Eight Ministers): Peshwa (PM/Chief Minister), Amatya (Finance), Sachiv (Secretary), Mantri (Political Advisor), Senapati (Army chief), Sumant (Foreign affairs), Nyayadhish (Chief Justice), Panditrao (Religious head).
  • Revenue system: Reformed the existing system; eliminated the Deshpande-Deshmukh intermediaries for direct collection; land measured using Kaathi (a measuring rod); introduced tankha (fixed assessment).
  • Chauth: 1/4 of revenue collected from non-Swaraj territories as protection money.
  • Sardeshmukhi: Additional 1/10 collected based on Shivaji's claim as hereditary Sardeshmukh of the Deccan.
  • Language: Promoted Marathi and Sanskrit in administration; compiled the Rajya Vyavahar Kosh (administrative glossary) to replace Persian court terms with Marathi/Sanskrit equivalents — a conscious assertion of Hindu/Marathi identity.

Naval Power — Shivaji as Father of Indian Navy

  • Shivaji built Maharashtra's — and arguably India's first organised naval fleet (though Zamorin of Calicut had earlier naval forces).
  • Sindhudurg Fort (1664–1667): Built on a sea island near Malvan (Sindhudurg district) — Shivaji's first and most important sea fort. Known for the famous handprint of Shivaji (mortared into the entrance wall). Guarded the southern Konkan.
  • Vijaydurg (Vijaydurg, Ratnagiri): Oldest fort taken over and strengthened by Shivaji; became headquarters of the southern Maratha fleet. The sea battle near Vijaydurg involved the Siddi and British later.
  • Kolaba Fort (Alibag): Northern Konkan seat of the Maratha navy; later headquarters of Kanhoji Angre (the great Maratha admiral, post-Shivaji).
  • Fleet composition: Gallivats, grabs (war vessels); Shivaji built ~300–400 vessels at his peak.
  • Danda-Rajpuri and Gheria (Vijaydurg): Major Maratha naval bases.

Key Forts (Maharashtra Heritage)

  • Raigad: Capital; coronation site (1674); remains of palace, Jagdishwar temple, market street (bajarpeth); Shivaji died here (1680); accessible by ropeway/trek from Mahad (Raigad district).
  • Shivneri: Birthplace of Shivaji (1630); Junnar, Pune district; managed by Maharashtra government; museum inside.
  • Torna (Prachandagad): First fort captured by Shivaji (1646), age ~16; Pune district.
  • Sinhagad (Lion Fort): Pune; famous for Battle of Sinhagad (1670) where Tanaji Malusare died capturing it for Shivaji from Mughals (Udayanraje Bhosale's son Tanaji — "Gad ala pan Sinha gela" = fort won but the lion is lost); near Pune city; tourist site.
  • Pratapgad: Near Mahabaleshwar, Satara; site of Afzal Khan episode (1659); Bhavani Devi temple on top.

Legacy in Maharashtra

  • "Hindavi Swarajya" (self-rule of the Hindus): Shivaji's concept of sovereignty went beyond mere territorial control — it was a civilisational assertion against Mughal cultural dominance. He consulted Brahmin scholars like Gagabhatt (Varanasi) to legitimise his Kshatriya status for coronation.
  • Shiv Jayanti: Celebrated on Falgun Vadya Tritiya (Hindu calendar) or February 19 — declared state holiday in Maharashtra.
  • Shivaji Memorial (Arabian Sea): Maharashtra government is building a grand memorial for Shivaji Maharaj on a reef in the Arabian Sea near Mumbai — the Grand Shivaji Statue project.
  • Indian Navy declared Shivaji the "Father of Indian Navy" — INS Shivaji (Lonavala) is the Navy's premier training establishment.

Maharashtra Connection

Every aspect of Shivaji's legacy is directly Maharashtra-tested: Shivneri (birthplace, Pune-Junnar), Raigad (capital, coronation 1674, death 1680, Raigad district), Sinhagad battle (Tanaji Malusare, 1670), Sindhudurg Fort (1664, Malvan), Ashtapradhan, Chauth and Sardeshmukhi. These are the most frequently asked MPSC Maharashtra history facts.

Key Facts

  • Born: 1630, Shivneri Fort, Junnar, Pune district
  • Coronation: 1674, Raigad — as Chhatrapati (emperor)
  • Died: 1680, Raigad; succeeded by Sambhaji
  • First fort captured: Torna (1646) at age ~16
  • Sindhudurg Fort: 1664–1667, Malvan (Sindhudurg district); first Maratha sea fort
  • Sinhagad battle: 1670; hero: Tanaji Malusare (lost his life); "Gad ala pan Sinha gela"
  • Ashtapradhan: 8 ministers; Peshwa = PM
  • Chauth: 1/4; Sardeshmukhi: 1/10
  • Rajya Vyavahar Kosh: Marathi-Sanskrit administrative glossary

Memory Aids

  • Birth-Coronation-Death: 1630-1674-1680 = "30-74-80"
  • Shivneri = birthplace; Raigad = coronation + death; Torna = first fort
  • Ashtapradhan: P-A-S-M-S-S-N-P (Peshwa, Amatya, Sachiv, Mantri, Senapati, Sumant, Nyayadhish, Panditrao)
  • "Chauth = 1/4, Sardeshmukhi = 1/10" — C before S (quarter before tenth)

Exam Traps

  • Torna (first fort) ≠ Raigad (capital). People confuse the two; Torna was first captured, Raigad was the capital.
  • Tanaji Malusare died at Sinhagad (1670) — NOT at Pratapgad (where Afzal Khan was killed, 1659).
  • Peshwa in Shivaji's time (Moropant Pingle) = a minister in the Ashtapradhan; the hereditary Peshwa system came AFTER Shivaji under the Bhats.
