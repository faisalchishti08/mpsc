Classical dance forms PREMAINS

Overview

India has 8 classical dance forms recognised by the Sangeet Natak Akademi — each rooted in a specific region, temple tradition, and the Natyashastra of Bharata Muni (2nd c. BCE–2nd c. CE), the foundational text of Indian performing arts. They share the grammar of abhinaya (expression), mudra (hand gesture), tala (rhythm) and rasa (emotional essence). MPSC tests state-form linkage, key features, and Natyashastra basics in both Prelims and Mains.

In-Depth

Natyashastra — The Foundation

  • Composed by Bharata Muni (c. 200 BCE–200 CE); 36 chapters; the oldest surviving text on performing arts.
  • Defines Navarasas (9 emotions): Shringara (love), Hasya (humour), Karuna (pathos), Raudra (fury), Vira (heroism), Bhayanaka (terror), Bibhatsa (disgust), Adbhuta (wonder), Shanta (peace).
  • Three components of dance: Nritta (pure movement), Nritya (expressive movement), Natya (dramatic/complete dance-drama).
  • Mudras: systematic hand gestures — 28 asamyuta (single-hand) and 23 samyuta (double-hand) mudras in the classical taxonomy.

Key Distinctions

Tandava vs Lasya:

  • Tandava: vigorous, masculine style — Shiva's cosmic dance; associated with Bharatanatyam, Kathakali, Kathak (certain aspects).
  • Lasya: gentle, feminine style — Parvati's dance; associated with Mohiniyattam, Manipuri.

Gharanas of Kathak:

  • Lucknow gharana (Nawabi court): emphasis on grace (thumri, romantic expression), slow elegant movements, Mughal influence; exponent: Bindadin Maharaj, Birju Maharaj.
  • Jaipur gharana: emphasis on vigorous footwork (tatkar), fast rhythmic precision; exponent: Lachhu Maharaj tradition.

Devadasi tradition: Temple dancers who performed as a form of worship; system abolished in several states under social reform (Anti-Devadasi Act, Madras, 1947); but classical forms were "revived" and formalised by figures like Rukmini Devi Arundale (Bharatanatyam revival, founded Kalakshetra, Chennai, 1936).

Sangeet Natak Akademi

  • Established 1953 by the Government of India — apex body for performing arts.
  • Recognises classical dance forms; awards Sangeet Natak Akademi Awards (Ratna) annually.
  • Sattriya was the last to be recognised as classical — in 2000.

Maharashtra Connection

No classical dance form is exclusive to Maharashtra. However:

  • Lavani is Maharashtra's most famous folk dance — performed to the rhythm of dholki; associated with the Tamasha theatre form; expressions of shringara (love) and vira (heroism); exponents include Vithabai Narayangaokar.
  • Gondhal — ritual performance dedicated to goddess Renuka/Yellamma; Maharashtra tribal tradition.
  • Koli dance — folk dance of the Koli fishing community of coastal Maharashtra.
  • Bharatanatyam schools are active in Pune and Mumbai; Manipuri workshops common in the northeast Maharashtra cultural scene.
  • Rukmini Devi Arundale — though Tamil — her Kalakshetra model influenced dance training institutions across India including Maharashtra.

Key Facts

  • Natyashastra: Bharata Muni, ~2nd c. BCE–2nd c. CE; 36 chapters.
  • 9 Rasas (Navarasas); 3 components: Nritta, Nritya, Natya.
  • 8 classical dance forms recognised by Sangeet Natak Akademi.
  • Sattriya — youngest classical recognition: 2000.
  • Rukmini Devi Arundale: revived Bharatanatyam; founded Kalakshetra, Chennai (1936).
  • Birju Maharaj: foremost Kathak exponent (Lucknow gharana); Padma Vibhushan.
  • Kathakali: Kerala; male performers; elaborate makeup (chutti).
  • Odissi: Tribhangi posture; Jagannath temple origin.
  • Maharashtra folk: Lavani (Tamasha), Gondhal, Koli dance.

Memory Aids

  • 8 forms by state: "Tamil-UP-Kerala-AP-Odisha-Manipur-Kerala-Assam" → Bharatanatyam, Kathak, Kathakali, Kuchipudi, Odissi, Manipuri, Mohiniyattam, Sattriya.
  • Lasya = gentle/female (Mohiniyattam, Manipuri); Tandava = vigorous/male (Shiva's dance).
  • Sattriya = Assam, 2000 (last recognised).
  • Natyashastra = Bharata Muni (Bharata → Bharatanatyam also named partly from him).

Exam Traps

  • Mohiniyattam (Kerala) ≠ Kathakali (Kerala) — both from Kerala; Kathakali is theatrical/male/elaborate makeup; Mohiniyattam is solo/female/gentle.
  • Sattriya was recognised as classical only in 2000 — not part of the original Sangeet Natak Akademi list; easy trap question.
  • Kuchipudi's brass plate dancing is a specific feature — don't attribute it to other forms.
  • Bharatanatyam is Tamil Nadu, NOT Maharashtra — despite being the most performed form in cities including Mumbai/Pune.
