Maharshi Karve, Vitthal Ramji Shinde PREMAINSINTMH

Overview

Maharshi Dhondo Keshav Karve (1858–1962) was Maharashtra's pioneer of women's education and widow remarriage — he established the Hindu Widows' Home (1896) and later founded the Shreemati Nathibai Damodar Thackersey (SNDT) Women's University (1916), India's first women's university. Vitthal Ramji Shinde (1873–1944) was a leading crusader for the rights of Depressed Classes, founding the Depressed Classes Mission (1906). Both are static-GK staples for MPSC.

Why widows? In 19th-century Maharashtra, upper-caste Hindu widows were treated harshly: head-shaving, white saris, exclusion from social functions, confined to domestic service. Child marriage meant many young girls became widows at 10–15. Karve dedicated his life to rehabilitating these women through education and creating institutional support.

SNDT Women's University (1916):

  • India's first women's university.
  • Initially at Pune; later main campus shifted to Mumbai.
  • Now named Shreemati Nathibai Damodar Thackersey (SNDT) Women's University — named after the women whose son Nathibai (woman benefactress) or the male donor Damodar Thackersey (accounts differ slightly in popular telling).
  • Offers degrees in multiple streams to women students.

Depressed Classes Mission Society (1906)

  • Founded by V.R. Shinde in Pune on 18 October 1906.
  • One of the earliest organised efforts specifically for untouchable/Depressed Classes in India — predating Ambedkar's Bahishkrit Hitakarini Sabha (1924).
  • Opened 5 schools and 2 hostels for Depressed Classes by 1912.
  • Organised public meetings demanding civic rights — first public demand for water access for untouchables in Pune predated Mahad.

Maharashtra Connection

Maharshi Karve's Hindu Widows' Home at Hingne (Pune) is now a landmark educational institution. The SNDT Women's University has campuses in Pune and Mumbai — central to women's higher education in Maharashtra. Karve received the Bharat Ratna on his 100th birthday (18 April 1958) — one of the few people to receive it while alive at 100 years. His longevity (lived 104 years) is itself a factual MCQ trap. V.R. Shinde's Depressed Classes Mission (1906) is the institutional forerunner of Ambedkar's movement.
