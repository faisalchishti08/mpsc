Godavari and tributaries PREMAINSINTMH

Overview

The Godavari is India's second-longest river (after the Ganga) and the largest river system in Maharashtra, draining approximately 49% of the state's area. It originates at Trimbakeshwar (Nashik district) in the Sahyadri and flows eastward across the Deccan Plateau, eventually reaching the Bay of Bengal in Andhra Pradesh. The Godavari and its major Maharashtra tributaries (Pravara, Mula, Mula-Mutha, Manjra, Wardha, Wainganga, Penganga) are a cornerstone of MPSC Geography questions.

In-Depth

Origin and Course in Maharashtra

  • Origin: Brahmagiri Hill, Trimbakeshwar, Nashik district (~920 m above sea level).
  • Total length: ~1,465 km; Maharashtra length: ~668 km.
  • Flow direction: West to East across the Deccan Plateau; exits Maharashtra near Nanded/Basmath area into Telangana.
  • Holy city: Nashik is the major pilgrimage city on the Godavari — site of Kumbh Mela (Simhastha) every 12 years (last: 2015; next: 2027). Also Trimbakeshwar Jyotirlinga — one of the 12 Jyotirlingas.

Major Dams on Godavari System

  • Jayakwadi Dam (Paithan, Aurangabad district): On the Godavari main stream; largest project in Marathwada; reservoir called Nath Sagar; irrigates ~2.3 lakh ha; also near Paithan (Pratishthana) — ancient Satavahana capital.
  • Gangapur Dam (Nashik): On Godavari near Nashik; supplies drinking water to Nashik city.
  • Bhandardara Dam (Ahmednagar): On Pravara tributary; the Arthur Lake; vintage British-era dam (1926); near Kalsubai.
  • Yeldari and Siddheshwar Dams: On Purna river in Parbhani-Hingoli; Marathwada irrigation.
  • Upper Wardha Dam (Amravati): On Wardha river; Vidarbha irrigation.

Religious Significance

  • Trimbakeshwar (Nashik): Jyotirlinga temple; source of Godavari.
  • Nashik: Kumbh Mela (Simhastha); also Panchavati — where Rama, Lakshmana, and Sita lived during vanvas (Ramayan connection).
  • Paithan (Pratishthana): Ancient Satavahana capital on Godavari; now in Aurangabad district; Jayakwadi Dam's reservoir is called Nath Sagar (from the saint Eknath Maharaj, from Paithan).

Maharashtra Connection

Godavari is Maharashtra's "lifeline of the Deccan" — the Jayakwadi Dam transformed Marathwada's irrigation landscape. The Nashik Kumbh Mela is the world's largest religious gathering (held here every 12 years) — a massive economic and logistics event for Maharashtra. The Trimbakeshwar Jyotirlinga + source of Godavari in one location is a high-frequency MPSC question. The Pranhita-Chevella Lift Irrigation Scheme (across MH-Telangana border) and the Godavari Water Disputes Tribunal (GWDT, 1969) are inter-state water dispute topics.

Key Facts

  • Origin: Trimbakeshwar, Nashik district (Brahmagiri Hill)
  • Total length: ~1,465 km (India's 2nd longest river)
  • Maharashtra drainage: ~49% of state area
  • Largest dam on Godavari in MH: Jayakwadi Dam (Paithan, Aurangabad); reservoir = Nath Sagar
  • Kumbh Mela (Simhastha) at Nashik: every 12 years (2015, 2027)
  • Trimbakeshwar: Jyotirlinga + Godavari source — dual significance
  • Pranhita = Wardha + Wainganga confluence (Vidarbha's combined exit into Godavari)
  • GWDT formed: 1969 (inter-state water dispute — MH, AP, MP, Karnataka, Odisha)

Memory Aids

  • "Godavari = Ganga of the South" — same reverence, eastern flow
  • Origin: T-N-B = Trimbakeshwar, Nashik, Brahmagiri Hill
  • Jayakwadi = Paithan + Nath Sagar — "Paithan + Sant Eknath = Nath Sagar"
  • Pranhita = Wardha + Wainganga → "W + W = P"

Exam Traps

  • Godavari is NOT India's longest river — it is the 2nd longest (Ganga is 1st; Narmada is NOT 2nd in India by any standard measure).
  • Mula-Mutha at Pune joins the Bhima (Krishna system), NOT the Godavari — Pune's rivers go to Krishna, not Godavari.
  • Nath Sagar is the reservoir of Jayakwadi Dam (named after Sant Eknath of Paithan) — do NOT call it "Jayakwadi reservoir" without this linkage.
