Carnatic music — trinity, forms PREMAINS

Overview

Carnatic music is the classical music tradition of South India (Tamil Nadu, Karnataka, Andhra Pradesh, Kerala, Telangana). Unlike Hindustani music, Carnatic music is composition-centric (less improvised) and maintains its pre-Mughal Hindu character, with minimal Islamic influence. The tradition is defined by the Trinity of Carnatic music — Tyagaraja, Muthuswami Dikshitar, and Syama Sastri — all born in the same village in the same generation. MPSC tests the Trinity, key forms (Kriti/Varnam), and Karnataka Sangama system (72 melakarta ragas).

In-Depth

Foundations

  • Ancient basis: Samaveda (musical chanting); Bharata Muni's Natyashastra (rasa/tala theory).
  • Sangita Ratnakara (13th c., Sarangadeva): encyclopaedic music treatise; bridge between ancient and medieval.
  • Purandaradasa (1484–1564): "Father of Carnatic Music" (Karnataka Sangita Pitamaha); fixed the 72 melakarta parent-raga system; composed the foundational exercises (Swaravali, Alankaras, Gitas, Kritis) used in teaching even today; belonged to Haridasa bhakti movement; from Karnataka.

Raga System

  • 72 Melakarta (parent) ragas: the systematic classification of all possible heptatonic ragas (Purandaradasa/Venkatamakhi).
  • Each melakarta raga uses all 7 svaras (Sa Ri Ga Ma Pa Da Ni); Janya ragas (derivative ragas) derived from them.
  • Commonly asked: Raga Bilahari, Kalyani, Bhairavi, Shankarabharanam.

Key Instruments

Melodic: Veena (saraswati veena; Carnatic chordophone; 4 main + 3 drone strings); Violin (in Carnatic accompaniment since early 19th c.); Nadaswaram (double-reed; nagaswaram; temples).

Percussion: Mridangam (primary; barrel drum; right-hand: bass; left-hand: treble); Ghatam (clay pot; Vikku Vinayakram); Kanjira (tambourine; reptile skin); Morsing (jaw harp).

Maharashtra Connection

  • Carnatic music is primarily South Indian; Maharashtra sits at the cultural junction between North and South India musical traditions.
  • Natyasangeet (Marathi musical theatre) draws more from Hindustani.
  • M.S. Subbalakshmi: performed widely in Maharashtra; her devotional recordings (Suprabhatam, Bhaja Govindam) are widely known.
  • Maharashtra's classical music scene is predominantly Hindustani; however the Sawai Gandharva festival in Pune has hosted Carnatic performances.
  • Pune hosts occasional Carnatic concerts — city's arts culture is broad enough to include South Indian classical.

Key Facts

  • Carnatic music's "Father": Purandaradasa (15th–16th c.); 72 melakarta system.
  • Trinity: Tyagaraja + Muthuswami Dikshitar + Syama Sastri — all from Tiruvarur, Tamil Nadu.
  • M.S. Subbalakshmi: first musician Bharat Ratna (1998).
  • Main Carnatic composition form: Kriti (pallavi + anupallavi + charanam).
  • Main percussion: Mridangam.
  • Main string: Veena (Saraswati Veena).
  • Raga system: 72 Melakarta parent ragas.

Exam Traps

  • M.S. Subbalakshmi's Bharat Ratna = 1998 (not 1999 or 2001); she also received it as the first musician.
  • Purandaradasa ≠ one of the Trinity — he is "Father of Carnatic Music" (15th–16th c.); Trinity is 18th c.
  • Tyagaraja composed in Telugu (not Tamil or Sanskrit) — common confusion since he's from Tamil Nadu.
  • Mridangam is the primary Carnatic percussion; tabla is Hindustani.
