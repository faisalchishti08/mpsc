Wildlife sanctuaries and tiger reserves PREMAINSINTMH

Overview

Maharashtra has over 50 wildlife sanctuaries — the second-highest count in India — covering diverse ecosystems from the Sahyadri Western Ghats to Vidarbha's dry deciduous forests. Key sanctuaries include Bhimashankar (Shekru habitat), Radhanagari (Indian Bison), Koyna, Nawegaon-Nagzira, and Purna. MPSC tests sanctuary names, districts, flagship species, and tiger reserve designations.

Maharashtra has 6 Tiger Reserves — one of the highest in any state.

Bhimashankar WLS — Special Focus

  • District: Pune (Ambegaon taluka).
  • State animal habitat: Primary protected area for Indian Giant Squirrel (Shekru, Ratufa indica) in Maharashtra.
  • River source: Bhima river originates here; also one of the 12 Jyotirlingas (Bhimashankar temple).
  • Forest type: Semi-evergreen and moist deciduous (Sahyadri Western Ghats).
  • Birds: Malabar Pied Hornbill, Malabar Grey Hornbill; important IBA (Important Bird Area).

Great Indian Bustard — Nannaj Sanctuary

  • Nannaj (Solapur district) = Maharashtra's only habitat for the Great Indian Bustard (GIB, Ardeotis nigriceps) — listed as Critically Endangered (IUCN Red List).
  • GIB is one of the heaviest flying birds in the world; ground-nesting; victim of power line collisions, pesticide use, and habitat degradation.
  • Supreme Court (2021): Directed underground power lines in GIB habitats in Rajasthan and Gujarat — relevant conservation policy.
  • Maharashtra's GIB population: extremely small (possibly <5 birds remain in the state).

Maharashtra Connection

Bhimashankar WLS is the only place in India where you can reliably spot the Shekru (state animal) in the wild — a combined ecology + symbolism topic for MPSC. Nannaj's Great Indian Bustard is Maharashtra's most endangered bird habitat and connects to national-level wildlife law and court orders. Bor Tiger Reserve (Wardha) is significant as India's smallest Tiger Reserve — a trivia fact MPSC has targeted.
