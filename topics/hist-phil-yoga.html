Yoga PREMAINS

Overview

Yoga as a philosophical school (distinct from yoga as physical practice) was systematised by Patanjali in the Yogasutras (~2nd c. BCE–4th c. CE). It accepts Samkhya's metaphysical framework (Purusha-Prakriti dualism) but adds Ishvara (God) and provides a practical eightfold path (Ashtanga Yoga) for attaining liberation. In 2016, UNESCO inscribed Yoga on its Intangible Cultural Heritage list. MPSC tests Patanjali, the eight limbs, and Yoga's distinction from Samkhya.

In-Depth

Patanjali and the Yogasutras

  • Patanjali: Systematised yoga into 195 sutras (aphorisms) in the Yogasutras, divided into 4 chapters (Padas): Samadhi Pada, Sadhana Pada, Vibhuti Pada, Kaivalya Pada.
  • Same Patanjali also authored the Mahabhashya (commentary on Panini's Ashtadhyayi grammar) — though some scholars debate if these are the same person.

Key Philosophical Position

  • Accepts: Samkhya's 25 tattvas; Purusha-Prakriti dualism; three gunas; 24 evolutes.
  • Adds: Ishvara — a special Purusha who is eternally liberated, never entangled with Prakriti; used as an object of meditation (Ishvara Pranidhana).
  • Goal: Chitta vritti nirodha (cessation of the modifications of the mind) → Purusha rests in its own pure nature → Kaivalya (isolation/liberation).

Ashtanga Yoga (Eight Limbs)

LimbNameMeaning
1YamaEthical restraints: non-violence (ahimsa), truthfulness, non-stealing, celibacy, non-possessiveness
2NiyamaPersonal disciplines: purity, contentment, austerity (tapas), self-study, surrender to God
3AsanaPhysical posture — stable, comfortable seat for meditation
4PranayamaBreath control — regulation of life-force (prana)
5PratyaharaWithdrawal of senses from external objects
6DharanaConcentration — fixing the mind on one point
7DhyanaMeditation — unbroken flow of awareness on the object
8SamadhiAbsorption — complete union; the goal

Limbs 1–5 are external (bahiranga); limbs 6–8 are internal (antaranga). Dharana + Dhyana + Samadhi together = Samyama (perfect restraint).

UNESCO Recognition

  • Yoga was inscribed on UNESCO's Intangible Cultural Heritage list in 2016.
  • India celebrates International Day of Yoga on 21 June (initiated at UN by PM Narendra Modi, first observed 2015).
  • The UN proclaimed 21 June as International Day of Yoga in December 2014 (resolution 69/131).

Maharashtra Connection

Maharashtra has been an active centre for yoga traditions:

  • Pune (Iyengar Yoga Institute): B.K.S. Iyengar (1918–2014), born in Karnataka but based in Pune for most of his life; founder of Iyengar Yoga (emphasis on alignment, use of props); Padma Vibhushan; Light on Yoga (1966) is the most widely read yoga text globally.
  • Lonavala (near Pune): Kaivalyadhama Yoga Institute (founded 1924 by Swami Kuvalayananda) — one of the world's oldest scientific yoga research institutes.

Key Facts

  • Founder: Patanjali; text: Yogasutras (~195 aphorisms; 4 chapters).
  • Core formula: "Chitta vritti nirodha" = cessation of mind-modifications.
  • Eight limbs (Ashtanga): Yama, Niyama, Asana, Pranayama, Pratyahara, Dharana, Dhyana, Samadhi.
  • Accepts Samkhya + adds Ishvara (God).
  • Liberation = Kaivalya.
  • UNESCO ICH: 2016.
  • International Yoga Day: 21 June (first observed 2015; UN resolution 2014).
  • B.K.S. Iyengar (Pune): greatest modern yoga teacher; Padma Vibhushan.

Memory Aids

  • 8 limbs: "Y-N-A-Pr-Pr-Dh-Dh-Sa" → Yama, Niyama, Asana, Pranayama, Pratyahara, Dharana, Dhyana, Samadhi.
  • Simplified: "Ethics → Body → Breath → Senses → Mind → Meditation → Samadhi".
  • Yoga = "Samkhya + God + practice".
  • International Yoga Day = 21 June, 2015 first.

Exam Traps

  • Yoga school (Patanjali) ≠ physical yoga as practised today — Patanjali's Yoga is primarily a philosophical and meditative system; Asana is just one of eight limbs.
  • UNESCO ICH 2016 for Yoga; 2017 for Kumbh Mela — don't confuse the two recent Indian ICH inscriptions.
  • International Yoga Day UN resolution: December 2014; first observed: 21 June 2015.
