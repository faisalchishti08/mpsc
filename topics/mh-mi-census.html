Overview

Maharashtra's demographic profile — drawn primarily from Census 2011 (and provisional/projected data for 2021, which was delayed due to COVID-19) — is a core MPSC-GK topic. Key metrics: population rank (2nd), literacy rate (~83%), sex ratio (929), urbanisation level (~45%), and district-level disparities. Questions appear as direct factual MCQs and as Mains analysis of development challenges.

Urbanisation

Maharashtra has the highest urban population in absolute numbers in India (~5.08 crore urban residents, Census 2011). Mumbai Metropolitan Region (MMR) is the largest metropolitan region. Urban agglomerations: Greater Mumbai (1.24 crore), Pune, Nagpur, Nashik, Aurangabad (now Chhatrapati Sambhajinagar). The state drives urban growth through industrial corridors (DMIC, Mumbai-Pune Expressway corridor) and IT hubs in Pune.

Key observations:

  • Ratnagiri and Sindhudurg: High sex ratio due to male out-migration (men work in Mumbai); high female population relative to males.
  • Mumbai City: Low sex ratio (working-age male migrants); high literacy.
  • Nandurbar: Tribal district; lowest literacy in Maharashtra; highest poverty indicators.
  • Western Maharashtra (Kolhapur, Sangli, Pune): Economic prosperity but poor child sex ratio — sex-selective practices linked to patriarchal attitudes despite higher incomes.

Scheduled Caste and Scheduled Tribe Profile

  • Maharashtra's SC population ~1.32 crore (Mahar, Matang, Chambhar, Dhor being significant communities). The Neo-Buddhist community (followers of Ambedkar) is large and counted under SC.
  • ST population ~1.05 crore; concentrated in Nashik, Nandurbar, Thane, Gadchiroli, Amravati, Chandrapur districts.

Population Density

Maharashtra overall density ~365/km². Highest: Mumbai City (~very high, thousands/km²). Lowest: Gadchiroli (~74/km²) — forested, tribal.

Religion (Census 2011)

Hindu ~79.8%, Muslim ~11.5%, Buddhist ~5.8% (inflated by Neo-Buddhists/Ambedkarites — highest Buddhist % among large states), Christian ~1%, Jain ~1.3% (notably high — Jains are economically significant in MH).

Maharashtra Connection

Maharashtra's demographic data directly drives state policy — the low child sex ratio in western Maharashtra led to the Beti Bachao Beti Padhao programme focus in Pune, Satara, Kolhapur, Sangli. High urbanisation drives the AMRUT and Smart Cities mission. Nandurbar's low literacy informs tribal education budgets. The 2021 Census (delayed) will revise these figures — MPSC still asks on 2011 data.

Key Facts

  • Population rank: 2nd (after Uttar Pradesh).
  • Literacy: 82.9% (above national 74%).
  • Sex ratio: 929 (below national 943).
  • Child sex ratio: 883 (well below national 919).
  • Urbanisation: ~45% — highest share among large states.
  • Largest city: Greater Mumbai (~1.24 crore, Census 2011 city proper).
  • Highest sex ratio district: Ratnagiri.
  • Lowest literacy district: Nandurbar.
  • Buddhist %: ~5.8% — highest among large states (Neo-Buddhist community).

Memory Aids

  • "MH is 2nd in pop, 1st in urban, above avg in literacy, below avg in sex ratio."
  • Sex ratio trap: MH (929) < national (943) — both below 1000.
  • Child sex ratio: western MH districts are poorest despite being richest economically — paradox for Mains.
  • Ratnagiri high sex ratio = male migration to Mumbai; Mumbai City low sex ratio = male migrants arriving.

Exam Traps

  • Maharashtra is 2nd in population (not 3rd or 1st) — UP is 1st.
  • Child sex ratio (0–6) is different from overall sex ratio — MH's child sex ratio (883) is much worse than overall (929).
  • 2021 Census was not conducted on schedule (COVID delay); MPSC questions still based on 2011 data unless updated post-2023.
  • Buddhist population in Maharashtra is primarily Neo-Buddhist (Ambedkarites), not traditional Theravada/Mahayana — a distinct socio-historical fact.
