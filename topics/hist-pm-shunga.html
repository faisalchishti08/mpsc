Shungas, Kanvas PREMAINS

Overview

The Shunga dynasty (185–73 BCE) was founded by Pushyamitra Shunga, who overthrew the last Mauryan emperor Brihadratha, and ruled from Vidisha (Madhya Pradesh) rather than Pataliputra. They were followed by the short-lived Kanva dynasty (73–28 BCE). The post-Mauryan period is important for MPSC because it saw the revival of Brahmanical culture, the Ashvamedha tradition, and important artistic patronage (Sanchi, Bharhut) alongside renewed political fragmentation.

Political Character

The Shungas were Brahmin in origin and strongly associated with Brahmanical revival. Pushyamitra performed two Ashvamedha yajnas (horse sacrifices) to assert sovereignty — one each recorded in the Ayodhya inscription and literary sources. The capital shifted from Pataliputra to Vidisha (modern Besnagar, near Sanchi, MP). The empire was considerably smaller than the Mauryas: essentially the Gangetic plain, parts of MP, and Rajasthan.

Shunga-Greek Conflict

The Yuga Purana and Patanjali's Mahabhashya (written c. 150 BCE) mention Yavana (Greek) armies reaching as far as Saketa (Ayodhya) and Pataliputra — remarkable evidence that Indo-Greek forces under Menander nearly sacked the Shunga capital. Vasumitra (grandson of Pushyamitra) is credited with repelling them at the Sindhu river. This conflict is also associated with the visit of Heliodorus (Greek ambassador, c. 110 BCE) who erected the Garuda pillar at Vidisha (Heliodorus pillar), calling himself a Bhagavata (Vaishnava devotee) — early evidence of foreign adoption of Hinduism.

Art and Architecture under Shungas

The Shungas are best known for completing/elaborating Buddhist monuments even as they promoted Brahmanical rites — demonstrating that royal patronage was not rigidly sectarian:

  • Sanchi Stupa 1: The four stone toranas (gateways) were built in the Shunga period (the core mound was Ashokan). The gateways bear elaborate narrative relief carvings of Jataka tales, yakshas, and Buddhist symbols. Notably, the Buddha is represented symbolically (footprints, wheel, empty throne, umbrella) — not in human form.
  • Bharhut Stupa (Satna district, MP): Built/enlarged by Shungas; famous for narrative reliefs with donor inscriptions, now mostly in the Indian Museum, Kolkata. It introduced the vedika (railing) with medallion carvings.
  • These sites show the transition from Mauryan sculptural tradition to the more exuberant narrative style of the post-Mauryan period.

Kanva Dynasty (73–28 BCE)

Vasudeva Kanva, minister of the last Shunga Devabhuti, murdered him and founded the Kanva dynasty. The Kanvas (Vasudeva → Bhumimitra → Narayana → Susharman) ruled for 45 years. Susharman was overthrown by the Satavahana king Simuka, ending the Kanva dynasty and beginning Satavahana ascendancy in the Deccan.

Maharashtra Connection

Not directly Maharashtra-specific. The Shunga–Kanva period is the direct prologue to the Satavahana dynasty, which had its heartland in Maharashtra and Andhra (the Satavahanas overthrew the Kanvas). The art traditions of Sanchi and Bharhut directly influenced Satavahana-era cave art at Bhaja, Karla, and Nashik in Maharashtra.

Key Facts

  • Pushyamitra: founded Shunga dynasty 185 BCE by killing Brihadratha.
  • Shungas: Brahmins; capital at Vidisha (Besnagar, MP).
  • Ashvamedha: Pushyamitra performed two horse sacrifices.
  • Heliodorus pillar: erected at Vidisha (Besnagar) c. 110 BCE — Greek ambassador turned Vaishnava.
  • Sanchi toranas: built in Shunga period (core stupa was Ashokan).
  • Bharhut Stupa: built/enlarged under Shungas; reliefs now in Indian Museum, Kolkata.
  • Kanva dynasty: 73–28 BCE; 4 rulers; ended by Simuka (Satavahana).
  • Duration: Shunga dynasty lasted ~112 years; Kanva ~45 years.

Memory Aids

  • "Push Agni Vasu Bha Dev" — Pushyamitra → Agnimitra → Vasujyeshtha → Bhagabhadra → Devabhuti.
  • "Shungas built gates, Ashoka built stupas" — remember who built the Sanchi toranas.
  • Heliodorus = "Helen goes Hindu" — Greek ambassador becomes Vaishnava.
  • Kanvas: "V-B-N-S" (Vasudeva, Bhumimitra, Narayana, Susharman) — 4 kings, 45 years.

Exam Traps

  • Sanchi Stupa 1's toranas are Shunga, not Ashokan — the mound is Ashokan, the gateways are not.
  • The Heliodorus pillar at Vidisha is a Garuda pillar (Vaishnava), not Buddhist — a Greek erected a Vaishnava monument.
  • Pushyamitra Shunga is often called "anti-Buddhist" but the Shunga period saw Buddhist monuments flourish — the "anti-Buddhist reaction" theory is contested.
  • Kanvas are sometimes confused with Kanishka (Kushana) — completely different dynasty, different period, different religion.
