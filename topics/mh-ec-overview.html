GSDP, sectors and economic survey of Maharashtra PREMAINSINTMH

Overview

Maharashtra is India's economic powerhouse — contributing approximately 15% of national GDP while accounting for ~9.3% of India's population (2021 estimates). The state's Gross State Domestic Product (GSDP) makes it the largest economy among all Indian states, driven by a diversified mix of services, industry, and agriculture. Maharashtra's Economic Survey (published annually before the state budget) and the Directorate of Economics and Statistics (DES) are the key reference authorities. MPSC tests current GSDP rank, sectoral shares, key economic corridors, and flagship economic initiatives.

Sectoral Composition (Three Sectors)

Maharashtra's economy broadly follows the national trend but with a much higher services sector:

  • Primary Sector (Agriculture): ~10–12% of GSDP. Despite a large farming population, agriculture's share is lower than most states because of the huge services/industry base. Key crops: sugarcane, cotton, soybean, jowar, grapes, onion, oranges, turmeric.
  • Secondary Sector (Industry): ~28–30% of GSDP. Led by pharmaceuticals (Pune), automobiles (Pune-Nashik), petrochemicals (Ratnagiri-Raigad PCPIR), textiles (Mumbai-Nashik), food processing, IT hardware.
  • Tertiary Sector (Services): ~58–62% of GSDP. Dominant. Led by financial services (Mumbai — BSE, NSE, RBI HQ, SEBI HQ), IT/ITeS (Pune's Rajiv Gandhi InfoTech Park, Hinjewadi), trade, logistics, real estate.

Maharashtra's Economic Corridors and Industrial Regions

  1. Mumbai Metropolitan Region (MMR): Financial services, media, entertainment, shipping (JNPT — India's largest container port).
  2. Pune Metropolitan Region (PMR): Automobiles (Tata Motors, Bajaj, Mercedes), IT (Hinjewadi), defence manufacturing (Bharat Forge, IAF base at Lohegaon).
  3. Nashik: Grapes (wine capital — Sula, York), onions, defence (HAL, M4 aircraft), Mahindra tractors.
  4. Aurangabad (Chhatrapati Sambhajinagar): Marathwada industrial hub; Auric (Aurangabad Industrial City) under Delhi-Mumbai Industrial Corridor (DMIC); pharmaceuticals.
  5. Nagpur: Orange trade capital; Multi-Modal International Cargo Hub and Airport (MIHAN); Zero Mile Centre of India; proposed Hyperloop corridor.
  6. Konkan (Ratnagiri-Sindhudurg-Raigad): JNPT port; Dapoli/Ratnagiri tourism; Petro-Chemical and Petroleum Investment Region (PCPIR) at Raigad.

Key Economic Documents

  • Maharashtra Economic Survey: Published by Planning Dept/Finance Dept before state budget; key reference for MPSC Mains.
  • Directorate of Economics and Statistics (DES), Pune: Official statistics body; publishes District Income Estimates, Agricultural Census data, Household Survey.
  • Maharashtra Budget: Presented in Vidhan Sabha (usually February); Maharashtra is the highest-tax-collecting state in India.
  • Vibrant Maharashtra / Invest Maharashtra Summits: Organized by MIDC to attract investments; Nagpur hosted the 2023 Global Investors Summit.

Notable Economic Characteristics

  • Regional imbalance: Vidarbha and Marathwada are significantly less developed than Mumbai-Pune corridor. Article 371(2) addresses this constitutionally. Bacchawat Commission (1984) documented this.
  • Cooperative economy: Sugar cooperatives in western Maharashtra (Kolhapur-Sangli-Satara belt) and Nashik wine cooperatives are globally known.
  • MSMEs: Maharashtra has one of India's largest MSME bases (~40 lakh registered); especially in Pune, Nashik, Kolhapur (foundry cluster — Kolhapur is India's "Foundry Capital").

Maharashtra Connection

Maharashtra's economic weight (15% GDP) means every central scheme investment — PM Gati Shakti, DMIC, Sagarmala — has major Maharashtra components. The Mumbai Metropolitan Region Development Authority (MMRDA) and Maharashtra Industrial Development Corporation (MIDC) are the two key state economic agencies. Mumbai as India's financial capital (BSE 1875, NSE 1992, RBI 1935, SEBI 1988 all headquartered here) gives Maharashtra a structural advantage.

Exam Traps

  • Maharashtra is 1st by GSDP but NOT 1st in per capita income (Goa, Sikkim, Telangana, Haryana, Karnataka often rank higher per capita).
  • GSDP figures change yearly — in exams, "India's largest state economy" is the safe stable answer.
  • MIDC ≠ MMRDA: MIDC = industrial land; MMRDA = metro region infrastructure.
