Vidhan Sabha and Vidhan Parishad PREMAINSINTMH

Overview

Maharashtra has a bicameral state legislature — the Vidhan Sabha (Lower House, 288 seats) and the Vidhan Parishad (Upper House, 78 seats). Among India's 6 states with bicameral legislatures (UP, Bihar, Maharashtra, Karnataka, Telangana, Andhra Pradesh), Maharashtra's composition, election methods, and session details are core MPSC polity topics.

Vidhan Sabha — Details

  • 288 constituencies: All are single-member constituencies elected through First Past the Post (FPTP) system.
  • Quorum: 1/10th of total membership = 29 members.
  • Sessions: Three per year by convention — Budget Session (Feb-March, Mumbai), Monsoon Session (July, Mumbai), Winter Session (December, Nagpur).
  • Speaker (Adhyaksha): Elected by Assembly members; presides; Speaker's ruling is final on procedure.
  • Leader of House: Chief Minister (if CM is a MLA); or the senior minister designated.
  • Pro-tem Speaker: Senior-most member who presides until Speaker is elected after elections.

Chairperson (Sabhapatee): Elected by VP members; presides over Vidhan Parishad. Deputy Chairperson (Upa-Sabhapatee): Elected similarly.

Sessions and Winter Session at Nagpur

  • By convention derived from the Nagpur Pact (1953), the Winter Session (December) is held at Nagpur's Vidhan Bhavan.
  • All other sessions (Budget, Monsoon) are held at the Vidhan Bhavan, Mumbai (Nariman Point area).
  • The winter session's location in Nagpur symbolises the commitment to Vidarbha's political inclusion.

Money Bills and Legislative Powers

  • Money Bills can only be introduced in the Vidhan Sabha — the Vidhan Parishad has limited power (14-day delay; VS can override).
  • Constitutional Amendment: State legislature participates via Article 368 ratification (for certain amendments requiring majority of states' legislatures).
  • Confidence/No-Confidence: Only Vidhan Sabha can pass no-confidence motion against the Council of Ministers (government); Vidhan Parishad cannot.

Reservation in Legislature

  • SC reservation: 29 seats in Vidhan Sabha.
  • ST reservation: 25 seats in Vidhan Sabha.
  • Under 104th Constitutional Amendment (2020): The provision for nominating Anglo-Indian members to Parliament and State Legislatures was discontinued (it expired after 70 years from 1950).

Maharashtra Connection

The Nagpur Winter Session is a regular news item and MPSC question. The 288 seats of the Vidhan Sabha and the 78 seats of the Vidhan Parishad are basic numeric facts. The election of Governor's nominees to VP from arts, literature, etc. is tested (sometimes cricket player or cultural icon is nominated). The Maharashtra Assembly building in Mumbai (Vidhan Bhavan near Churchgate) is an architectural landmark.

Key Facts

  • Vidhan Sabha: 288 seats; 5-year term; FPTP direct election
  • Vidhan Parishad: 78 seats; 6-year term (permanent house, 1/3 retire every 2 years)
  • VP composition: 30 (MLAs) + 22 (Local bodies) + 7 (Graduates) + 7 (Teachers) + 12 (Governor's nominees) = 78
  • Winter session: Nagpur (December); other sessions: Mumbai
  • SC reserved seats (VS): 29; ST reserved: 25
  • Speaker of VS = Adhyaksha; Chairman of VP = Sabhapatee
  • India's 6 bicameral state legislatures (as of 2024): UP, Bihar, Maharashtra, Karnataka, Telangana, Andhra Pradesh

Exam Traps

  • VP = 78 seats, NOT 76 or 80 — 30+22+7+7+12 = 78.
  • Money Bills: only Vidhan Sabha can introduce them; VP has 14 days to return (not approve/reject).
  • Nagpur Pact (1953) is why winter session is in Nagpur — NOT a constitutional provision; it is a political convention.
