Anglo-Maratha wars and fall PREMAINSMH

Overview

The Anglo-Maratha Wars (1775–1818) were three successive conflicts between the British East India Company and the Maratha Confederacy that ended in the complete British conquest of the Maratha territories and the abolition of the Peshwaship. They mark the transition of Maharashtra from an imperial power to a British province. Very high MPSC MH frequency.

In-Depth

First Anglo-Maratha War (1775–1782)

Cause: British supported Raghunathrao (Raghoba) — the Peshwa's uncle who had murdered Peshwa Narayanrao (1773) and was now claimant to the Peshwaship — against the legitimate Maratha government (the Barbhai regency/Nana Fadnavis).

Treaty of Surat (1775): Raghoba ceded Salsette and Broach to British; British agreed to restore him as Peshwa.

Key events:

  • Battle of Talegaon (1775): Maratha forces under Nana Fadnavis and Hari Pant Phadke surrounded a British column under Cockburn and forced surrender; Treaty of Wadgaon (1779) — British agreed to restore all gains since 1773 (the most humiliating British defeat in Maharashtra, later repudiated by the Bombay Presidency's superiors).
  • British forces from Bengal (under General Goddard) invaded from the north; captured Bassein (1780), Ahmednabad (1780).

Treaty of Salbai (1782): Status quo ante restored; British recognised the Maratha regency under Nana Fadnavis; Raghunathrao's claim abandoned; Salsette retained by British. Gave British 20 years of Maratha peace.

Second Anglo-Maratha War (1803–1805)

Cause: Internal Maratha rivalry — Yashwantrao Holkar defeated Peshwa Baji Rao II and Scindia's combined forces at Pune (1802) → Peshwa Baji Rao II fled and signed Treaty of Bassein (31 December 1802) with British → British guaranteed Peshwa's throne in exchange for subsidiary alliance.

This violated Maratha sovereignty; Scindia and Nagpur Bhosale allied against British.

British strategy: Two-pronged attack:

  • General Lord Lake (north) vs Scindia and Bhosale in north India.
  • General Arthur Wellesley (later Duke of Wellington) (south) vs Scindia in Deccan.

Treaties after Second War:

  • Treaty of Surji-Anjangaon (1803): Scindia ceded territories in Rajputana, Bundelkhand, Delhi-Agra region.
  • Treaty of Deogaon (1803): Raghuji Bhosale (Nagpur) ceded Cuttack (Orissa).
  • Holkar escaped this war; fought in 1804–05 but eventually also signed a treaty (Treaty of Rajghat, 1806).

Third Anglo-Maratha War (1817–1818)

Cause: Peshwa Baji Rao II — frustrated by subsidiary alliance constraints and British control — secretly contacted all Maratha chiefs to form a united front. British, anticipating revolt, demanded Peshwa disband his army.

Key battles:

  • Battle of Kirkee (1817): Peshwa attacked the British Residency near Pune; British repulsed him.
  • Battle of Koregaon (1 January 1818): A small British force (mostly Mahar Regiment soldiers) repulsed a much larger Peshwa force — celebrated as a symbol of Dalit soldiers' valour against Brahmin Peshwa oppression; the Koregaon Pillar (victory obelisk) was erected by British; still a site of annual Dalit commemoration.
  • Battle of Sitabaldi (1817): British defeated Nagpur Bhosale.
  • Battle of Mahidpur (1817): British (Malcolm) defeated Holkar.

Outcomes:

  • Peshwa Baji Rao II surrendered; pensioned off to Bithur (near Kanpur); Peshwaship abolished.
  • Nagpur annexed; Gaikwad of Baroda accepted subsidiary alliance.
  • Pune came under British control; became part of the Bombay Presidency.
  • Holkar and Scindia survived as subordinate princely states.

Maharashtra Connection

All three wars primarily contested in Maharashtra and its environs:

  • Wadgaon (1779): near Pune — British humiliation.
  • Kirkee and Koregaon (1817–18): near Pune.
  • Abolition of Peshwaship (1818) ended Maharashtra's independent polity.
  • Peshwa's last seat of power: Pune; British set up Pune as their Deccan administrative centre.
  • The Koregaon pillar (Pune district) remains a powerful memorial — annually visited by Dalit communities on 1 January.

Key Facts

  • First war: 1775–1782; ended with Treaty of Salbai (1782) — status quo.
  • Treaty of Wadgaon (1779): most humiliating British defeat in Maharashtra; later repudiated.
  • Second war: 1803–1805; caused by Treaty of Bassein (31 Dec 1802).
  • Battle of Assaye (1803): Wellesley vs Scindia/Bhosale; Wellesley called it his toughest battle.
  • Lake captured Delhi (1803).
  • Third war: 1817–1818; Battle of Kirkee (1817), Koregaon (1 Jan 1818).
  • Peshwa Baji Rao II surrendered; pensioned to Bithur.
  • Peshwaship abolished: 1818.

Exam Traps

  • Treaty of Salbai (1782) ended the First War, not Wadgaon — Wadgaon was a battle midway; British repudiated the Wadgaon treaty.
  • Wellesley (future Duke of Wellington) fought Second War in India, not to be confused with Lord Wellesley (Governor-General).
  • Treaty of Bassein (1802) caused the Second Anglo-Maratha War — but it was Peshwa Baji Rao II who signed it to save his throne from Holkar.
  • Koregaon pillar was erected by British (not a Maratha monument) — British celebrated the Mahar soldiers' service; Dalits now commemorate it as an assertion of dignity.
