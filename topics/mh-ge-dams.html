Major dams — Koyna, Jayakwadi, Bhandardara, Ujani PREMAINSINTMH

Overview

Maharashtra has the largest number of large dams in India — over 1,800 dams (large and medium) across the state. The major dams — Koyna (hydroelectric), Jayakwadi (Marathwada irrigation), Bhandardara (Pune water), Ujani (Solapur) — are perennial MPSC targets. Dam names, rivers, districts, purposes (irrigation/hydro/drinking water), and capacities are tested as direct MCQs.

Koyna Dam — Flagship Project

  • Built: 1956–1964; located in Koyna taluka, Satara district.
  • Reservoir: Shivasagar Lake — one of Maharashtra's largest.
  • Power: ~1,960 MW — Maharashtra's and one of India's largest hydroelectric generating stations.
  • The 1967 Koyna Earthquake (M 6.5, 11 December): India's worst Reservoir-Induced Seismicity (RIS) event; killed ~180 people; triggered seismic monitoring at all large Indian dams.
  • Water is diverted through a tunnel under the Sahyadri to the western face (Konkan side) to generate power — the turbines are at Pophali on the Konkan slope.

Ujani Dam — Largest Storage

  • Ujani Dam (also: Bhigwan Dam) on the Bhima river, Solapur district.
  • Largest storage capacity among Maharashtra's dams: ~3,313 MCM.
  • The backwaters at Bhigwan (Dyamani) attract massive flamingo congregations (Lesser Flamingo, Phoenicopterus minor) — making it a key ecotourism and bird-watching site; seasonal (October–March).

1961 Panshet Dam Breach

  • On 11 July 1961, Panshet Dam (Pune district, on Ambi river, a Mutha tributary) failed during heavy monsoon — causing catastrophic flooding of Pune city. The downstream Khadakwasla Dam also breached. Estimated 1,000+ deaths; massive destruction of Pune's Peth areas. This led to strengthening of all Maharashtra dams.

Maharashtra Connection

Maharashtra's dam-building history is interwoven with its sugar cooperative economy (Pune-Satara-Kolhapur belt) and its chronic water disputes (Marathwada vs. western Maharashtra water allocation). The Jayakwadi controversies — upper riparian dams on the Godavari (Nashik, Ahmednagar) releasing less water — led to the Godavari River Basin Authority mediation and Maharashtra government orders on controlled releases.

Exam Traps

  • Koyna is in Satara district, NOT Pune (people confuse because Pune region is nearby).
  • Largest dam by storage = Ujani (Solapur); most famous dam = Koyna — different metrics.
  • Nath Sagar (Jayakwadi) ≠ Shivasagar (Koyna) — two different dams, two different reservoirs.
