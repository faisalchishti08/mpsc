Shivaji — rise, conquests, coronation PREMAINSMH

Overview

Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj (1630–1680) founded the Maratha kingdom in the 17th century, establishing a sovereign Hindavi Swarajya in the Deccan against the Adil Shahi of Bijapur and the Mughal Empire. He is studied not only as a military genius but as an able administrator whose Ashtapradhan council, revenue system and naval power laid the institutional base of the Maratha state. Extremely high-frequency for MPSC (Prelims facts + Mains administration themes + Maharashtra identity).

In-Depth

Early Life & Rise

  • Born at Shivneri fort (1630) to Shahaji Bhosale and Jijabai.
  • Guided by Jijabai and Dadoji Konddev; began capturing Bijapur forts (Torna, 1646) in his teens.

Key Conflicts

  • Afzal Khan episode (1659) at Pratapgad — Shivaji killed the Bijapur general with the bagh nakh (tiger claws).
  • Shaista Khan raid (1663) in Pune — Shivaji's daring night attack.
  • Sack of Surat (1664) — looted the rich Mughal port.
  • Treaty of Purandar (1665) with Mughal general Raja Jai Singh I — surrendered 23 forts.
  • Escape from Agra (1666) — fled Aurangzeb's captivity hidden in sweet baskets.

Coronation

Crowned Chhatrapati at Raigad in 1674, assuming sovereign status; a second coronation followed. He took titles like Kshatriya Kulavantas and Haindava Dharmoddharak.

Administration

  • Ashtapradhan — council of eight ministers (Peshwa/PM, Amatya, Sachiv, Mantri, Senapati, Sumant, Nyayadhish, Panditrao).
  • Revenue based on Chauth (1/4) and Sardeshmukhi (1/10) levies; land revenue reformed (Kathi of Malik Ambar adapted).
  • Strong forts (~300) and a pioneering navy (Kanhoji Angre era followed) guarding the Konkan coast.
  • Promoted Marathi and Sanskrit in administration (Rajya Vyavahar Kosh).

Maharashtra Connection

The bedrock of Maharashtra's cultural-political identity. Forts like Raigad, Pratapgad, Sinhagad, Torna, Shivneri are heritage and tourism anchors; Shiv Jayanti is widely celebrated. Shivaji's model of Swarajya recurs in state symbolism and politics.

Key Facts

  • Born 1630, Shivneri; parents Shahaji & Jijabai.
  • Coronation: Raigad, 1674 (Chhatrapati).
  • Afzal Khan killed at Pratapgad, 1659.
  • Treaty of Purandar, 1665 (with Jai Singh I) — 23 forts ceded.
  • Council of ministers: Ashtapradhan (eight).
  • Taxes: Chauth & Sardeshmukhi.
  • Died 1680 at Raigad; succeeded by Sambhaji.

Memory Aids

  • "Shivneri born, Raigad crowned" (1630 → 1674).
  • Ashtapradhan = 8 ministers; Peshwa = PM.
  • "Purandar = 23 forts, 1665."

Exam Traps

  • Coronation year is 1674, not the birth/Afzal Khan year — keep 1630/1659/1665/1674 distinct.
  • Chauth (1/4) vs Sardeshmukhi (1/10) — don't swap the fractions.
  • The Peshwa under Shivaji was a minister (Moropant Pingle); the hereditary Peshwa rule came later under the Bhats.
