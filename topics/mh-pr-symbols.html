State symbols — animal, bird, tree, flower, butterfly PREMAINSINTMH

Overview

Maharashtra's official state symbols — animal, bird, tree, flower, and butterfly — are defined by government notifications and tested repeatedly in MPSC Prelims as direct-recall MCQs. They also carry ecological significance, anchoring Maharashtra's biodiversity profile in broader Environment questions.

Ecological Notes

  • Indian Giant Squirrel (Shekru): Multi-coloured coat (cream, maroon, dark brown); builds large leaf nests in tree canopies. Key habitat: Bhimashankar Wildlife Sanctuary, Radhanagari. Listed as Least Concern (IUCN) but locally threatened by habitat loss.
  • Yellow-footed Green Pigeon (Hariyal): Maharashtra's folklore figure — "Hariyal ki maa" phrase derives from its nesting behaviour. Diet: figs and forest fruits; indicator species for healthy forest canopy.
  • Blue Mormon: India's second-largest butterfly. Males bright blue; females brownish-black. Host plant: wild lime (Toddalia asiatica). Declared as state butterfly by a 2015 Maharashtra government notification.

Maharashtra Connection

The Konkan Alphonso mango (Ratnagiri Alphonso holds GI tag since 2018) directly connects the state tree to Maharashtra's agriculture economy. Bhimashankar is both a Jyotirlinga pilgrimage site and the core habitat of Shekru — an MPSC favourite linkage.

Maharashtra Connection

All five symbols are endemic to or highly associated with Maharashtra's biodiversity zones: Sahyadri, Konkan coast, and Deccan plateau. Bhimashankar Wildlife Sanctuary (Pune district) is the flagship conservation area for Shekru and also a Jyotirlinga site — combines ecology with heritage in MPSC questions.

Exam Traps

  • Do NOT confuse Shekru (state animal, squirrel) with the Indian Bison (Gaur) — Gaur is state animal of Bihar, not Maharashtra.
  • Yellow-footed Green Pigeon is the state bird, NOT the peacock (peacock = national bird).
  • Blue Mormon was declared state butterfly in 2015 — questions may give wrong years (2010, 2012).

Previous-Year Trend
