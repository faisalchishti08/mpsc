Article 371(2) — special provisions for Vidarbha/Marathwada PREMAINSINTMH

Overview

Article 371(2) of the Indian Constitution provides special provisions for Maharashtra and Gujarat, specifically ensuring that the interests of the Vidarbha, Marathwada, and rest of Maharashtra regions are protected in matters of equitable distribution of development expenditure and public service recruitment. This is a unique and often misunderstood provision — MPSC tests its exact scope, the three regions mentioned, and what exactly it protects.

In-Depth

Article 371(2) — Text and Scope

Article 371(2) empowers the President to make an order ensuring that there is an equitable arrangement for the following in respect of:

  1. Establishment of separate development boards for Vidarbha, Marathwada, and the rest of Maharashtra.
  2. Equitable allocation of funds from the state budget for developmental expenditure to each of these regions.
  3. Equitable distribution in public service recruitment — posts must be filled by persons from each of the three regions.

This provision was introduced because of fears — particularly from Vidarbha leaders — that the new state of Maharashtra (1960) would be politically dominated by Western Maharashtra (Desh) at the expense of the less-developed east and Marathwada.

Development Boards Under Article 371(2)

The President's Order under Article 371(2) (issued shortly after Maharashtra's formation) led to the establishment of:

  • Vidarbha Development Board (VDB) — Nagpur
  • Marathwada Development Board (MDB) — Aurangabad (Chhatrapati Sambhajinagar)
  • Rest of Maharashtra Development Board (ROMDB) — Pune

These boards:

  • Monitor development expenditure in their respective regions.
  • Advise the state government on equitable development allocation.
  • Submit reports to the Governor.

The boards have been criticised for being toothless advisory bodies with no independent power to compel the state government.

Recruitment Reservation Under Article 371(2)

  • Government jobs in Maharashtra (state services) must provide equitable regional representation.
  • Domicile requirement: For state service posts, candidates must be domiciled in their region; regional allocation in recruitment is a constitutional protection.
  • This prevents a situation where all state jobs go to candidates from one (western Maharashtra) region.
  • Controversy: The implementation of this provision has been contested — questions about what counts as "equitable," whether the three-region division still makes sense, etc.

Vidarbha Statehood Demand vs. Article 371(2)

  • Vidarbha leaders argue that Article 371(2)'s development boards have been ineffective — real allocations continue to favour western Maharashtra.
  • The demand for a separate Vidarbha state (Vidarbya Rajya Andolan) persists precisely because Article 371(2) protections are seen as inadequate.
  • Bacchawat Commission (1984): Investigated regional imbalance in Maharashtra; confirmed Vidarbha and Marathwada's relative underdevelopment.
  • Fadnavis Commission and other similar initiatives have periodically examined this.

Maharashtra Connection

Article 371(2) is a live political issue in Maharashtra — any debate about Vidarbha statehood, development boards, or regional recruitment reservation will refer to this article. The Vidarbha Development Board's annual report is tabled in the Maharashtra legislature — a transparency mechanism that activists use to highlight underfunding. MPSC has tested this article's specific content and the names of the three regions.

Key Facts

  • Article 371(2): Special provisions for Maharashtra (and Gujarat had sub-clause 1)
  • Three regions: Vidarbha (11 districts), Marathwada (8 districts), Rest of Maharashtra (17 districts)
  • Three Development Boards: Vidarbha (Nagpur), Marathwada (Aurangabad), Rest of Maharashtra (Pune)
  • Scope: Equitable development expenditure + public service recruitment across three regions
  • Boards are advisory — no binding power on state government
  • Context: Nagpur Pact (1953) commitments → Article 371(2) protections at formation (1960)
  • Article 371J (Karnataka) is a similar provision for Hyderabad-Karnataka region (introduced 2012)

Exam Traps

  • Article 371 (clause 1) covers Maharashtra AND Gujarat; Article 371(2) covers specifically the three-region provision for Maharashtra only.
  • The Development Boards are advisory — they do NOT have power to compel the state government.
  • The three regions are Vidarbha, Marathwada, Rest of Maharashtra — NOT "Vidarbha, Khandesh, Western Maharashtra" (Khandesh is not a separate region under this Article).
