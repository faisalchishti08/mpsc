Hindustani and Carnatic music PREMAINS

Overview

Indian classical music divides into two great traditions: Hindustani (north India, influenced by Persian-Mughal synthesis) and Carnatic (south India, relatively uninfluenced by Islamic contact). Both share the foundational concepts of Raga (melodic framework) and Tala (rhythmic cycle) but differ in form, instruments, compositions, and philosophy. MPSC tests gharana identification, Carnatic Trinity, key instruments, and composers.

In-Depth

Common Fundamentals

  • Raga: a melodic scale with specific ascending (arohana) and descending (avarohana) patterns; associated with moods, times of day, seasons. Over 300 ragas in Hindustani, ~72 melakarta parent ragas in Carnatic.
  • Tala: rhythmic cycle (e.g., Teentaal = 16 beats, most common in Hindustani; Adi Tala = 8 beats, common in Carnatic).
  • Shruti: microtonal intervals; 22 shrutis in the octave.
  • Saptak: 7 basic notes — Sa, Re, Ga, Ma, Pa, Dha, Ni (solfège: analogous to Do Re Mi...).
  • Sangeet Natak Akademi — apex performing arts body; Tansen Samaroh (Gwalior) is India's biggest classical music festival.

Hindustani Music (North India)

Origin and Evolution:

  • Rooted in the ancient Vedic tradition; significantly transformed by Amir Khusrau (1253–1325) who fused Persian elements, invented (or developed) the sitar, tabla, and the khayal genre.
  • Tansen (Akbar's court, 1506–1589): most celebrated Hindustani musician; master of Dhrupad; credited with ragas Darbari Kanada, Miyan ki Todi, Miyan ki Sarang, Miyan ki Malhar.

Instruments (Hindustani):

  • String (Chordophone): Sitar (Ravi Shankar), Sarod (Ali Akbar Khan), Sarangi, Santoor (Shivkumar Sharma), Veena.
  • Percussion (Membranophone): Tabla (Zakir Hussain), Pakhawaj.
  • Wind (Aerophone): Bansuri (Hariprasad Chaurasia), Shehnai (Bismillah Khan).

Carnatic Music (South India)

Origin: Developed in Tamil Nadu, Karnataka, Andhra Pradesh, Kerala; largely preserved without Mughal influence; devotional (Bhakti) tradition central.

The Carnatic Trinity (18th century):

ComposerPeriodLanguageKey Compositions
Tyagaraja1767–1847TeluguPancharatna Kritis; 600+ compositions; devotee of Rama; refused royal patronage
Muthuswami Dikshitar1775–1835SanskritNavagrahakritis; Kamalamba Navavaranam; deeply influenced by Sanskrit grammar
Syama Sastri1762–1827Telugu/SanskritSwarajatis; compositions on goddess Kamakshi; most concise of the three

All three were born in Tiruvarur (Tamil Nadu) — making Tiruvarur the birthplace of the Carnatic Trinity.

Main Vocal Forms (Carnatic):

  • Kriti: main composition form; Pallavi, Anupallavi, Charana.
  • Varnam: practice piece; teaches basic techniques.
  • Ragam-Tanam-Pallavi: improvised elaboration — equivalent of a Hindustani raga-alap.
  • Padam and Javali: expressive, light compositions.

Instruments (Carnatic):

  • Veena (Saraswati Veena) — the classical string instrument.
  • Mridangam — primary Carnatic percussion (double-headed drum); M.S. Subbulakshmi, T.V. Sankaranarayanan.
  • Violin — adapted into Carnatic music in the 19th century (unusual adoption).
  • Ghatam (clay pot), Kanjira, Morsing — secondary percussion.

M.S. Subbulakshmi (1916–2004): Greatest Carnatic vocalist; first musician to receive Bharat Ratna (1998); performed at the United Nations (1966).

Maharashtra Connection

Maharashtra has a strong Hindustani tradition:

  • Kirana Gharana: Gangubai Hangal (Karnataka/North Karnataka/Maharashtra) and Pandit Bhimsen Joshi (Pune-based; Bharat Ratna 2008) — both Kirana exponents; Bhimsen Joshi's legacy is deeply rooted in Pune.
  • Jaipur-Atrauli Gharana: Kishori Amonkar — born Bombay (1932–2017); one of the greatest Jaipur-Atrauli exponents; Sangeet Natak Akademi award winner.
  • Vasantrao Deshpande — from Maharashtra; strong Agra gharana association.
  • Sawai Gandharva Bhimsen Sangeet Mahotsav (Pune) — India's leading Hindustani music festival, held annually in December, started by Bhimsen Joshi's disciples.
  • Natyasangeet (music for Marathi drama/Tamasha) — a uniquely Maharashtra musical tradition blending classical and folk elements.

Key Facts

  • Raga: melodic framework; Tala: rhythmic cycle.
  • Amir Khusrau: credited with sitar, tabla, khayal — Sultanate period.
  • Tansen: Akbar's court; Dhrupad; Raga Darbari Kanada.
  • Carnatic Trinity: Tyagaraja, Muthuswami Dikshitar, Syama Sastri (all from Tiruvarur).
  • M.S. Subbulakshmi: first musician with Bharat Ratna (1998).
  • Bhimsen Joshi (Kirana Gharana): Bharat Ratna 2008; based in Pune.
  • Gwalior Gharana: oldest Hindustani khayal gharana.
  • Tabla: associated with Zakir Hussain (Padma Vibhushan); Bansuri with Hariprasad Chaurasia.

Exam Traps

  • Tyagaraja composed in Telugu, not Sanskrit (Dikshitar composed in Sanskrit).
  • M.S. Subbulakshmi = first musician Bharat Ratna (1998) — not Bismillah Khan (who got Bharat Ratna in 2001).
  • Tansen belonged to Akbar's court, NOT Jahangir's or Shah Jahan's.
  • Mridangam is Carnatic, Tabla is Hindustani — don't interchange.
