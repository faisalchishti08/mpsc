Preamble — significance and keywords PREMAINS

Overview

The Preamble is the introductory statement of the Constitution of India that embodies its philosophy, objectives and the source of its authority. It declares India to be a Sovereign, Socialist, Secular, Democratic, Republic and secures Justice, Liberty, Equality and Fraternity to all citizens. Frequently asked in MPSC Prelims (keywords, amendment) and Mains (philosophy, basic structure).

In-Depth

Text & Source

The Preamble begins "We, the People of India…" — declaring that the Constitution derives its authority from the people of India. It was adopted on 26 November 1949 and the Constitution came into force on 26 January 1950. It is based on the Objectives Resolution moved by Jawaharlal Nehru (1946) and adopted by the Constituent Assembly.

Key Words (in order)

  • Sovereign — India is internally and externally free, not subordinate to any external authority.
  • Socialist — added by the 42nd Amendment (1976); democratic socialism (mixed economy), not communist.
  • Secular — added by the 42nd Amendment (1976); equal respect to all religions (no state religion).
  • Democratic — government by the people; universal adult franchise.
  • Republic — head of state (President) is elected, not hereditary.
  • Objectives: Justice (social, economic, political), Liberty (thought, expression, belief, faith, worship), Equality (status and opportunity), Fraternity (dignity of the individual and unity & integrity of the nation).

Amendment & Legal Status

  • Amended only once — the 42nd Amendment Act, 1976 added Socialist, Secular, and Integrity.
  • In Berubari Union case (1960), the SC held the Preamble is not a part of the Constitution.
  • In Kesavananda Bharati case (1973), the SC reversed this — the Preamble is a part and can be amended without altering the basic structure.
  • The Preamble is non-justiciable (not enforceable in court) but aids in interpreting the Constitution.

Maharashtra Connection

Not directly Maharashtra-specific. Indirectly, the Preamble's values of social justice and equality resonate with Maharashtra's reform tradition (Phule, Shahu, Ambedkar) — Dr. B.R. Ambedkar, from Maharashtra, chaired the Drafting Committee.

Key Facts

  • Adopted: 26 Nov 1949; in force: 26 Jan 1950.
  • Based on: Objectives Resolution (Nehru, 1946).
  • Amended once: 42nd Amendment, 1976 → added Socialist, Secular, Integrity.
  • Berubari (1960): not part; Kesavananda (1973): part of the Constitution.
  • Non-justiciable; reflects basic structure.

Memory Aids

  • Keywords: "S-S-S-D-R" (Sovereign, Socialist, Secular, Democratic, Republic).
  • 42nd Amendment additions: "S-S-I" (Socialist, Secular, Integrity).
  • Objectives: "JLEF" — Justice, Liberty, Equality, Fraternity.

Exam Traps

  • Socialist & Secular were not in the original Preamble — added in 1976 (42nd Amendment).
  • The Preamble has been amended only once — beware options claiming "never" or "twice".
  • Berubari (not part) vs Kesavananda (part) — get the chronology and verdicts right.
