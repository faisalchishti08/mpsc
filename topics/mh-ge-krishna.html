Krishna and tributaries (Koyna, Panchganga) PREMAINSINTMH

Overview

The Krishna river originates at Mahabaleshwar (Satara district) in the Sahyadri and drains south-western Maharashtra before flowing through Karnataka and Andhra Pradesh to reach the Bay of Bengal. In Maharashtra, its major tributaries include the Koyna, Panchganga, Bhima, Yerala, and Warna rivers. The Krishna system irrigates the prosperous sugarcane belt of western Maharashtra (Kolhapur, Sangli, Satara). MPSC asks about origins, dams (Koyna is the biggest), and inter-state water disputes.

In-Depth

Origin and Course

  • Origin: Mahabaleshwar, Satara district, at the Panchganga temple (elevation ~1,300 m); the spring here gives rise to five rivers including Krishna.
  • Maharashtra course: Flows eastward (~280 km in Maharashtra) through Satara and Sangli districts before entering Karnataka at the Almatti Dam area.
  • Total length: ~1,400 km; exits India into the Bay of Bengal at Vijayawada-Hamasaladeevi area (Andhra Pradesh).

Koyna River and Dam

  • Koyna Dam (Shivasagar): Built 1956–1964; reservoir called Shivasagar Lake; located in Satara district (Koyna taluka); generates ~1,960 MW of electricity — Maharashtra's and one of India's largest hydroelectric power stations.
  • 1967 Koyna Earthquake (11 December 1967, Magnitude 6.5–6.7): India's most damaging reservoir-induced seismic event (RIS); killed ~180 people; caused massive damage to the dam structure and surrounding area; led to strengthening of dam design standards in India. Warna and Koyna WLS together protect the Sahyadri core zone.

Panchganga River

  • The Panchganga (five rivers / five-fold stream) is formed by the confluence of five small streams at Prajapuri, near Panchganga temple, Mahabaleshwar — Koyna, Krishna, Venna, Savitri, Gayatri (some lists vary).
  • Flows through Kolhapur city — Kolhapur routinely floods due to Panchganga overflow during heavy monsoon.
  • Rajaram Dam (on Panchganga, Kolhapur district) regulates flow.

Bhima River — The Longest Krishna Tributary in Maharashtra

  • Originates at Bhimashankar, Pune district (one of the 12 Jyotirlingas).
  • Flows through Pune, Solapur, Osmanabad (Dharashiv) before entering Karnataka.
  • Ujani Dam (Bhigwan, Solapur district): Largest dam in Maharashtra by storage capacity (reservoir = Ujani / Yedgaon); important for Solapur-Pune irrigation; also a major flamingo congregation site (flamingo festival at Bhigwan).
  • Khadakwasla Dam (Pune district): Upstream on Mutha tributary; primary water supply to Pune city; cascaded with Panshet, Varasgaon dams.

Krishna Water Disputes Tribunal (KWDT)

  • KWDT-I (1969): First tribunal for Krishna water allocation among Maharashtra, Karnataka, and Andhra Pradesh. Maharashtra's share: ~560 TMC (thousand million cubic feet).
  • KWDT-II (2004): Second tribunal; revised allocation; Maharashtra's total share: ~666 TMC. Final order still contested.

Maharashtra Connection

The Koyna earthquake (1967) is India's classic case of Reservoir-Induced Seismicity (RIS) — a standard Environment/Science topic. Kolhapur's Panchganga flooding is a recurring climate-disaster topic. Bhigwan (Solapur, on Ujani backwaters) is Maharashtra's flamingo corridor — a popular ecotourism-biodiversity question. Sugarcane cooperatives of Kolhapur-Sangli-Satara are watered by Krishna tributaries — linking river system to Maharashtra's cooperative economy.

Exam Traps

  • Koyna Dam reservoir = Shivasagar Lake (NOT "Koyna Lake" or "Koyna reservoir" in official usage).
  • Bhima joins Krishna in Karnataka, not in Maharashtra — don't place the confluence inside MH.
  • Panchganga is NOT formed at the confluence of five different rivers in the traditional sense — it is five sub-streams at one sacred spot; MPSC questions may list different rivers.
