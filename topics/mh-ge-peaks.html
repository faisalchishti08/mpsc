Highest peaks — Kalsubai, Salher, Mahabaleshwar PREMAINSINTMH

Overview

Maharashtra's highest peaks are concentrated in the Sahyadri (Western Ghats) range. Kalsubai (1,646 m) is the highest peak in Maharashtra and a popular trekking destination. Knowing peak names, heights, and districts is a direct-recall MPSC Prelims target, often tested with wrong heights or wrong districts as distractors.

Kalsubai — Detailed

  • Location: Akole taluka, Ahmednagar district (not Nashik or Pune — a common error).
  • Height: 1,646 m — "Maharashtra's Everest."
  • Lies within Kalsubai Harishchandragad Wildlife Sanctuary.
  • Access: Via Bari village; ~6 km trek; iron ladder sections near summit.
  • Bhandardara reservoir (Arthur Lake) and Randha Falls are nearby — popular tourist circuit.

Salher — Historical Significance

  • Battle of Salher (1672): Maratha forces under Prataprao Gujar and Anandrao Makaji defeated Mughal forces — one of the largest cavalry battles of the Maratha period. First major Maratha victory against the Mughals in open-field battle.
  • Located in Baglan taluka, Nashik — the northernmost extension of the Sahyadri.

Mahabaleshwar — Geographic Hub

  • 5 rivers originate here: Krishna, Venna, Koyna, Gayatri, Savitri — from Panchganga temple (Wilson Point area).
  • Strawberry capital: Mahabaleshwar produces ~85% of India's strawberries.
  • British-era hill station; summer capital of Bombay Presidency.

Chikhaldara — Vidarbha's Hill Station

  • Only hill station in Vidarbha (Amravati district); part of the Satpura–Balaghat range system.
  • Coffee: Only place in Maharashtra (and one of few in central India) where coffee is commercially grown.
  • Close to Melghat Tiger Reserve (Amravati district).
  • Mythological link: Believed to be the place where Bhima (Mahabharata) killed the demon Keechak — hence "Kee-cha-Dara" → Chikhaldara.

Maharashtra Connection

Kalsubai and Harishchandragad (both in Ahmednagar) are part of the same wildlife sanctuary, tying geography with ecology in MPSC questions. The Battle of Salher (Nashik) is a favourite history-geography intersection question. Chikhaldara's association with Melghat Tiger Reserve links peaks to conservation.

  • 2nd highest: Salher (1,567 m) — Nashik district (Baglan taluka)
  • Battle of Salher: 1672 — Maratha vs. Mughal; first major open-field Maratha victory
  • Mahabaleshwar: 1,438 m — source of 5 rivers (Krishna, Venna, Koyna, Gayatri, Savitri); 85% of India's strawberries

Memory Aids

  • K-S-M (Kalsubai-Salher-Mahabaleshwar) = top three heights: 1646-1567-1438
  • "Kalsubai is in Ahmednagar, not Nashik" — memorise the trap
  • Salher = 1672 battle (Salher rhymes with "seventeen" → 1672 is 17th century)
  • Chikhaldara = Vidarbha = Coffee (3 C's: Chikhaldara, Coffee, C-Vidarbha)

Exam Traps

  • Kalsubai is in Ahmednagar, NOT Nashik district — most distractors place it in Nashik.
  • Mahabaleshwar is the source of rivers but NOT the highest peak — at 1,438 m it is 3rd.
  • Battle of Salher (1672) ≠ Treaty of Purandar (1665) — don't mix Salher military history with Purandar treaty history.
