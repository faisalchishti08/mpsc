Overview

Maharashtra has over 350 forts — the highest number in any Indian state — ranging from hill forts (Raigad, Sinhagad, Pratapgad) to sea forts (Sindhudurg, Janjira, Kolaba). They were the backbone of the Maratha military-political system. MPSC tests individual forts' districts, rivers/locations, historical events associated with them, and their current status (UNESCO, ASI-protected, state).

Forts by Category

Hill Forts (Ghad):

  • Most Maratha forts are hill forts, occupying strategic heights in the Sahyadri.
  • Three types: Girigrha (hill fort), Vandurga (jungle/forest fort), Jaldurag (water/sea fort).

Sea Forts (Jaldurga / Samudra Durga):

  • Maharashtra has India's highest concentration of sea forts.
  • Shivaji built or acquired: Sindhudurg, Khanderi, Suvarnadurg, Padmadurg.
  • Siddi-held: Murud-Janjira (NEVER surrendered); Revadanda.

Daulatabad Fort — Architectural Marvel

  • Originally called Devagiri (Yadava capital), renamed Daulatabad by Delhi Sultan Muhammad bin Tughluq who shifted his capital here in 1327 (and then returned to Delhi — a great administrative folly ridiculed by historians).
  • The fort has extraordinary defensive features: a moat, a dark spiralling tunnel (through which defenders could trap attackers), multiple gates, and a massive cannon (Qila-e-Arth or Mendha Toph).
  • ASI protected; UNESCO tentative site.

Murud-Janjira — The Invincible Fort

  • Never captured — by Shivaji, his son Sambhaji, the Peshwas, Kanhoji Angre, the British, or the Portuguese.
  • Held by the Siddi (Habshi/Abyssinian) rulers as an independent principality.
  • Located on a natural island; circular walls with 19 bastions; fresh water spring inside; Arabic cannon "Kalal Banga" still on the fort.

Maharashtra Connection

Maharashtra's fort heritage anchors its cultural tourism and Shiv-Jayanti celebrations. Raigad Fort is perhaps the most revered — Shivaji's coronation (1674) and death (1680) occurred here. Sinhagad Fort outside Pune is a major day-trek and holds the memory of Tanaji Malusare. The Maharashtra Fort Conservation Committee (Sahyadri Mitra, various NGOs) works on restoration. Several forts are now proposed for UNESCO WH status as a group.

Exam Traps

  • Sinhagad (Tanaji, 1670) ≠ Pratapgad (Afzal Khan, 1659) — two different forts, two different events.
  • Torna was captured first (1646), NOT Raigad (which became capital 1674) — Rajgad was capital before Raigad.
  • Murud-Janjira is in Raigad district, NOT Sindhudurg — the two coastal fort districts are confused.
