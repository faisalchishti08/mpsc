Talukas, tehsils and administrative hierarchy PREMAINSINTMH

Overview

The taluka (tehsil) is the sub-district administrative unit in Maharashtra, sitting between the district and the village. Maharashtra has approximately 355 talukas, each headed by a Tahsildar (in charge of revenue matters) and a Tehsildar/Block Development Officer (for development). Understanding the hierarchy — Division → District → Taluka → Village — is essential for MPSC given that it directly mirrors the civil service posting structure.

The Tahsildar

  • Tahsildar (head of taluka) is recruited through Maharashtra Public Service Commission (MPSC) via the Maharashtra Rajyaseva / Subordinate Services exams.
  • Key functions: land records maintenance, revenue collection, birth/death certificates, mutation entries, natural calamity relief at taluka level, caste certificate issuance, election duties.
  • A Naib Tehsildar (junior) assists and can be promoted to Tahsildar.
  • Promotion track: Talathi → Naib Tehsildar → Tahsildar → Deputy Collector → Additional Collector → District Collector.

Talathi (Village Accountant)

  • The Talathi maintains the village-level land record: 7/12 extract (saat-baara utara), Form 8A, mutation entries.
  • The 7/12 extract is Maharashtra's iconic land record document (survey number + cultivator details + crop details) — used in thousands of daily transactions. It can now be accessed online via the Mahabhumi portal.
  • Talathi recruitment: Class III/Group C, through district-level recruitment or MPSC.

Maharashtra's Revenue Laws

  • Maharashtra Land Revenue Code (MLRC), 1966: Governs all revenue administration (surveys, records, land use change, penalties).
  • Maharashtra Tenancy and Agricultural Lands Act, 1948: Regulates tenancy rights.
  • Land records are maintained in the village cadastre (property register) and Tharav Pustika.

Maharashtra Connection

The 7/12 extract (Saat-Baara Utara) is one of the most encountered government documents in rural Maharashtra — used for farm loans, crop insurance, court disputes, and welfare schemes. Digitisation of 7/12 through Mahabhumi (launched ~2015) and e-Mutation has been a major governance reform. MPSC often tests the Tahsildar's functions and MLRC provisions. The Bhulekh (land records) portal is the equivalent reference in other states.

Key Facts

  • Total talukas: approximately 355 (exact count varies slightly with administrative re-division)
  • Taluka head: Tahsildar
  • Village accountant: Talathi (maintains 7/12, Form 8A)
  • Governing law: Maharashtra Land Revenue Code (MLRC), 1966
  • Online land records: Mahabhumi portal (also app: Mahabhumi)
  • Promotion chain ends at: District Collector (from Talathi → Naib Tehsildar → Tahsildar → Deputy Collector → DC)
  • Sub-divisional officer sits between district and taluka

Memory Aids

  • Hierarchy bottom-up: V-T-SD-D-Div-S: Village (Talathi) → Taluka (Tahsildar) → Sub-Division → District (Collector) → Division (Commissioner) → State
  • "7/12 = Saat-Baara" — seven-twelve, not "seven-to-twelve"
  • MLRC = 1966 (not 1961 or 1971 — a common trap year)

Exam Traps

  • Tahsildar ≠ Talathi — Tahsildar heads the taluka; Talathi maintains village records. Confusing the two is a frequent MCQ trap.
  • MLRC year: 1966 — do not confuse with Maharashtra Zilla Parishad Act (1961).
  • The 7/12 extract number refers to two separate registers — Register 7 (survey details) and Register 12 (crop/cultivation details); combined as "7/12."
