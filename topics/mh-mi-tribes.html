Tribes of Maharashtra (Bhil, Warli, Gond, Katkari) PREMAINSINTMH

Overview

Maharashtra has 47 Scheduled Tribes listed under the Constitution (Fifth Schedule areas cover parts of the state). The major tribes — Bhil, Warli, Gond, Katkari, Kokna, Mahadev Koli, Pardhan, Gondi — are concentrated in the Sahyadri ranges, northern/Nandurbar district, and Vidarbha. Tribal demography, culture, and welfare schemes are asked in MPSC across Prelims (identify tribe/region/art) and Mains (tribal rights, development, Fifth Schedule).

Particularly Vulnerable Tribal Groups (PVTG)

Maharashtra has 5 PVTGs (formerly Primitive Tribal Groups): Katkari, Kolam, Maria Gond, Madia Gond, Thakar. PVTGs are identified by declining/stagnant population, pre-agricultural economy, extreme isolation, and backwardness. The Centre provides special funds under the PM PVTG Development Mission (2023) and the Development of Particularly Vulnerable Tribal Groups scheme.

Fifth Schedule Areas

Maharashtra does not have Sixth Schedule areas (which apply to northeast). It is a Fifth Schedule state — tribal areas in Nandurbar, Gadchiroli, and parts of Nashik/Amravati/Chandrapur are under Tribal Sub-Plan and governed by Fifth Schedule provisions (Governor's report, Tribes Advisory Council/TAC).

Art, Culture, and Identity

  • Warli Art: White pigment on mud walls; depict daily life, weddings, hunts in geometric motifs. Jivya Soma Mashe (Padma Shri 2011) brought global recognition.
  • Gondi art / Pardhan Gond art: Intricate dot-and-line patterns; artists like Jangarh Singh Shyam (from MP/Madhya Vidarbha region) popularized it.
  • Tarpa dance: Performed by Warli, Bhil, Kokna tribes to the music of the tarpa (wind instrument); key during harvest festivals.
  • Bhavari/Bhagoria: Bhil festival associated with Holi; pre-marital courtship traditions.

Tribal Welfare Schemes in Maharashtra

  • Scheduled Tribes Development Department: Manages hostels, ashram schools, scholarships.
  • Tribal Research and Training Institute (TRTI), Pune: Research and policy body.
  • Maharashtra Adivasi Vikas Pradhikaran (Nashik): Implements tribal sub-plan projects.
  • Van Adhikar Kanoon (Forest Rights Act, 2006): Implementation in Maharashtra has been contested — state courts have seen multiple FRA cases.

Maharashtra Connection

Tribal communities are deeply woven into Maharashtra's historical, cultural, and ecological identity. Umaji Naik (Mahadev Koli), Raghoji Bhangria (Bhil), and others resisted colonial rule. The Naxal-affected tribal belt (Gadchiroli) continues to be a governance challenge. Warli art is used as Maharashtra's cultural brand in tourism and exports.

Exam Traps

  • Warli painting is from Palghar (not Pune or Nashik) — district matters in MCQs.
  • Katkari is a PVTG; Bhil is not (PVTGs are the more vulnerable, smaller groups).
  • Maharashtra follows Fifth Schedule, NOT Sixth Schedule — Sixth Schedule applies to Assam, Meghalaya, Tripura, Mizoram.
  • Gondi language ≠ Hindi; it is a Dravidian-family language spoken by Gonds.
