First-in-Maharashtra facts PREMAINSINTMH

Overview

"Firsts in Maharashtra" is a high-yield MPSC topic — testing isolated facts about historical precedents, institutional firsts, and state-specific milestones. Maharashtra hosts India's first in many categories: first nuclear plant (Tarapur, 1969), first employment guarantee scheme (Rozgar Hami Yojana, 1965), first women's university (SNDT, 1916), first cooperative sugar mill, first Bombay Plan (1944), and numerous more. This topic consolidates Maharashtra's "firsts" in one place for rapid revision.

Maharashtra Connection

Maharashtra's "firsts" densely cluster in the colonial-era Bombay period (1853 railway, 1875 BSE, 1888 BMC, 1913 cinema, 1916 SNDT) and in the post-Independence scientific era (1956 Apsara, 1969 Tarapur, 1991 PARAM). This concentration reflects Bombay's role as India's commercial capital from the 1860s and its progressive intellectual climate (Phule, Karve, Gokhale, Ambedkar all from Maharashtra).

Exam Traps

  • Bombay-Thane railway (1853) was India's first passenger train — NOT the first railway line in Asia (that is earlier, in UK); first IN INDIA.
  • SEEPZ (1965) was an Export Processing Zone first; the SEZ Act 2005 made it a formal SEZ.
  • Tukaram's Gatha on UNESCO Memory of World (2005) is different from UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage.
