Namdev PREMAINSMH

Overview

Sant Namdev (1270–1350 CE) is one of the most important Warkari saints of Maharashtra — a tailor by caste, universal by spirit. His significance is uniquely pan-Indian: his devotional compositions in Marathi and Hindi are included in the Adi Granth (Guru Granth Sahib), making him the only Maharashtrian saint whose works are enshrined in the Sikh holy scripture. He was a contemporary and devotee-companion of Sant Dnyaneshwar and later undertook extensive journeys across India including a long stay in Punjab, where he deeply influenced the Bhakti tradition there. MPSC tests his works, Punjab stay, and Adi Granth inclusion.

In-Depth

Life

  • Born: ~1270 CE, Narasi Bamani village, Hingoli district, Maharashtra.
  • Caste: Shimpi (Tailor) — a low artisan caste; demonstrates Warkari Bhakti's cross-caste character.
  • Parents: Damasheti (father, a tailor), Gonabai (mother).
  • Guru: Visoba Khechar — a Nath Sampradaya saint; Namdev's spiritual transformation came after meeting Visoba Khechar at Pandharpur.
  • Companion: Close companion of Sant Dnyaneshwar in the early period.
  • Punjab Journey: Namdev spent many years (estimated 20 years) in Punjab — especially at Ghuman village (near Gurdaspur) where a temple in his name stands.
  • Death: ~1350 CE; his samadhi is at Pandharpur, Maharashtra, near the Vitthal temple (inside the Mahadwara).

Adi Granth Contribution: 61 padas (shabads) by Namdev in the Adi Granth — more than any Bhakti saint from outside Punjab. This inclusion happened during Guru Arjan Dev's compilation (1604 CE). Namdev is referred to in the Adi Granth as "Nam Dev".

Philosophy and Teaching

  • Saguna Bhakti (primarily): devotion to Vitthal (Vishnu) with form; personal God.
  • Later compositions (after Punjab): show Nirguna tendencies — formless God; some scholars see a shift.
  • Core teaching: God is present everywhere; Vitthal is omnipresent; ritual caste distinctions are irrelevant before God.
  • Famous Abhanga: "Vitthal Vitthal jai jai Vitthal" — universally known Warkari chant.
  • Janabai: Namdev's household maid/disciple who also became a saint; her abhangas are in the Warkari corpus; she describes doing work with Vitthal's help.

Punjab Stay and Legacy

  • Namdev's stay in Punjab (estimated 14th c.) was transformative for North Indian Bhakti.
  • Ghuman, Gurdaspur district, Punjab: Namdev's Dargah/Samadhi here; still a pilgrimage site; local Hindus and Sikhs revere it.
  • Guru Nanak's compositions are believed to have been influenced by Namdev's bhakti.
  • Namdev is the bridge between Maharashtra's Warkari Bhakti and Punjab's Bhakti tradition.

Social Significance

  • Shimpi (Tailor) caste: Namdev's elevation to sainthood challenges the Brahmin monopoly on spiritual authority.
  • Stories (Abhanga narratives): Namdev's devotion was so pure that Vitthal (God) came personally to complete Namdev's unfinished work (one famous story: Vitthal helped grind flour for Namdev).
  • Janabai: his maid-disciple — female and servant-class; both marginalised groups included in Warkari tradition.

Maharashtra Connection

  • Born in Hingoli district, Maharashtra: Narasi Bamani village; major site of veneration.
  • Samadhi at Pandharpur: inside the Vitthal temple precinct; deepest connection to the heart of Warkari tradition.
  • 61 padas in Adi Granth: unique national-level recognition for a Maharashtrian saint.
  • Warkari Tradition: Namdev is the second in the great chain (Dnyaneshwar → Namdev → Eknath → Tukaram → Ramdas).
  • The Namdev Maharaj Palkhi (palanquin procession) travels from Narasi Bamani/Pandharpur during Wari.
  • Hingoli district holds annual Namdev-related events; Narasi Bamani is a pilgrimage site.

Exam Traps

  • Namdev's 61 padas in Adi Granth — many candidates know he is in Adi Granth but forget the number (61) or attribute the inclusion to Guru Nanak (who was before him; compiler was Guru Arjan Dev, 1604).
  • Namdev's samadhi = Pandharpur (inside Vitthal temple gate) — NOT Alandi (which is Dnyaneshwar's).
  • Shimpi = Tailor (not Mahar/Chamar/other low castes — Namdev was a tailor, not an untouchable).
