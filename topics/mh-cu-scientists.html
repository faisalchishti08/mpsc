IUCAA, NCCS, NCL — Maharashtra's Science Institutions

  • IUCAA (Inter-University Centre for Astronomy and Astrophysics): Pune; founded 1988 by J.V. Narlikar under UGC; India's premier astrophysics centre; Chandrayaan, AstroSat data analysis.
  • NCCS (National Centre for Cell Science): Pune; premier cell biology research; under Dept. of Biotechnology.
  • NCL (CSIR-National Chemical Laboratory): Pune; catalysis, polymer science, nanotechnology; ~800 scientists.
  • CSIR-NEERI (National Environmental Engineering Research Institute): Nagpur; environmental research; water/air quality; Vidarbha pollution studies.
  • CDAC (Centre for Development of Advanced Computing): Pune and other centres; developed PARAM supercomputers.

Homi Bhabha — Deeper Notes

  • Bhabha's three-stage nuclear power programme (designed in 1950s):
  • Stage 1: Pressurised Heavy Water Reactors (PHWR) using natural uranium → produce plutonium.
  • Stage 2: Fast Breeder Reactors (FBR) using Pu + Thorium → breed U-233.
  • Stage 3: Advanced Heavy Water Reactors using U-233 (thorium cycle) → long-term energy from thorium (India has world's 3rd largest thorium reserves).
  • Tarapur (1969) used imported technology; domestic PHWRs (RAPS, MAPS, NAPS, KAPS, KGS) followed Bhabha's model.
  • BARC's Apsara reactor (1956): Asia's first operating nuclear reactor; used enriched uranium fuel from USA; achieved first criticality 4 August 1956.

Maharashtra Connection

Maharashtra is home to India's premier science institutions — TIFR, BARC (Mumbai), NIV, Serum Institute, IUCAA, NCL, NCCS (Pune). This concentration is not accidental — J.R.D. Tata, Homi Bhabha, and Narlikar all chose Pune or Mumbai as their base. Vijay Bhatkar's PARAM supercomputer (1991 Pune) showed India's indigenous technological capability when the US denied Cray computers (technology denial → innovation response).
