Painting schools — Rajput, Pahari, Mughal, Deccan PREMAINS

Overview

Indian painting schools span from prehistoric cave paintings (Bhimbetka, 30,000 BCE) through medieval miniature traditions — Mughal, Rajput, Pahari, Deccan — to folk art forms (Warli, Madhubani, Pattachitra). MPSC tests the miniature school names and their features, famous paintings/painters, and Maharashtra's Warli tradition. Ajanta Murals (Cave Paintings)

  • Medium: Tempera (not fresco) — lime plaster applied to cave wall; pigments applied on dry surface.
  • Pigments: local minerals + imports (lapis lazuli blue from Afghanistan).
  • Themes: Jataka stories (past lives of Buddha), court scenes, flora/fauna.
  • Cave 1: Padmapani Bodhisattva (holding lotus) + Vajrapani — two most famous murals.
  • Cave 17: Most paintings; largest narrative programme.
  • Painting technique: outline drawn first, then colours filled; shadows from darker shading.

Maharashtra Connection

Maharashtra's painting heritage is multi-layered:

  • Warli Painting: indigenous tribal art of the Warli community (Palghar/Nashik tribal belt); white on mud-coloured background; geometric human figures in circles; themes of nature, farming, marriage, festivals.
  • Jivya Soma Mhase (1934–2018): credited with taking Warli to canvas and global recognition; Padma Shri awardee; from Ganjad village, Palghar.
  • Soma Dubu (Jivya's son): continues tradition.
  • Ajanta Murals: Maharashtra's crown jewel in painting; 30 caves; Gupta Buddhist art; UNESCO 1983.
  • Deccan School of Miniature Painting: centred in Bijapur (now Karnataka border region) and Ahmednagar — depicting Adil Shahi, Nizam Shahi courts; strong Maharashtra cultural connection.
  • Chitrakathi (Pinguli): tribal painted narrative tradition from Sindhudurg, Maharashtra; visual story-scrolls of Pandava stories.

Exam Traps

  • Ajanta = Tempera, NOT fresco — pigments applied on dry lime plaster, not wet.
  • Bani Thani = Kishangarh (Rajputana) — NOT Mughal school.
  • Deccan school is independent of Mughal school — developed parallel to it; earlier Deccani style precedes Mughal influence.
  • Warli is from Palghar/Nashik Maharashtra — NOT any other tribal community or state.
