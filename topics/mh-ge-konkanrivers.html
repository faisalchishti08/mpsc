West-flowing Konkan rivers (Ulhas, Savitri, Vashishti) PREMAINSINTMH

Overview

The west-flowing Konkan rivers originate on the western escarpment of the Sahyadri and drain directly into the Arabian Sea across the narrow Konkan coastal strip. They are short, fast, rain-fed rivers — unlike the long eastward-flowing Deccan rivers. The major ones are Ulhas, Savitri, Vashishti, Kalu, Patalganga, Kundalika, Amba, Vasai Creek (in the north) and Shastri (Vashishti), Jagadi, Terekhol (in the south). MPSC tests their names, district associations, and any associated dams or estuaries.

Mumbai's Water Supply Rivers

The Ulhas river system (Ulhas + Kalu + tributaries) along with the Vaitarna system (north of Thane) supplies most of Mumbai's water:

  • Tansa Lake/Dam (on Tansa river, Thane district): One of the oldest and most important Mumbai water supply dams (1892, British era).
  • Modak Sagar, Vihar, Tulsi, Bhatsa dams — all in Thane-Nashik area, feeding Mumbai.
  • Vaitarna (Nashik-Thane): Two dams — Upper and Middle Vaitarna — critical to Mumbai water supply.

Savitri River and Disasters

  • The Savitri bridge (Mahad, Raigad) collapsed on 2 August 2016 during the monsoon floods, killing ~28 people when a state transport bus fell into the river — a major infrastructure tragedy.
  • 2005 Mahad floods: Triggered by a cloudburst; entire Savitri valley flooded.

Maharashtra Connection

The Konkan rivers are central to Mumbai's water supply (Ulhas-Vaitarna system), flood disasters (Savitri-Mahad, Vashishti-Chiplun), hydroelectric power (Kundalika/Bhira), and industrial belts (Patalganga). The 2021 Chiplun floods (Vashishti river) and 2016 Savitri bridge collapse are critical current-affairs questions. Kolad (Kundalika river, Roha, Raigad) is Maharashtra's white-water rafting hub — a tourism question.

Exam Traps

  • Savitri originates at Mahabaleshwar (same source as Krishna, Venna etc.) — it is a Konkan/west-flowing river, NOT a Deccan river despite Mahabaleshwar origin.
  • Vashishti river is also called Shastri in its lower reaches near Ratnagiri — both names refer to the same river.
  • Terekhol forms the Maharashtra-Goa border (extreme south of Konkan); do not confuse with Ulhas (extreme north).
