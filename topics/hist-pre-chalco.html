Chalcolithic cultures — Jorwe, Daimabad (Maharashtra) PREMAINSMH

Overview

The Chalcolithic Age (Copper-Stone Age, ~3500–700 BCE) is defined by the use of both copper/bronze tools and stone tools — bridging the Neolithic and Iron Age. In the Indian subcontinent, Chalcolithic cultures flourished in the Deccan, Rajasthan, Central India, and the Ganga Plain, producing distinctive painted pottery, agricultural communities, and evidence of early trade. Maharashtra is particularly central to this period via the Jorwe and Malwa cultures and the extraordinary site of Daimabad. High MPSC priority due to the Maharashtra-specific angle.

Jorwe Culture — Maharashtra's Heart of Chalcolithic

The Jorwe culture (~1400–700 BCE) is the most extensively studied Chalcolithic phase in Maharashtra:

  • Type site: Jorwe village, Ahmednagar district (excavated by H.D. Sankalia, 1950).
  • Key excavated sites: Inamgaon (Pune district, excavated 1969–1986 by Deccan College), Daimabad (Ahmednagar), Nevasa, Chandoli, Songaon.
  • Pottery: Fine black-on-red ware with geometric and floral motifs.
  • Houses: Rectangular mud-walled structures; post-holes indicate thatched roofs; evidence of storage pits (grain).
  • Diet: Wheat, barley, jowar, gram; cattle, sheep, goat, pig; fish and deer hunted.
  • Burials: Dead buried in urn burials (children in pots placed under house floors), adults in extended burials in settlement or near it — unusual in Indian prehistoric context.
  • Social structure at Inamgaon: Evidence of differential status — some houses larger with more storage; some burials with more goods → beginning of social inequality.
  • Late Jorwe decline (~1000–700 BCE): Evidence of settlement abandonment, smaller sites, reduced craft production — possibly linked to desiccation (drought) in the Deccan.

Daimabad — The Bronze-Age Marvel

Daimabad (Ahmednagar district) is the most spectacular Chalcolithic site in Maharashtra:

  • Located on the left bank of the Pravara river — a tributary of the Godavari.
  • A large multi-phase site (Savalda → Daimabad → Malwa → Jorwe occupation layers).
  • Famous for the Daimabad Bronzes: A hoard of four large cast-bronze objects found in 1974:
  1. Chariot (with driver) — large bronze chariot with articulated wheels.
  2. Rhinoceros with rider.
  3. Elephant with rider.
  4. Buffalo with rider.
  • These are the largest and most complex Bronze Age castings from the Deccan; some scholars debate whether they are Harappan or indigenous Chalcolithic (the site has a Harappan-phase layer too).
  • Currently in the collection of the Prince of Wales Museum (Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Vastu Sangrahalaya, Mumbai).

Inamgaon — The Most Studied Village

Inamgaon (Shrigonda taluka, Pune district) has the most detailed Chalcolithic village picture in India:

  • Occupation phases: Malwa (transitional) → Early Jorwe → Late Jorwe.
  • Excavated structures: Rectangular houses, streets, storage pits, hearths.
  • Agriculture: Jowar and wheat; irrigation channels (preliminary evidence).
  • Craft: Copper tools, terracotta figurines, painted pots, bone tools.
  • Burial: Child urn burials; adult burials in extended position.
  • Evidence of community leadership (a large central structure interpreted as a chief's house or assembly hall).

Maharashtra Connection

Maharashtra is the core of the South Asian Chalcolithic study. Jorwe, Daimabad, Inamgaon, Nevasa are all in Maharashtra and represent India's best-studied Chalcolithic village complexes. The Daimabad bronzes are a cultural treasure of Maharashtra. Research by H.D. Sankalia (Deccan College) established the chronological sequence for the entire region.

Key Facts

  • Jorwe type site: Jorwe, Ahmednagar (H.D. Sankalia, 1950).
  • Inamgaon (Pune dist.): Most excavated Chalcolithic village in India.
  • Daimabad (Ahmednagar dist.): Largest Bronze Age castings from Deccan — 4 bronzes (chariot, rhino, elephant, buffalo).
  • Daimabad bronzes now at: Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Vastu Sangrahalaya (Prince of Wales Museum), Mumbai.
  • Jorwe pottery: black-on-red ware.
  • Chalcolithic = copper + stone (no iron).
  • Child burials: urn burials under house floors (Jorwe culture).
  • Jorwe decline: ~700 BCE possibly due to drought/desiccation.

Exam Traps

  • Jorwe is the name of both the culture and the type site (Ahmednagar) — don't say Inamgaon is the type site.
  • Daimabad bronzes are Chalcolithic/Bronze Age finds — not Iron Age, not Harappan (though the site has a Harappan layer).
  • Chalcolithic ≠ Bronze Age exclusively — Chalcolithic literally means copper-stone, not full bronze metallurgy.
  • Child urn burials (pots) are a Jorwe feature, not common to all Chalcolithic cultures.
