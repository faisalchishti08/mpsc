Agriculture schemes — Jalyukt Shivar, Magel Tyala Shettale PREMAINSINTMH

Overview

Maharashtra has launched numerous agriculture schemes targeting drought-proofing, water conservation, irrigation expansion, farmer credit, and crop insurance. Two flagship schemes — Jalyukt Shivar Abhiyan (drought-proofing, 2014–19) and Magel Tyala Shettale / Magel Tyala Jodani (irrigation linkage on demand) — define the Devendra Fadnavis-era rural policy. Under subsequent governments, Namo Shetkari Mahasanman Nidhi (₹6,000/year top-up) and Chhatrapati Shivaji Shetkari Sanman Yojana (loan waiver) are central. MPSC tests scheme names, year, amount, implementing agency, and coverage.

Jalyukt Shivar Abhiyan — Details

  • Launched: 2015 (first phase); run till 2019 under Fadnavis government.
  • Aim: Make villages water self-sufficient by deepening streams, building bandharas (check dams), diverting nullahs.
  • ~16,000 villages covered; ~9,500 villages declared drought-free by government.
  • CAG report (2020): Questioned water storage figures; alleged inflated outcomes; methodology questioned.
  • Revived: Jalyukt Shivar Abhiyan 2.0 announced under Devendra Fadnavis (when he returned as CM in 2022).

Drone and Technology in Maharashtra Agriculture

  • Nanaji Deshmukh Krishi Sanjivani Yojana (PoCRA): Project for Climate Resilient Agriculture (₹4,000 crore World Bank aided); Marathwada + Vidarbha; promotes climate-smart agriculture (CSA).
  • Atal Bhujal Yojana: Groundwater management for water-scarce areas.
  • e-Pik Pahani (e-Crop Survey): Digital crop survey app; farmers report sown area via mobile; links to crop insurance and scheme benefits.
  • MH AgriStack: Maharashtra's digital agriculture database — farmer registry, land records, crop data linked to Aadhaar.

PM-Kisan and Maharashtra

  • PM-Kisan (2019 — central): ₹6,000/year in 3 installments of ₹2,000 to eligible farmers owning up to 2 hectares.
  • Maharashtra initially had delays in database integration; now operational.
  • Namo Shetkari Mahasanman doubles this to ₹12,000/year total from 2023.

Maharashtra Connection

Agriculture schemes are the most politically sensitive area in Maharashtra — loan waivers and price support affect election outcomes. The Onion Export Ban (periodic, by central govt) triggers protests in Nashik and is a local political flashpoint. The cotton MSP (Minimum Support Price) and procurement in Vidarbha are annually debated issues. Maharashtra's drought-prone Marathwada and Vidarbha make water and credit schemes more critical here than most states.

Exam Traps

  • Jalyukt Shivar is a state scheme — NOT a central scheme (PMKSY is central).
  • Namo Shetkari adds ₹6,000 to PM-Kisan's ₹6,000; total = ₹12,000 (two separate schemes, both required).
  • PMFBY premium for Kharif = 2%, Rabi = 1.5%, Horticulture = 5% (farmer's share; rest borne by govt).
