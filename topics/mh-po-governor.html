Governor and Chief Minister PREMAINSINTMH

Overview

Maharashtra's Governor (Rajyapal) is the constitutional head of the state appointed by the President of India under Article 153–162. The Chief Minister (Mukhyamantri) is the real executive head, heading the Council of Ministers. MPSC tests the powers, appointment, term, and relationship between the Governor, CM, and Mantralaya in the Maharashtra context — often with application-based questions about Governor's discretionary powers.

Governor's Powers

Executive Powers (Article 154, 163–166):

  • All executive actions of state government taken in Governor's name.
  • Appoints CM; appoints other ministers on CM's advice; appoints Advocate General, State Election Commissioner, State Finance Commission, MPSC members.
  • Can ask CM to prove majority in assembly (discretionary in hung assembly situations).

Legislative Powers:

  • Summons, prorogues, and dissolves the Vidhan Sabha on CM's advice.
  • Addresses the first session of legislature after elections.
  • Nominates 12 members to Vidhan Parishad.
  • Assent to bills: Can assent, return (for reconsideration), or reserve for President's assent (for central consideration — e.g., bills affecting High Court's jurisdiction).
  • Ordinance power (Article 213): Can promulgate ordinances when Vidhan Sabha is not in session; has effect of Act of Legislature; must be placed before legislature within 6 weeks of next session.

Discretionary Powers:

  • Appointing CM when no single party has majority (hung assembly) — most politically sensitive discretionary power.
  • Dismissing Council of Ministers if it loses majority.
  • Seeking information from CM (Article 167).
  • Reserving a bill for President's consideration.

Maharashtra-Specific Controversies

  • 1999 Political Crisis: After elections, Governor P.C. Alexander controversially swore in a BJP-led government (Narayan Rane as CM) that did not have majority on the floor — later reversed; Vilasrao Deshmukh became CM.
  • 2019 Political Crisis: One of India's most dramatic constitutional episodes — Governor Bhagat Singh Koshyari swore in Devendra Fadnavis as CM at 5 AM without a proper majority; Fadnavis resigned within 80 hours; the Shiv Sena-NCP-Congress Maha Vikas Aghadi government (Uddhav Thackeray as CM) was then formed. Tested President's/Governor's discretionary powers extensively.

Chief Minister (Mukhyamantri)

  • Article 164: CM appointed by Governor; must command confidence of Vidhan Sabha.
  • Council of Ministers: Headed by CM; collectively responsible to Vidhan Sabha.
  • Cabinet: CM + Cabinet Ministers; also Ministers of State and Deputy Ministers.
  • Oath: Administered by Governor; oath of office and secrecy.
  • CM can be from either Vidhan Sabha or Vidhan Parishad.
  • Article 167: CM has duty to communicate to Governor all cabinet decisions + proposed legislation.

Raj Bhavan, Mumbai

  • Governor's official residence: Raj Bhavan, Malabar Hill, Mumbai — one of India's most beautiful governors' residences (former Governor-General's mansion); also has a Pune Raj Bhavan (summer residence).

Maharashtra Connection

The 2019 political crisis (Fadnavis midnight oath) became a nationally discussed case study of Governor's discretionary powers — covered in multiple law journals and UPSC/MPSC questions. The Bombay High Court (Mumbai) is the competent court for challenges to Governor's actions in Maharashtra. Maharashtra's political volatility (multiple coalition governments) makes the Governor-CM relationship a live administrative topic.

Key Facts

  • Governor appointed by: President (Article 155); serves at President's pleasure
  • Raj Bhavan: Malabar Hill, Mumbai; also Pune Raj Bhavan
  • Governor's term: 5 years (but at President's pleasure)
  • VP nominations by Governor: 12 members (arts, literature, science, social service)
  • Ordinance power: Article 213; must be placed before legislature within 6 weeks
  • Article 167: CM must communicate cabinet decisions to Governor
  • 2019 crisis: Governor Koshyari swore in Fadnavis without floor test; reversed within 80 hours

Exam Traps

  • Governor's term = 5 years but "at President's pleasure" means effectively no security of tenure.
  • Governor cannot be removed by the state legislature or state CM — only by President.
  • Ordinance under Article 213 has the same force as an Act but expires 6 weeks after next assembly session (if not re-enacted or disapproved).
