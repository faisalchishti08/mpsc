Zilla Parishads, Panchayat Samitis, Gram Panchayats PREMAINSINTMH

Overview

Rural local self-government in Maharashtra operates through a three-tier Panchayati Raj structure established by the Maharashtra Zilla Parishads, Panchayat Samitis, Gram Panchayats and Nyaya Panchayats Act, 1961. This Act — often called the "ZPPS Act 1961" — preceded the 73rd Constitutional Amendment (1992) and has been amended to comply with it. MPSC tests the structure, composition, powers, elections, and key differences from other states.

Zilla Parishad (ZP)

  • 34 Zilla Parishads in Maharashtra (Mumbai City and Mumbai Suburban have BMC instead; Thane and Palghar have separate ZPs).
  • Members (Sadsya) are directly elected from wards for 5-year terms.
  • President (Adhyaksha): elected by members from among themselves (indirect election).
  • CEO (Chief Executive Officer): an IAS officer appointed by state government — the principal executive authority. CEO countersigns most ZP resolutions.
  • ZP has standing committees (Finance, Public Works, Education, Health, Women & Child, Social Justice).
  • ZP controls district-level functions: secondary education, district hospitals, district roads, agriculture extension, social welfare.

Panchayat Samiti (PS)

  • Approximately 351 Panchayat Samitis across Maharashtra (one per taluka, roughly).
  • Members include directly elected Panch Sadsya + ex-officio members (Sarpanches of GPs within the samiti, cooperative bank reps, MLAs/MLCs as non-voting members).
  • Sabhaneti (Chairman): elected by PS members.
  • BDO (Block Development Officer, Class I/II officer) is the chief executive; recruited through MPSC.

Gram Panchayat (GP)

  • Approximately 27,920 Gram Panchayats in Maharashtra covering ~43,665 villages.
  • Population range: GP for population 600–24,999; above 25,000 → Nagar Panchayat threshold.
  • Sarpanch: elected — originally by GP members (indirect); post 2017 amendment, Maharashtra switched to direct election of Sarpanch by voters.
  • Gram Sabha: All registered voters of the GP — the foundational democratic body; must meet at least twice a year (Maharashtra rule: 4 times recommended).
  • Gram Sevak (also called Gramsevak): Government-appointed multipurpose village-level worker, acts as GP secretary.
  • Nyaya Panchayat: Dispute resolution body for petty civil and criminal disputes at village level; established under the 1961 Act.

73rd Amendment Compliance

Maharashtra amended the 1961 Act to comply with the 73rd Constitutional Amendment (1992), which mandated:

  • 33% reservation for women in all three tiers (Maharashtra provides 50% reservation for women since 2011 — state exceeded the constitutional minimum).
  • Reservation for SC/ST in proportion to population.
  • State Election Commission (SEC) to supervise elections.
  • State Finance Commission (SFC) to recommend devolution of funds.
  • Gram Sabha mandatory.

Maharashtra Connection

Maharashtra's 50% reservation for women in Panchayati Raj (since 2011, enhanced from 33%) is a landmark policy. The Gram Sabha meetings are linked to welfare scheme implementation (MGNREGS, housing, toilets). The ZP CEO model — where an IAS officer controls ZP administration — is distinctive compared to fully elected-body states. Panchayat Raj Prashikshan Sanstha (Pune) trains elected representatives.

Key Facts

  • Governing Act: ZPPS Act, 1961 (Maharashtra ZPs, Panchayat Samitis, GPs and Nyaya Panchayats Act)
  • Zilla Parishads: 34 (excluding Mumbai city which has BMC)
  • Panchayat Samitis: ~351
  • Gram Panchayats: ~27,920
  • ZP head (elected): Adhyaksha; executive: CEO (IAS)
  • PS head (elected): Sabhaneti; executive: BDO
  • GP head (directly elected since 2017): Sarpanch; appointed: Gram Sevak
  • Women reservation in ZP/PS/GP: 50% (enhanced from 33% in 2011)
  • 73rd Amendment year: 1992

Memory Aids

  • ZP-PS-GP: "Z-P-G — Zilla (district), Panchayat Samiti (block), Gram (village)"
  • CEO = IAS (district); BDO = Class I/II (taluka); Gramsevak = Group B/C (village)
  • 73rd Amendment = 1992; Maharashtra enhanced women's quota to 50% in 2011

Exam Traps

  • ZP head is Adhyaksha (not President in English usage in MPSC context) — both are correct but Marathi term Adhyaksha is more often seen in papers.
  • Mumbai City and Mumbai Suburban districts do NOT have Zilla Parishads — they have the Brihanmumbai Municipal Corporation (BMC).
  • Sarpanch is now directly elected (since 2017 amendment) — older materials say indirectly elected.
