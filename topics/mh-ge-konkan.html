Konkan coastal strip PREMAINSINTMH

Overview

The Konkan is Maharashtra's western coastal strip, lying between the Sahyadri (Western Ghats) escarpment and the Arabian Sea. It stretches from the Tapi river mouth in the north to the Goa border in the south, covering approximately 720 km of coastline. Konkan is topographically, culturally, and economically distinct from the Deccan plateau — its geography, rivers, agriculture (Alphonso mango, cashew, coconut, rice), and ports are high-frequency MPSC topics.

Topography and Drainage

  • Ria coast: The Konkan has a ria coastline — drowned river valleys creating natural creeks (khadi) and small estuaries. This makes it ideal for small fishing harbours but difficult for large port construction (exception: Mumbai's natural harbour).
  • Short, west-flowing rivers: Ulhas, Savitri, Vashishti, Kalu, Patalganga — all originate in the Sahyadri, flow westward, and drain into the Arabian Sea. They are seasonal (monsoon-fed) and carry heavy silt.
  • Islands: Mumbai's geography has historically been a group of seven islands (merged by British land reclamation). Elephanta Island (Gharapuri) — UNESCO WH — lies in Mumbai harbour.

Climate and Rainfall

  • Heavy monsoon rainfall: 2,000–5,000 mm annually (among the highest in India). Mumbai averages ~2,400 mm; Mahabaleshwar area (Sahyadri face) gets 5,000+ mm.
  • July is the peak rainfall month; the coast receives SW monsoon directly.
  • Humidity is very high; temperatures moderate (20–35°C range).

Agriculture and Economy

  • Alphonso Mango (Hapus): GI-tagged from Ratnagiri and Devgad; world-renowned premium variety.
  • Cashew nut: Sindhudurg is Maharashtra's top cashew district.
  • Kokum (Garcinia indica): Konkan's unique crop; GI tag; used in cuisine, beverages, cosmetics.
  • Rice: Paddy is the primary cereal (not wheat/jowar of the Deccan).
  • Fishing: Major industry — Malvan (Sindhudurg), Ratnagiri, Alibag are important fishing centres. Bombay Duck (Bombil) is a Konkan-specific dried fish.
  • Mumbai Port and JNPT: India's largest container port (JNPT/Nhava Sheva) and historic Mumbai Port (Mumbai Port Trust) are the economic anchors of the Konkan coast.

Key Locations

  • Alibag: HQ of Raigad district; historic Kolaba Fort (sea fort, 17th century Maratha).
  • Ratnagiri: GI Alphonso mango district; fort of Ratnadurg; birthplace of Lokmanya Tilak.
  • Malvan: Sindhudurg coast; Sindhudurg Fort (1664–67, built by Shivaji on an island — first Maratha sea fort; Shivaji's famous handprint in mortar on entrance wall).
  • Harihareshwar, Ganpatipule, Tarkarli: major Konkan beaches.
  • Murud-Janjira: Raigad district; the only fort never captured — even by Shivaji or the British; seat of Siddi rulers (Abyssinians/Habshis).

Konkan Railway

  • Konkan Railway Corporation (KRC): Built 1993–1998; Roha (Raigad) to Thokur (Mangalore border); ~760 km; crosses 2,000 bridges and 92 tunnels (longest: Karbude Tunnel, 6.5 km); an engineering marvel cutting through Sahyadri.

Maharashtra Connection

The Sindhudurg Fort (1664) built by Shivaji Maharaj is both a history and geography staple — it was his first sea fort and proof of Maratha naval ambitions. Ratnagiri is the birthplace of Lokmanya Bal Gangadhar Tilak (Independence movement) and the home of Babasaheb Ambedkar's childhood links via Konkan. Murud-Janjira (the unconquered island fort) is a common "which fort was never captured" MCQ.

Exam Traps

  • Konkan Railway was completed in 1998, NOT 1993 (construction started 1993).
  • Sindhudurg Fort is in Malvan, Sindhudurg district, NOT Ratnagiri — same general region but different districts.
  • Murud-Janjira (Siddi fort) ≠ Janjira (Sindhudurg) — Murud-Janjira is in Raigad district, not Sindhudurg.
