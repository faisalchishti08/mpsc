Bharat Ratna and Padma awardees from Maharashtra PREMAINSINTMH

Overview

Maharashtra has produced more Bharat Ratna (India's highest civilian honour) and Padma Award recipients than most other states — a reflection of its disproportionate contribution to India's political, cultural, scientific, and social life. Key Bharat Ratna recipients with Maharashtra connections include B.R. Ambedkar (posthumous 1990), M.S. Subbulakshmi (1998) (not Maharashtra), Sachin Tendulkar (2014), Lata Mangeshkar (2001), and D.K. Karve (1958). MPSC tests recipient years, fields, and whether the award was posthumous.

Sachin Tendulkar — Bharat Ratna 2014 (Special Notes)

  • First sportsperson to receive Bharat Ratna.
  • First cricketer to receive Bharat Ratna.
  • Bharat Ratna awarded at 40 years — one of the youngest recipients.
  • He received it while still being a Rajya Sabha member (nominated 2012, retired from cricket Nov 2013).
  • The Bharat Ratna citation mentions his "extraordinary contribution to cricket, which has brought pride and joy to millions across the world."

B.R. Ambedkar — Bharat Ratna 1990 (Posthumous)

  • Awarded posthumously in 1990 by V.P. Singh government.
  • Ambedkar died 6 December 1956 — 34 years before the award.
  • This posthumous award was significant — it came during the Mandal Commission controversy (1990) and was seen as a political gesture.
  • MPSC specifically tests: Ambedkar's Bharat Ratna was posthumous and in 1990 (NOT 1956 or 1947).

D.K. Karve — Bharat Ratna 1958 (Oldest Recipient at that Time)

  • D.K. Karve (Dhondo Keshav Karve, "Annasaheb Karve") received Bharat Ratna in 1958 on his 100th birthday (born 18 April 1858).
  • He was the first social reformer from Maharashtra to receive Bharat Ratna.
  • SNDT Women's University (1916) was his key contribution.
  • Died 9 November 1962 aged 104 — one of the longest-lived Bharat Ratna recipients.

Maharashtra Connection

The density of Bharat Ratna and Padma awards for Maharashtra nationals (Ambedkar, Karve, JRD Tata, Lata, Sachin, Asha Bhosle) reflects Maharashtra's outsized contribution to India's public life across fields. MPSC tests these awards frequently because they combine "who from Maharashtra won what national award and when" — a factual category with predictable MCQ potential.

Exam Traps

  • Ambedkar's Bharat Ratna is POSTHUMOUS (1990) — he died in 1956; do NOT say 1956 award.

  • Asha Bhosle received Dadasaheb Phalke Award in 2000 (NOT Lata — Lata got Phalke in 1989, Bharat Ratna in 2001).
