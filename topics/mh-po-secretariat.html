Mantralaya and state secretariat PREMAINSINTMH

Overview

The Mantralaya (State Secretariat) in Mumbai is the nerve centre of Maharashtra's executive government — home to the Chief Minister's Office, all department secretaries, and the administrative machinery through which cabinet decisions are implemented. Understanding the Mantralaya's structure, key positions, and the officer cadre is essential for MPSC aspirants who will work within this system.

In-Depth

Mantralaya — Physical and Administrative Setup

  • Location: Mantralaya Building, Madame Cama Road, Nariman Point, South Mumbai.
  • The building is a major skyscraper in South Mumbai's business district.
  • All state government departments (Finance, Revenue, Home, Agriculture, PWD, etc.) have their secretariat offices here.
  • Vidhan Bhavan (Legislature building) is nearby (also in South Mumbai, Churchgate area) — not part of Mantralaya but adjacent in administrative geography.

Maharashtra Civil Service (MCS) — MPSC-Recruited

  • MPSC recruits officers for the Maharashtra Civil Service (MCS) through the Maharashtra Rajyaseva Exam.
  • MCS officers serve as Tehsildars, Deputy Collectors, Sub-Divisional Officers, and eventually as Secretaries/Additional Secretaries to the state government.
  • Cadre strength: Maharashtra cadre IAS (~300 all-India cadre allocated to MH) + Maharashtra State Civil Service (~5,000+ MCS officers).
  • MPSC also recruits for Maharashtra Police Service, Maharashtra Finance Service, Maharashtra Revenue Service, etc.

Mantralaya Departments

Maharashtra has approximately 48 departments organized into groups. Key ones tested in MPSC:

  • Home Department: Law and order, police; Mumbai Police Commissioner reports to it.
  • Finance Department: Budget; fiscal policy; FFC recommendations.
  • Revenue and Forest Department: Land records, revenue administration; also Forest Department (forests, wildlife).
  • Agriculture Department: Farm schemes; drought relief.
  • Public Works Department (PWD): Roads, bridges, government buildings.
  • General Administration Department (GAD): IAS/MCS postings, transfers, promotions; "Personnel Department."
  • Planning Department: Five-year plans, annual plans; Annual Budget coordination.

Cabinet and Secretariat Flow

  • Policy-making: Cabinet meeting (CM + Ministers) → decision → cabinet note prepared by department secretary → published as Government Resolution (GR/Shasan Nirnaya) → implementation by field offices.
  • Government Resolution (GR): The primary official document through which state government communicates policy; every MPSC-aspirant will work with GRs in their career.
  • Circular: Administrative communication from secretariat to field offices on procedure.

MPSC-Recruited Posts in Mantralaya

  • Section Officer (Mantralaya Kadha): MPSC Group B examination; works in Mantralaya sections.
  • Clerk-Typist (Mantralaya Kaksha): Group C; clerical staff in Mantralaya.
  • The Mantralaya Service is a distinct cadre from the District/Field cadre — officers recruited for secretariat work specifically.

Maharashtra Connection

Every MPSC Rajyaseva officer will eventually work in or interact with the Mantralaya system. The GR (Government Resolution) is the primary document of governance — citing a specific GR number is standard administrative practice in Maharashtra. The Chief Secretary (typically 30+ years IAS) is the most powerful civil servant in the state. Controversially, some CMs have bypassed the Mantralaya using the CM's Private Office (CMO).

Memory Aids

  • Mantralaya = Nariman Point = "Nariman-Mantralaya" (N-M: same initials as "Narayan's Ministry")
  • CS → ACS → PS → Secretary → DS → US (hierarchy top to bottom)
  • GR = Shasan Nirnaya = Maharashtra's policy Bible

Exam Traps

  • Mantralaya (secretariat) ≠ Vidhan Bhavan (legislature) — two different buildings in South Mumbai.
  • Chief Secretary ≠ Principal Secretary — CS is one post (head of all); PS heads a particular department.
  • Maharashtra has ~48 departments — NOT 30 or 60; approximate number is often tested.
