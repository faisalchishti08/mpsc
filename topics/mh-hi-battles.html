Key battles — Pratapgad, Sinhagad, Panipat III PREMAINSINTMH

Overview

Maharashtra's history is studded with decisive battles that shaped the Maratha Empire and India's political map. The key battles — Pratapgad (1659), Sinhagad (1670), Salher (1672), Treaty of Purandar (1665), Third Battle of Panipat (1761) — are perennial MPSC targets for dates, participants, outcomes, and significance.

Battle of Pratapgad (1659) — In Detail

  • Context: Shivaji had been expanding in the Bijapur Sultanate's territory; Bijapur sent its best general Afzal Khan with 10,000 troops to subdue Shivaji.
  • Afzal Khan's treachery: Khan planned to kill Shivaji during a supposed peace meeting at the foot of Pratapgad Fort.
  • Shivaji's counter: Shivaji wore armour beneath his clothes and brought bagh-nakh (iron tiger claws); when Khan moved to embrace and stab him, Shivaji killed Khan with the bagh-nakh.
  • Aftermath: Shivaji's forces routed the leaderless Bijapur army; captured Khan's treasury and artillery; dramatically raised Maratha prestige.
  • Afzal Khan's tomb is near the base of Pratapgad — still visible.

Battle of Salher (1672) — Significance

  • First major open-field cavalry battle victory of the Marathas against Mughal forces.
  • Scale: Mughal force of ~30,000 cavalry + infantry; Maratha force under Prataprao Gujar and Anandrao Makaji.
  • Result: Maratha victory; ~6,000 Mughals killed, ~10,000 captured; large booty including 125 war elephants; released many prisoners.
  • A crucial confidence-builder before Shivaji's coronation in 1674.

Third Battle of Panipat (14 January 1761)

Already covered under mh-hi-peshwas; key fact: Sadashivrao Bhau (Maratha commander-in-chief) underestimated Abdali's coalition; extended supply lines; Maratha army besieged at Panipat; both Vishwas Rao and Bhau killed. Called "the great grief" of Maharashtra.

Battle of Bhima Koregaon (1 January 1818)

  • ~500 Mahar soldiers of the 2nd Battalion, 1st Regiment of the Bombay Infantry (East India Company) + British officers held off Peshwa Baji Rao II's force (~25,000–28,000).
  • The Vijay Stambha (Victory Pillar) was erected by the British; later adopted by Dr. Ambedkar as a symbol of Mahar pride and Dalit assertion — Ambedkar visited on 1 January 1927 (centenary); his followers continue to gather on 1 January each year.
  • 2018 violence: Dalit groups gathering at Bhima Koregaon were attacked by groups identified as upper-caste — led to statewide Dalit bandh; became a major political controversy.

Exam Traps

  • Sinhagad battle (Tanaji, 1670) ≠ Pratapgad battle (Afzal Khan, 1659) — two different battles.
  • Treaty of Purandar (1665) = Shivaji surrendered 23 forts — NOT a Maratha victory.
  • Third Battle of Panipat (1761) ≠ First Battle of Panipat (1526) — always specify which Panipat.
