Mohiniyattam (Kerala) PREMAINS

Overview

Mohiniyattam (literally "dance of the enchantress/Mohini") is the classical female solo dance form of Kerala, rooted in the lasya (gentle, graceful, feminine) aesthetic. It is performed in the white-and-gold kasavu saree and features swaying, wave-like body movements drawing from the Kerala attaprakaram (expressive) tradition. MPSC tests its contrast with Kathakali (also Kerala), costume, and Swati Tirunal connection.

In-Depth

Origin and History

  • Origin is debated; the name Mohini refers to the enchantress avatar of Vishnu.
  • References in early 18th century texts; possibly performed at Kerala temples.
  • Swati Tirunal (Maharaja of Travancore, 1813–1846): great patron; composed major pieces (padam, varnam) for Mohiniyattam in Malayalam; his compositions are the core of the modern repertoire.
  • The form declined in the 19th–early 20th century along with devadasi stigma.
  • Revival: Vallathol Narayana Menon (who also founded Kerala Kalamandalam for Kathakali) brought attention to Mohiniyattam; Kalamandalam Kalyanikutty Amma is the most celebrated early modern practitioner.

Technical Features

FeatureDescription
Aesthetic styleLasya — gentle, graceful, swaying, like the movement of coconut palms or flowing water
Body movementSwaying circular movements (horizontal figure-8 of the torso), soft curved lines; no angular postures
CostumeOff-white/cream saree with gold border (*kasavu*), traditional Kerala style; jasmine flowers in hair; gold jewellery
ThemeDevotional to Vishnu/Krishna; also Shiva mythology; performed by women only
MusicSopana Sangeet (Kerala's devotional music tradition); *mridangam*, *veena*, *edakka* (hourglass drum)
LanguageMalayalam (primary); Manipravalam (mixed Sanskrit-Malayalam)

Contrast with Kathakali (both Kerala):

  • Kathakali: elaborate makeup, male performers, all-night drama, vigorous, no speech.
  • Mohiniyattam: minimal makeup, female solo, gentle swaying, intimate concert.

Structure

Similar to Bharatanatyam's Margam: Cholkettu → Vandanam → Jatisvaram → Varnam → Padams → Tillana → Shlokam.

Key Exponents

  • Kalamandalam Kalyanikutty Amma (1922–2006): the founding mother of modern Mohiniyattam; Sangeet Natak Akademi award; trained generations.
  • Shanta Rao: prominent mid-20th century exponent.
  • Kalamandalam Leelamma: another major practitioner.
  • Sunanda Nair: contemporary.

Maharashtra Connection

No origin connection. Mumbai and Pune have Mohiniyattam schools. The white-gold kasavu saree aesthetic is immediately recognisable in Maharashtra's Kerala cultural festivals and performances.

Key Facts

  • State: Kerala.
  • Mohiniyattam = "dance of the enchantress (Mohini)".
  • Aesthetic: Lasya — soft, swaying, feminine.
  • Costume: White kasavu saree (Kerala gold-border weave).
  • Swati Tirunal (Maharaja of Travancore): key patron; composed core pieces.
  • Revival: Vallathol Narayana Menon + Kalamandalam Kalyanikutty Amma.
  • Music: Sopana Sangeet (Kerala); Malayalam songs.
  • Performed by women only.

Exam Traps

  • Mohiniyattam ≠ Kathakali — both Kerala; key distinguishing features as above.
  • Kasavu saree is the white-gold Kerala weave — specific to Mohiniyattam; don't confuse with Bharatanatyam's Kanjivaram.
  • Performed by women only — unlike Kathakali which is traditionally male.
