Municipal corporations and councils PREMAINSINTMH

Overview

Urban local bodies (ULBs) in Maharashtra manage civic services in cities and towns. Maharashtra has a layered urban structure: Municipal Corporations (Mahanagar Palikas) for large cities, Municipal Councils (Nagar Palikas) for medium towns, and Nagar Panchayats for transitional areas. With ~45% urbanisation, Maharashtra's ULBs are among India's largest and most financially significant. MPSC tests the number of corporations, their governing acts, BMC's special status, and the 74th Amendment.

Municipal Corporations in Maharashtra

  • Maharashtra has 27 Municipal Corporations (as of 2024).
  • Major corporations:
  • Brihanmumbai Municipal Corporation (BMC) — largest municipal corporation in India by budget (~₹37,000–45,000 crore annual budget); governed by Mumbai Municipal Corporation Act, 1888 (British-era statute, still in force); Commissioner is a senior IAS officer.
  • Pune Municipal Corporation (PMC) — second largest in Maharashtra by budget.
  • Nagpur Municipal Corporation (NMC) — winter capital city's civic body; Nagpur Improvement Trust (NIT) handles development.
  • Pimpri-Chinchwad Municipal Corporation (PCMC) — Pune's twin industrial city (Tata Motors, Bajaj Auto belt).
  • Thane Municipal Corporation, Nashik Municipal Corporation, Kalyan-Dombivali Municipal Corporation, Navi Mumbai Municipal Corporation (NMMC).
  • Newer corporations: Mira-Bhayandar, Solapur, Kolhapur, Aurangabad (Chhatrapati Sambhajinagar), Amravati, etc.

BMC — Special Notes

  • BMC annual budget exceeds budgets of many Indian states.
  • BMC Mayor (Mahapour) is elected by corporators; the Municipal Commissioner (IAS) holds executive power.
  • The Municipal Commissioner is appointed by the state government and is the real executive — a frequent MPSC point of distinction from the ceremonial Mayor.
  • BMC runs the largest public hospital network in Mumbai: KEM, Sion, Nair, JJ (state hospital), Cooper.
  • BMC also manages Mumbai's drainage, roads, markets, schools, and the iconic BEST buses (Brihanmumbai Electric Supply and Transport — now separate from BMC).

74th Constitutional Amendment (1992)

The 74th Amendment mandated:

  • Elected urban local bodies with direct elections to wards.
  • 33% reservation for women (Maharashtra provides 40–50% in some corporations).
  • Ward committees in cities with population >3 lakh.
  • State Election Commission to conduct ULB elections.
  • State Finance Commission to recommend funds.
  • Twelfth Schedule: 18 functions listed for municipalities (urban planning, roads, parks, markets, public health, etc.).

Maharashtra amended its municipal acts to comply and added area sabhas (ward-level assemblies) in some corporations.

Maharashtra Connection

Mumbai's BMC is the financial backbone of Maharashtra's urban governance. MMRDA (Mumbai Metropolitan Region Development Authority) handles metro rail, bridges, and regional planning beyond BMC's limits. The Navi Mumbai Municipal Corporation (NMMC) is notable as one of India's cleanest civic bodies (Swachh Survekshan rankings). Pune's Smart City Mission (2015–) and Nagpur's metro rail (MahaMetro) are flagship urban infra projects.

Key Facts

  • Municipal Corporations in Maharashtra: 27
  • BMC governing act: Mumbai Municipal Corporation Act, 1888
  • All other corporations: Maharashtra Municipal Corporations Act, 1949
  • Municipal Councils/Nagar Panchayats: Maharashtra Municipal Councils, Nagar Panchayats and Industrial Townships Act, 1965
  • BMC budget: ~₹37,000–45,000 crore — largest among Indian ULBs
  • Mayor: ceremonial head; Municipal Commissioner (IAS): executive head
  • 74th Amendment: 1992 (Twelfth Schedule — 18 urban functions)
  • MMRDA: Mumbai Metropolitan Region Development Authority (planning body, not ULB)

Exam Traps

  • BMC is NOT governed by the 1949 Act — it has its own 1888 Act (pre-Independence, still valid).
  • The Municipal Commissioner (IAS) holds executive power, NOT the Mayor — a classic MPSC trap.
  • MMRDA is NOT a municipal corporation — it is a planning/development authority.
