Overview

Maharashtra — particularly Mumbai — has produced some of India's greatest sportspersons across cricket, hockey, wrestling, kabaddi, badminton, and athletics. Sachin Tendulkar (cricket, Bharat Ratna 2014), Sunil Gavaskar (cricket), Milkha Singh (athletics — not MH born but Pune connection), and Leander Paes (tennis — not MH). Key Maharashtra-specific sportspersons: Sania Mirza (Hyderabad, not MH), but cricket is dominated by Mumbai. Maharashtra has 36 districts with distinct wrestling (kushti) and kabaddi traditions in rural areas. MPSC tests names, sports, key achievements, and awards.

Ranji Trophy — Mumbai's Record

  • Mumbai (erstwhile Bombay) is the most successful Ranji Trophy team — 41 titles (most by any team).
  • Ranji Trophy dominated by Mumbai from 1930s to 2000s; recent years more competitive.
  • Maharashtra has its own Ranji team (different from Mumbai); plays from Pune (MCA stadium).

Wrestling (Kushti) Tradition

  • Kolhapur is the wrestling capital of Maharashtra — its akhada (gymnasium) tradition and mud-pit wrestling (mati kushti) is centuries old.
  • Maruti Mane, Dadu Chaugule, Ganpat Andhalkar: Legendary Kolhapur wrestlers.
  • Khashaba Dadasaheb Jadhav: India's FIRST individual Olympic medal winner (Bronze, 1952 Helsinki Olympics); wrestling (freestyle 57 kg); from Goleshwar, Satara district; Padma Shri 1993 (posthumous); National Sports Day observation (29 August) was established around his era.
  • Maharashtra organises annual Mahar Kabaddi Tournaments and state-level kushti competitions.

Maharashtra's Hockey and Football Legacy

  • Hockey: Maharashtra not a dominant state post-independence (Punjab, Odisha, Jharkhand dominate), but historically Mumbai had a strong hockey tradition in corporate/inter-company leagues.
  • Football: Mumbai has FC Goa (not MH based) and Mumbai City FC (ISL franchise); Pune FC; historically ONGC, Air India football teams based in Mumbai.

Kabaddi — Maharashtra's Strength

  • Maharashtra is one of India's kabaddi powerhouses — strong tradition in villages.
  • Pardeep Narwal and Ajay Thakur are national stars with MH training connections.
  • Maharashtra Pro Kabaddi Puneri Paltan: Pune-based ISL kabaddi franchise; multiple finals appearances.
  • Maharashtra school kabaddi competitions are among India's most competitive.

Maharashtra Connection

Mumbai is India's cricketing capital — more Test cricketers have come from Mumbai than any other city. Wankhede Stadium has hosted iconic matches including the 2011 World Cup Final (India won). Khashaba Jadhav's 1952 Olympic bronze — India's first individual medal — is a Maharashtra pride point that MPSC tests every few years. Lalita Babar from Satara (steeplechase) and Suyash Jadhav (para-swimming) represent Maharashtra's rural athletic talent.
