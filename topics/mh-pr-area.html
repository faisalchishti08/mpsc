Overview

Maharashtra is India's third-largest state by area (307,713 sq km) and second most populous state (population 11.24 crore, 2011 Census). These rankings — area, population, density, sex ratio, literacy — are bread-and-butter MPSC Prelims data points that appear almost every exam cycle.

Key Statistical Details

  • Area share: Maharashtra covers ~9.36% of India's total land area (3,287,263 sq km).
  • MP vs MH: Madhya Pradesh (3,08,252 sq km) is marginally larger than Maharashtra (3,07,713 sq km) — a common exam trick. Rajasthan > MP > Maharashtra.
  • Population share: Maharashtra accounts for ~9.28% of India's population (2011).
  • Districts: 36 (as of 2014 after Palghar was carved from Thane).
  • Urban agglomeration: Mumbai Metropolitan Region (MMR) — population ~18.4 million (2011 urban agglomeration), making it India's largest.
  • Decadal growth rate (2001–2011): 15.99%, lower than the national rate of 17.64%.

2011 Census Highlights

  • Scheduled Castes: 11.8% of population
  • Scheduled Tribes: 9.4% (mainly Gondi, Bhil, Warli, Mahadeo Koli in Vidarbha, Nashik, Thane)
  • Child Sex Ratio (0-6 years): 883 girls per 1000 boys — significantly low; targeted by Beti Bachao Beti Padhao scheme
  • Workers (main + marginal): 48.9% of population

Maharashtra Connection

Maharashtra's high urbanisation (45.2%) traces directly to Mumbai's role as the financial capital and Pune's emergence as an IT/education hub. The Child Sex Ratio of 883 is a sensitive social-policy concern — districts like Beed, Kolhapur, Sangli have sex ratios well below 900 at birth, prompting state schemes like Majhi Kanya Bhagyashree (scholarship for girl children).

  • Maharashtra literacy 82.9% > national 73% → "MH is literate"

Exam Traps

  • MP beats MH in area by only ~539 sq km — do NOT say Maharashtra is 2nd largest.
  • Sex ratio 929 is below national average 940 — don't confuse direction.
  • Child sex ratio 883 ≠ overall sex ratio 929 — two different metrics; MPSC exploits this confusion.
