Temple styles — Nagara, Dravida, Vesara with examples PREMAINS

Overview

Temple architecture in India developed into three major styles — Nagara (North Indian), Dravida (South Indian), and Vesara (mixed/Deccan). Each style has a distinct superstructure (shikhara vs gopuram) and evolved under different royal patronage. MPSC tests temple style identification, key examples (Brihadeeswara, Kailasa, Khajuraho, Konark), and Maharashtra-specific Hemadpanthi temples. This topic partially overlaps with hist-art-temple — this file focuses on classification logic and regional coverage.

Dravida Sub-elements

  • Gopuram: massive entrance gateways; later Vijayanagara temples had very tall gopurams; Meenakshi Temple (Madurai) — the most famous.
  • Prakara: concentric enclosure walls.
  • Mandapa: pillared halls; 1000-pillared mandapas (Madurai, Srirangam).
  • Pushkarini: sacred tank.

Hemadpanthi Temples (Maharashtra)

  • Hemadpant (Hemadri): minister of Yadava kings of Devagiri (Daulatabad); 13th c.
  • Introduced black basalt dry-masonry style — without mortar (interlocking stones).
  • Examples: Gondeshwar Temple (Sinnar, Nashik), Ambernath Shiva Temple (Thane district; 11th c., pre-Hemadpant but similar style), Siddheshwar Temple (Solapur).
  • "Hemadpanthi" = local term for this dry-stone, interlocking-block basalt temple construction style.

Maharashtra Connection

Maharashtra is uniquely placed — spanning Nagara, Dravida, and Vesara influences:

  • Kailasa Temple, Ellora (Rashtrakuta): largest monolithic rock-cut temple worldwide; Dravida style; Aurangabad.
  • Hemadpanthi temples (Yadava period): distinctive Maharashtra temple style; black basalt; Gondeshwar (Sinnar), Ambarnath.
  • Pandharpur Vitthal Temple (Solapur): focal point of Warkari bhakti; Hemadpanthi style; thousands visit on Ekadashi.
  • Trimbakeshwar (Nashik), Bhimashankar (Pune), Aundha Nagnath (Hingoli): three of the 12 Jyotirlingas in Maharashtra; ancient temple architecture.
  • Kopeshwar Temple, Khidrapur (Kolhapur/Karnataka border): Hemadpanthi + Chalukya; stunning carved temple.

Key Facts

  • Nagara = curvilinear shikhara; Dravida = pyramidal vimana + gopuram.
  • Brihadeeswara, Thanjavur: Dravida; Raja Raja Chola I; 1010 CE; 66m.
  • Kailasa, Ellora: monolithic rock-cut; Rashtrakuta Krishna I; ~757–775 CE; UNESCO 1983.
  • Khajuraho: Nagara; Chandela; erotic sculptures; UNESCO 1986.
  • Konark: Nagara (Kalingan); Narasimhadeva I; 13th c.; UNESCO 1984.
  • Hoysala temples: Vesara; star-plan; soapstone; Karnataka.
  • Hemadpanthi: Maharashtra; black basalt dry-masonry; Yadava period.

Exam Traps

  • Vimana in South Indian architecture = the main tower over the sanctum — NOT the same as Vimana (aeroplane). In Dravida style it is SHORTER than the gopuram (gateway).
  • Khajuraho = Chandela (not Solanki; not Rashtrakuta).
  • Kailasa Temple = Rashtrakuta dynasty (Krishna I) — not the Chalukyas (who are also in Ellora region).
  • Hemadpanthi ≠ any specific dynasty — it is an architectural style of the Yadava period; term coined later.
