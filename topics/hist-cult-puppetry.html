Puppetry, handicrafts and folk theatre PREMAINS

Overview

Puppetry and handicrafts are traditional art forms of India, classified by the government as intangible and tangible cultural heritage. India has four major types of puppetry — String, Shadow, Rod, and Glove. Handicrafts vary state by state and are often linked to GI (Geographical Indication) tags. MPSC tests Maharashtra's folk theatre (Tamasha, Kirtan, Dashavatari), puppetry traditions, and specific state-craft linkages that appear in general cultural awareness questions.

Maharashtra Connection

Maharashtra is particularly rich in:

  • Tamasha + Lavani: signature folk theatre + music of Maharashtra (covered in hist-art-theatre + hist-music-folk).
  • Chamadyacha Bahulya: leather shadow puppet tradition.
  • Dashavatari: Konkan coastal folk theatre.
  • Paithani Saree: globally recognised handwoven silk from Paithan (ancient Pratishthana/Paithan, Aurangabad) — GI-tagged; one of the finest silk sarees.
  • Kolhapuri Chappal: internationally known footwear craft.
  • Warli art: tribal painting (Palghar/Nashik) — now on canvas, fabric, products globally.

Exam Traps

  • Blue Pottery (Jaipur) is NOT clay-based — it is quartz/glass-based; unusual for a pottery form.
  • Chamadyacha Bahulya = Maharashtra shadow puppets; not to be confused with Rajasthan's Kathputli (string, not shadow).
  • Dashavatari = Konkan Maharashtra (coastal); different from Kerala's Krishnanattam (also 10 forms of Vishnu but different tradition).
  • Bidriware is from Bidar, Karnataka — NOT Maharashtra despite its Deccan location.
