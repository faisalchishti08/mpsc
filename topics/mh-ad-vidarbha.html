Vidarbha, Marathwada, Khandesh, Konkan, Desh regions PREMAINSINTMH

Overview

Maharashtra is informally divided into five major geographical-cultural regions — Vidarbha, Marathwada, Khandesh, Konkan, and Desh (Western Maharashtra/Paschim Maharashtra). These regions predate the current administrative divisions and carry historical, cultural, and political significance. Regional imbalance among them is a core Mains policy topic.

Region-Wise Profiles

Vidarbha

  • Historical: Part of Central Provinces and Berar (under British). Merged into Bombay State (1950), then Maharashtra (1960).
  • Economy: Cotton, orange (Nagpur), paddy, soybean. Wardha has historical significance — Mahatma Gandhi's Sevagram Ashram is here.
  • Politics: Vidarbha Rajya Andolan — persistent demand for a separate Vidarbha state. The Vidarbha Rajya Movement argues for equal share of Marathwada's cotton and dam water.
  • Development: Chronic farmer suicides (highest in India for prolonged periods); PM Modi's flagship farm relief packages repeatedly targeting Vidarbha.

Marathwada

  • Historical: Under Nizam of Hyderabad until Police Action (Operation Polo), 17 September 1948. Integration into India celebrated as Marathwada Muktisangram Din (17 September).
  • Economy: Drought-prone; sugar cooperatives; Latur known for soybean processing.
  • Infrastructure: The Jayakwadi Dam (Aurangabad on Godavari) is Marathwada's lifeline irrigation project.
  • Social: Marathwada University (now Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar Marathwada University, BAMU, Aurangabad) — renaming was a site of upper-caste violence in 1978.

Khandesh

  • Tapi river valley; tri-district core (Jalgaon, Dhule, Nandurbar).
  • Nandurbar: Highest tribal concentration (Bhil, Pavri, Koli tribes). Separated from Dhule in 1998.
  • Jalgaon's banana production earns it the tag "Banana City of India"; Maharashtra produces ~30% of India's bananas.

Konkan

  • Narrow coastal strip between the Sahyadri (Western Ghats) and the Arabian Sea.
  • Ratnagiri and Sindhudurg produce the GI-tagged Alphonso (Hapus) mango and Cashews.
  • Konkan Railway (Roha to Mangalore, 1998) transformed connectivity.

Desh / Paschim Maharashtra

  • The Deccan plateau heartland; politically dominant (sugarcane cooperatives, water, education).
  • Pune is its economic and educational centre; Kolhapur is a major centre for jaggery (gul) and leather.
  • Solapur: Textile (chaddars/bed sheets); also historically important — Gadkari's seat, etc.

Maharashtra Connection

The regional imbalance between Desh (developed), Vidarbha (backward, cotton), and Marathwada (drought, historically under the Nizam) is Maharashtra's most persistent governance issue. It underlies MPSC Mains questions on development planning, demand for separate states, and water disputes. The Marathwada Water Commission and Vidarbha Irrigation Development Corporation (VIDC) exist specifically to address sub-regional needs.

Key Facts

  • Vidarbha: 11 districts, 2 revenue divisions, formerly Central Provinces
  • Marathwada: 8 districts, 1 division, formerly Nizam's Hyderabad
  • Marathwada liberation: 17 September 1948 (Operation Polo)
  • Khandesh: 3 districts (Jalgaon, Dhule, Nandurbar); Nandurbar carved from Dhule 1998
  • Konkan Railway: 1998, Roha (Raigad) to Mangalore
  • Jayakwadi Dam: Aurangabad district, Godavari river — Marathwada's key project
  • Nagpur (Vidarbha): Orange capital + Winter capital + Zero Mile city

Exam Traps

  • Marathwada joined Maharashtra via the 1960 formation, but was liberated from Nizam in 1948 (via Police Action) — two separate events.
  • Vidarbha is NOT the same as the Nagpur Division — Vidarbha spans two divisions.
  • Nandurbar was carved from Dhule, not from Jalgaon (1998).
