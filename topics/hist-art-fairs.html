Overview

India's fairs (melas), festivals and folk culture are expressions of religious devotion, seasonal cycles, community identity, and regional culture. They are tested in MPSC for their state-associations, religious significance, UNESCO recognition, and Maharashtra-specific festivals (Ganesh Chaturthi, Gudhi Padwa, Wari). Maharashtra's Major Festivals and Fairs

Ganesh Chaturthi (Ganeshotsav):

  • Celebrated on Bhadrapada Shukla Chaturthi (August-September); 10 days.
  • Lokmanya Bal Gangadhar Tilak transformed it from a home festival into a mass public celebration in 1893 (Pune) — used as a platform for anti-British unity.
  • Mumbai's Lalbaugcha Raja and Pune's Kasba Ganapati (oldest, Manacha Pahila) are the most famous.
  • Maharashtra has 5 Manacha (honoured) Ganapatis in Pune: Kasba, Tambdi Jogeshwari, Guruji Talim, Tambat Ali, Kesariwada.

Wari (Pandharpur Pilgrimage):

  • Twice-yearly pilgrimage to Pandharpur (Solapur district); deity Vithoba/Vitthal.
  • Ashadhi Ekadashi (June-July) and Kartiki Ekadashi (October-November).
  • Millions of Warkaris (pilgrims) walk from various starting points; the largest starts from Alandi (Dnyaneshwar's samadhi) and Dehu (Tukaram's samadhi) near Pune.
  • One of the largest pilgrimage traditions in Maharashtra; community cohesion across castes.

Gudhi Padwa:

  • Marathi New Year; Chaitra Shukla Pratipada (March-April); the gudhi (bamboo pole with cloth, neem, sugar, copper pot) is raised outside homes.

Narali Purnima (Coconut Day):

  • Full moon of Shravan month (August); fishermen offer coconuts to the sea (Varuna) seeking permission to resume fishing after the monsoon.

Shimga (Holi in Maharashtra):

  • Marathi Holi; celebrated for 5 days with bonfires, colour play, and Lavani performances.

Makar Sankranti in Maharashtra:

  • 14 January; exchange of tilgul (sesame-jaggery) with the greeting "Tilgul ghya, god god bola" (take sesame-jaggery, speak sweet words).

Maharashtra Connection

Maharashtra-specific festivals are directly tested:

  • Ganesh Chaturthi: public festival started by Tilak (1893) — pivotal in national movement.
  • Pandharpur Wari (Vithoba pilgrimage) — Ashadhi and Kartiki Ekadashi.
  • Nashik Kumbh (Simhastha): held at Nashik (River Godavari) every 12 years — Nashik and Trimbakeshwar are the pilgrimage sites.
  • Gudhi Padwa: Marathi New Year — a defining Maharashtra cultural marker.
  • Tilgul (Makar Sankranti) — unique Maharashtra greeting tradition.

Exam Traps

  • Nashik Kumbh is on the Godavari River, NOT the Ganga — common trap.
  • Simhastha is the term used for Nashik/Ujjain Kumbh specifically (not Prayagraj or Haridwar).
  • Ganesh Chaturthi was made public by Tilak in 1893 — not Agarkar or Gokhale.
  • Bihu has THREE types (Bohag, Kati, Magh) — not just one.
