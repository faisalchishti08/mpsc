Employment and housing schemes PREMAINSINTMH

Overview

Maharashtra's employment and housing schemes span central flagship programmes (MGNREGS, PM Awas Yojana) and state-specific initiatives. Key employment schemes include Mahatma Gandhi NREGS (MGNREGS), Rozgar Hami Yojana (RHY — Maharashtra's predecessor, 1965), and Pradhan Mantri Rozgar Protsahan Yojana (PMRPY). For housing, Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana (PMAY-Urban and Gramin) and Maharashtra's Gharkul schemes for SC/ST/nomadic tribes are central. The Mukhyamantri Rozgar Nirman Karyakram (MRNK) and the Maharashtra government's self-employment schemes for educated youth round out the portfolio. MPSC tests scheme amounts, eligibility, and implementing agencies.

Maharashtra's Rozgar Hami Yojana — Pioneer Status

The Maharashtra Employment Guarantee Act, 1977 (providing the legal basis for RHY) was the world's first legal guarantee of employment — predating India's own MGNREGS (2005) and the International Labour Organization's recognition of the right to work. This is a standard MPSC pride fact.

Maharashtra Connection

Maharashtra's Rozgar Hami Yojana (1965, Act 1977) was the direct model and inspiration for MGNREGS (2005) — P.V. Sukhtankar's scheme in Maharashtra was studied by economists including Amartya Sen before national scaling. Mumbai's SRA mechanism for Dharavi redevelopment is India's largest slum redevelopment project — a case study in urban housing policy. MHADA's lottery draws attract hundreds of thousands of applicants, reflecting the extreme housing shortage in Mumbai Metropolitan Region.

Exam Traps

  • RHY (1965) ≠ Employment Guarantee Act 1977 — the scheme started 1965, the law was passed 1977.
  • MGNREGS wages are state-wise notified — Maharashtra's ₹273/day is higher than many states but lower than some.
  • SRA is Mumbai-specific; MHADA operates across Maharashtra.
