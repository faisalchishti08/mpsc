Welfare schemes for SC/ST/OBC and farmers PREMAINSINTMH

Overview

Maharashtra has a comprehensive welfare architecture for SC, ST, OBC, VJNT (Vimukta Jati and Nomadic Tribes), farmers, and senior citizens. Key institutions include Maharashtra SC Development Corporation (MSCDC), Maharashtra Tribal Development Department, OBC Mahamandal, and VJNT Mahamandal. Constitutional provisions (Articles 15, 16, 17, 46, 342, 243D, 243T), the Maharashtra Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities) Act, and Maharashtra-specific reservations are frequently tested. MPSC tests the names of departments, corporations, reservation percentages, and key welfare schemes.

Maratha Reservation — Major Controversy

  • Marathas are ~28–33% of Maharashtra's population; dominant in political and cooperative sectors.
  • Demand for reservation as SEBC (Socially and Educationally Backward Class) or OBC.
  • Maharashtra State Backward Class Commission (Gaikwad Commission) — 2018 report: Marathas are "socially and educationally backward"; recommended 16% reservation.
  • SEBC Act 2018: Maharashtra gave 16% Maratha reservation → Supreme Court stayed (exceeds 50% ceiling).
  • Supreme Court judgment (May 2021): Struck down SEBC (Maratha) reservation as unconstitutional; exceeded 50% cap; found no extraordinary circumstances to justify excess.
  • Manoj Jarange-Patil agitation (2023-24): Renewed demand for Kunbi caste certificate for Marathas (to enter OBC quota as Kunbi); politically sensitive as it affects other OBC communities.

SC/ST Prevention of Atrocities Act in Maharashtra

  • Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities) Act, 1989 (central, amended 2015 to make it stronger): Deals with crimes against SC/ST.
  • Maharashtra Atrocities Rules: Maharashtra implemented specific rules for FIR registration, support to victims.
  • Special Courts: Designated Special Courts in each district for fast-track trials of atrocity cases.
  • Bhima Koregaon (January 1, 2018): Violence during 200th anniversary of Battle of Bhima Koregaon (1818) — major SC community gathering; clashes reported; led to nationwide debate on caste violence and "Elgar Parishad" case.

Nomadic Tribes — Maharashtra-Specific

  • Maharashtra has 28 Nomadic Tribe communities and 42 Denotified (Vimukta) communities — highest in India.
  • Denotified tribes were listed under the Criminal Tribes Act, 1871 (British); repealed 1952; "denotified" but still face stigma.
  • Renke Commission (2008): Estimated 10.7 crore NT/DNT population nationally; recommended reservations and welfare measures; Maharashtra has significant NT/DNT communities.
  • Bhatkya Vimukt Jatichi Vikash Mandal (Maharashtra VJNT Development Corp.): Under Social Justice Dept.

Senior Citizen Schemes

  • Shravanbal Seva Rajya Nivritti Vetan Yojana: State pension for senior citizens (60+) below poverty line; ₹1,200–1,500/month (varying); linked to PM's Indira Gandhi National Old Age Pension Scheme.
  • Maintenance and Welfare of Parents and Senior Citizens Act, 2007 (central): Maharashtra has Maintenance Tribunals in each district.

Maharashtra Connection

Maharashtra's reservation debates — especially the Maratha reservation case (Supreme Court, May 2021) — are landmark constitutional moments. The state's ST/SC/VJNT welfare infrastructure is among the most institutionalized in India (separate departments + corporations for each). The Phule-Shahu-Ambedkar trinity's legacy directly shapes these policies: Shahu's 1902 OBC reservation in Kolhapur, Ambedkar's drafting of Art. 15/16/17, and Phule's critique of caste all feed into Maharashtra's welfare state.
