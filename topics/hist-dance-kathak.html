Kathak (North India) PREMAINS

Overview

Kathak is the principal classical dance form of north India, uniquely influenced by both Hindu devotional traditions (Krishna stories) and the Mughal court's Persian aesthetic — producing a dance of fast footwork, elaborate spins, and expressive storytelling. It is the only classical Indian dance to show clear Islamic influence in costume and movement. MPSC tests gharana distinction, key exponents, and Mughal-influence angle.

In-Depth

Origin and Development

  • Kathak derives from katha (story) + kaar (one who tells) — the Kathakas were hereditary storytellers in temples who enacted stories of Krishna and Radha with gesture and music.
  • Early phase (medieval): devotional Hindu temple performance; stories of Krishna (Rasleela) in Mathura-Vrindavan region.
  • Mughal phase (16th–18th c.): moved from temple to court; adapted to the Mughal aesthetic — ghaghra (skirt) costume, Persian-influenced thumri songs, graceful spins (chakkar), footwork precision. The court context gave it stylistic elegance but also its secular, romantic character.
  • Post-Mughal (Lucknow Nawabs, 18th–19th c.): refined further; the Lucknow Nawabs were great patrons; the Lucknow gharana was shaped by this patronage.

Wajid Ali Shah (last Nawab of Awadh, 1822–1887): Greatest royal patron of Kathak; himself a dancer, musician and poet; composed the thumri form; his court in Lucknow elevated Kathak to its most refined expression. Exiled by British in 1856.

Technical Features

  • Tatkar: rhythmic footwork — beats on the floor using ghungroo (ankle bells, 100–200 bells); the most distinctive Kathak feature.
  • Chakkar: fast spins (pirouettes); can be executed in rapid succession (the "spinning" image of Kathak).
  • Tukda, Toda, Paran: rhythmic compositions increasing in complexity.
  • Abhinaya: expressional component using facial gestures for Krishna-Radha stories (bhava).
  • Accompaniment: Tabla (primary percussion), Sarangi, Harmonium; in Hindustani music tradition.

Birju Maharaj (1938–2022)

The most celebrated Kathak exponent of the 20th–21st century:

  • Lucknow gharana (Kalka-Bindadin lineage); nephew of Lachhu Maharaj and Shambhu Maharaj (also Kathak legends).
  • Padma Vibhushan (1986); National Film Award; Kalidas Samman.
  • Choreographed Kathak sequences in films including Umrao Jaan and Dedh Ishqiya.
  • Passed away January 2022.

Maharashtra Connection

No direct origin. Mumbai (particularly Bandra, Juhu areas) has active Kathak schools — the Lucknow gharana is taught widely. Lata Mangeshkar (Maharashtra) was a great admirer and had deep connections with Hindustani thumri tradition that underpins Kathak's expressive repertoire.

Key Facts

  • State: North India (UP primarily; Mathura-Vrindavan origin).
  • Name from katha = story; Kathakas = temple storytellers.
  • Two gharanas: Lucknow (grace, thumri) and Jaipur (vigorous footwork).
  • Wajid Ali Shah: greatest royal Kathak patron; last Nawab of Awadh.
  • Distinctive features: tatkar (footwork) and chakkar (spins).
  • Bells (ghungroo) number: 100–200 on each ankle.
  • Birju Maharaj: greatest modern exponent; Lucknow gharana; Padma Vibhushan.
  • Only classical dance with significant Mughal/Islamic stylistic influence.

Exam Traps

  • Kathak's Islamic/Mughal influence distinguishes it from other classical forms — don't describe it as purely Hindu temple tradition.
  • Birju Maharaj = Lucknow gharana, NOT Jaipur gharana.
  • Lachhu Maharaj was Birju Maharaj's uncle and a separate Lucknow master — not the same person.
  • Kathak accompaniment = Tabla (Hindustani); Bharatanatyam uses Mridangam (Carnatic) — common confusion.
