Religious fairs and pilgrimage traditions PREMAINS

Overview

Religious fairs and pilgrimage traditions in India are major socio-cultural institutions combining faith, trade, and community gathering. MPSC tests the major fairs (Kumbh Mela, Pushkar Fair, Hemis), Maharashtra's pilgrimage traditions (Pandharpur Wari, Jejuri, Shirdi, Nanded Gurudwara), and the UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage inscriptions. The Kumbh Mela and Wari pilgrimage are the most tested items for Maharashtra papers.

In-Depth

Kumbh Mela

  • Largest religious gathering on Earth: millions gather every 3–6–12 years at four rotating locations.
  • Legend: Amrit Kumbh (nectar pot) spilled at four places during the cosmic churning (Samudra Manthan).

Char Dham (Hindu Four Pilgrimages)

  • Badrinath (Uttarakhand), Dwarka (Gujarat), Puri (Odisha), Rameshwaram (Tamil Nadu).
  • Adi Shankara is credited with establishing the Char Dham concept.
  • Chota Char Dham (Uttarakhand): Badrinath, Kedarnath, Gangotri, Yamunotri.

12 Jyotirlingas — Maharashtra's Share

  • Maharashtra has 3 of the 12 Jyotirlingas: Trimbakeshwar (Nashik), Bhimashankar (Pune), Aundha Nagnath (Hingoli).
  • Vaidyanath/Parli (Beed) is sometimes counted as the 4th Maharashtra Jyotirlinga (dispute with Deoghar, Jharkhand).

3.5 Shakti Peethas of Maharashtra

  • Tulja Bhavani (Tuljapur), Mahalakshmi (Kolhapur), Renuka Devi (Mahur, Nanded) = 3 full peethas.
  • Saptshruni (Nashik) = half peeth (0.5).

Maharashtra Connection

Maharashtra is a centre of pilgrimage:

  • Pandharpur Wari: arguably the most democratic mass pilgrimage in India — no caste barrier, saints of all backgrounds walked (Chokhamela, Tukaram, Namdev, Muktabai). Ashadhi Ekadashi and Kartiki Ekadashi are the main occasions.
  • Simhastha Kumbh (Nashik): every 12 years at Nashik-Trimbakeshwar; among India's most important.
  • Shirdi: Sai Baba's composite Hindu-Muslim spiritual centre; Shirdi Saibaba Sansthan Trust is Maharashtra's wealthiest religious trust.
  • Nanded: Sikh Takht in Maharashtra; large Sikh community; major international pilgrimage.
  • Ashtavinayak: unique 8-temple circuit entirely within Maharashtra; tourism + pilgrimage circuit.

Key Facts

  • Kumbh Mela: 4 locations (Prayagraj, Haridwar, Nashik, Ujjain); UNESCO ICH 2017.
  • Nashik Kumbh = Simhastha; at Ramkund (Godavari).
  • Pandharpur Wari: Vitthal; Ashadhi + Kartiki Ekadashi; Warkari sect.
  • Pushkar Mela: Rajasthan; camel fair; only Brahma temple.
  • 3 Jyotirlingas in Maharashtra: Trimbakeshwar, Bhimashankar, Aundha Nagnath.
  • Tulja Bhavani: Shivaji's Kuldevi; Tuljapur, Osmanabad.
  • Char Dham: Badrinath + Dwarka + Puri + Rameshwaram.

Exam Traps

  • Kumbh UNESCO ICH = 2017 (not 2016 = Yoga, not 2021 = Durga Puja).
  • Nashik Kumbh = Simhastha (when Jupiter in Leo/Simha); same term for Ujjain Kumbh.
  • Pushkar has the only Brahma temple in India — Brahma is rarely worshipped (not a tricky fact, but commonly asked).
  • Pandharpur Vitthal ≠ Tirupati Balaji — often mixed; Vitthal = Pandharpur (Maharashtra); Balaji = Tirupati (AP).
