Sambhaji, Rajaram, Tarabai PREMAINSMH

Overview

Sambhaji (1657–1689), Rajaram (1670–1700), and Tarabai (1675–1761) are the three rulers/regent who sustained the Maratha kingdom through the most severe Mughal pressure under Aurangzeb. Sambhaji's capture and execution (1689) tested Maratha resolve; Rajaram and the remarkable regent Tarabai kept resistance alive through guerrilla warfare until Aurangzeb's death (1707). Very high MPSC MH frequency.

In-Depth

Sambhaji (r. 1680–1689)

Shivaji's eldest son from Saibai; became Chhatrapati in 1680 after Shivaji's death. Key events:

Controversy and character: Sambhaji had a turbulent relationship with Shivaji — he briefly joined the Mughals (Dilir Khan, 1678) before reconciling. He was known for personal indulgence but showed military ability and political acumen.

Military actions:

  • Repelled Mughal pressure in early years (1680–1685) through aggressive counterattacks.
  • Supported Portuguese-besieged Goa indirectly — engaged in conflict with Portuguese (1683).
  • Gave shelter to Akbar (Aurangzeb's rebellious son) who had fled to the Deccan (1681–87).
  • Raided Mughal territories in Berar and Karnataka.

Capture and Execution (1689):

  • Captured at Sangameswar (near Chiplun, Ratnagiri district, Maharashtra) in February 1689 — betrayed by his own men, some accounts say by his brother-in-law Ganoji Shirke.
  • Brought before Aurangzeb at Tulapur (near Pune).
  • Sambhaji refused to reveal the location of Maratha treasure or convert to Islam despite prolonged torture.
  • Executed at Tulapur, 11 March 1689 — considered a martyr by Marathas; the manner of execution (eyes gouged, tongue cut, body dismembered) provoked outrage and stiffened Maratha resistance.

Rajaram (r. 1689–1700)

Shivaji's second son from Soyrabai; became Chhatrapati after Sambhaji's execution. Key events:

Flight to Jinji (1689): When Aurangzeb captured Raigad (1689), Rajaram escaped to Jinji fort (Tamil Nadu) where he conducted Maratha resistance from the deep south for 8 years (1690–1698).

  • Maratha commanders in Maharashtra — Santaji Ghorpade and Dhanaji Jadhav — led brilliant guerrilla campaigns (ganimi kava) against the Mughals.
  • Santaji Ghorpade raided Mughal camps and even temporarily captured Aurangzeb's senior commanders.
  • Rajaram issued Swarajya bonds and kept coordination from Jinji.

Return to Maharashtra and death:

  • Rajaram returned to Sinhagad fort (near Pune) after Jinji fell (1698); then moved to Satara fort.
  • Died at Sinhagad: 2 March 1700, reportedly from illness.

Tarabai (Regent 1700–1707/1708)

Tarabai (née Mohite) was Rajaram's second wife; after his death she appointed her infant son Shivaji II as Chhatrapati and became regent — effectively the commander and strategist of Maratha resistance for 7 critical years.

Military leadership:

  • Personally directed Maratha campaigns against Aurangzeb (1700–1707).
  • Maratha forces under her direction raided as far north as Malwa and Gujarat.
  • Aurangzeb — at his wits' end — wrote of the Marathas "like grass that springs up again and again after being cut."
  • She maintained contact with multiple Maratha chiefs and coordinated dispersed resistance.

After Aurangzeb (1707):

  • When Aurangzeb died (1707) at Ahmednagar, Shahu — Sambhaji's son who had been held captive by Mughals since 1689 — was released.
  • Shahu vs Tarabai power struggle (1707–1713): Shahu claimed the throne; Tarabai resisted.
  • Battle of Khed (1707): Shahu (supported by Dhanaji Jadhav) defeated Tarabai; Shahu became Chhatrapati; Tarabai retreated to Kolhapur — establishing the Kolhapur branch of Bhosale.
  • Tarabai's later machinations (against Peshwa Bajirao, supporting a pretender) shaped Maratha politics till her death in 1761 at age ~86.

Exam Traps

  • Sambhaji was Shivaji's son from Saibai; Rajaram from Soyrabai — different mothers; both Bhosale.
  • Shahu (captive, released 1707) ≠ Shivaji II (Tarabai's infant son) — two different claimants at the same time.
  • Tarabai was NOT Sambhaji's wife — she was Rajaram's wife; Sambhaji's wife was Yesubai (who remained Mughal captive along with infant Shahu).
  • Jinji is in Tamil Nadu, not Maharashtra — Rajaram ruled from the south during exile.
