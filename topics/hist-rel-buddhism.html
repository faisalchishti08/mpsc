Buddhism — Buddha, four noble truths PREMAINS

Overview

Buddhism was founded by Siddhartha Gautama (c.563–483 BCE), known as the Buddha ("the Enlightened One"), a Kshatriya prince of the Shakya clan in Kapilavastu (present-day Nepal/UP border). It rejected the Vedic varna system, sacrificial rituals, and Brahman authority, offering a practical path of ethical conduct, meditation, and wisdom to overcome suffering. Buddhism became a world religion; it shaped Indian art, philosophy, and statecraft before declining in India itself. MPSC tests extensively on the Buddha's life, the Four Noble Truths, Eightfold Path, and key Buddhist concepts.

Four Noble Truths (Ariya Saccas)

The core teaching of Buddhism:

  1. Dukkha: Existence involves suffering/dissatisfaction (birth, disease, old age, death, unfulfilled desires are all dukkha).
  2. Samudaya (Tanha): Suffering has a cause — craving/desire (Tanha = thirst for sensual pleasure, existence, non-existence).
  3. Nirodha: Suffering can be ended — by extinguishing craving, one attains Nibbana (Nirvana).
  4. Magga: There is a path to ending suffering — the Noble Eightfold Path.

Key Concepts

  • Nirvana: Literally "blowing out" — extinction of desire, ego, and the three fires (greed, hatred, delusion); liberation from the cycle of rebirth (Samsara). NOT the same as Hindu Moksha (soul merging with Brahman) — Buddhism denies a permanent soul.
  • Anatman (Anatta): "No-self" — the Buddhist doctrine that there is no permanent, unchanging self or soul; what we think of as "self" is a constantly changing bundle of physical and mental processes. Fundamental departure from Brahmanical/Hindu Atman concept.
  • Anicca (Impermanence): All conditioned things are impermanent — everything changes; nothing is fixed.
  • Dependent Origination (Pratityasamutpada): All phenomena arise in dependence on conditions; nothing exists independently. The chain of 12 links (Nidanas) explains how ignorance leads through a chain of conditions to rebirth and suffering.
  • Middle Way: The Buddha's path between extreme self-indulgence (his earlier princely life) and extreme self-mortification (his years of asceticism). Moderation is the key.

Three Jewels (Triratna) of Buddhism

  1. Buddha: The teacher; the Enlightened One.
  2. Dhamma: The teaching; the truth.
  3. Sangha: The community of monks (and later nuns and lay followers).

"I take refuge in the Buddha, the Dhamma, and the Sangha" (the Trisharana formula).

Five Precepts (Panchashila) for Lay Buddhists

  1. Abstain from taking life (Ahimsa).
  2. Abstain from taking what is not given (non-stealing).
  3. Abstain from sexual misconduct.
  4. Abstain from false speech.
  5. Abstain from intoxicants.

Maharashtra Connection

  • Ajanta Caves (Aurangabad, UNESCO WHS 1983): 30 Buddhist rock-cut caves (~2nd c. BCE–6th c. CE); finest examples of Buddhist art — murals depicting Jataka tales and the Buddha's life. Patronised by Satavahanas, Vakatakas.
  • Karla Caves (Lonavala, Pune): Largest Buddhist Chaitya (prayer hall) in India — Karla Chaitya (~2nd–1st c. BCE); Satavahana-era; impressive wooden/stone columns.
  • Bhaja Caves (near Lonavala): Early Buddhist viharas and chaitya; ~2nd c. BCE.
  • Kanheri Caves (Mumbai): Over 100 Buddhist caves; 1st–9th c. CE; largest Buddhist cave complex in Maharashtra.
  • Nashik Caves (Pandu Lena): ~1st–4th c. CE; Satavahana inscriptions; Hinayana Buddhist caves.
  • Ambedkar's Buddhist Conversion (1956): Dr B.R. Ambedkar led ~500,000 Dalits in mass conversion to Buddhism at Nagpur (Oct 14, 1956) — the largest mass religious conversion in 20th century India; "Navayana" Buddhism (his interpretation emphasising social justice). Nagpur's Deekshabhoomi is now a major pilgrimage site.

Exam Traps

  • Lumbini is in Nepal (modern), not India — but in Mauryan-era geography it was part of the Shakya territory adjoining the Terai.
  • Anatman (no-self) is the key philosophical difference from Hinduism's Atman concept.
  • Nirvana ≠ Hindu Moksha: In Buddhism, there is no permanent soul to merge with Brahman.
  • The first sermon was at Sarnath (Deer Park), NOT Bodh Gaya (that was the enlightenment).
