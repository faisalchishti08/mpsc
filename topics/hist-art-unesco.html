UNESCO World Heritage sites in India PREMAINS

Overview

India has 42 UNESCO World Heritage Sites (as of 2024) — the sixth-highest in the world — spanning natural, cultural, and mixed categories. Additionally, UNESCO recognises Intangible Cultural Heritage and Biosphere Reserves. MPSC tests the list of Indian UNESCO WHS, their states, year of inscription, and the three Maharashtra sites. High Prelims frequency; occasional Mains angle on heritage conservation.

In-Depth

UNESCO World Heritage Site Categories

  • Cultural: monuments, buildings, cities, cultural landscapes.
  • Natural: outstanding natural beauty, biodiversity, geological value.
  • Mixed: both cultural and natural outstanding universal value.

Maharashtra's UNESCO World Heritage Sites

  1. Ajanta Caves (1983) — Buddhist mural paintings; Aurangabad.
  2. Ellora Caves (1983) — multi-religious rock-cut; Aurangabad.
  3. Elephanta Caves (1987) — Shaiva sculptures; Gharapuri island, Mumbai.
  4. Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Terminus (CST) (2004) — Victorian Gothic; Mumbai.
  5. Victorian Gothic and Art Deco Ensembles of Mumbai (2018) — Fort area + Marine Drive.
  6. Western Ghats (2012) — Natural; includes Maharashtra's Sahyadri ranges.

Maharashtra Connection

Six UNESCO WHS in Maharashtra (see above). The Victorian Gothic and Art Deco Ensembles of Mumbai (2018) is the most recent — it covers:

  • Victorian Gothic cluster: CST, High Court, Rajabai Tower, Library, University of Mumbai.
  • Art Deco cluster: Marine Drive (Netaji Subhash Chandra Bose Road) buildings — India's largest Art Deco ensemble outside Miami.

The Western Ghats WHS covers Maharashtra's Sahyadri sub-cluster including Kas Plateau (Valley of Flowers of Maharashtra) in Satara district.

Key Facts

  • India total UNESCO WHS: 42 (as of 2024).
  • First inscribed: 1983 — Ajanta, Ellora, Agra Fort, Taj Mahal (all four in 1983).
  • Khangchendzonga NP (Sikkim, 2016): India's only Mixed WHS.
  • Dholavira (Gujarat, 2021): Harappan site; first Indus Valley site as UNESCO WHS.
  • Hoysala Temples, Santiniketan: inscribed 2023.
  • Kumbh Mela (2017): UNESCO Intangible Heritage.
  • Garba (Gujarat, 2023): most recent Indian ICH inscription.
  • Kudiyattam (2008): first Indian performing art on UNESCO ICH list.

Exam Traps

  • CST was inscribed in 2004, not in 2018 (2018 is the broader Victorian Gothic + Art Deco ensemble).
  • Kudiyattam (2008) was India's first ICH — not Yoga (2016) or Kumbh Mela (2017).
  • Dholavira (2021) is the first IVC/Harappan site — not Mohenjo-daro (which is in Pakistan and separately inscribed).
  • Western Ghats (2012) is Natural, not Cultural — it's a biodiversity WHS.
