Irrigation projects and water disputes PREMAINSINTMH

Overview

Maharashtra has the largest irrigation potential created among all Indian states but one of the lowest utilisation rates — a paradox rooted in incomplete projects, funds misuse, and inter-regional water disputes. Only about 20–22% of the net sown area is under irrigation (against a national average of ~50%). The Irrigation Scam (2012) involving cost escalation in 3,000+ irrigation projects is a landmark governance issue. Interstate water disputes — Godavari Water Disputes Tribunal (GWDT) and Krishna Water Disputes Tribunal (KWDT) — determine Maharashtra's water share. MPSC tests major dams, irrigation potential, disputes, and current schemes.

Inter-State Water Disputes

1. Godavari Water Disputes Tribunal (GWDT) — 1969

  • Constituted 1969 under Interstate River Water Disputes Act, 1956.
  • River: Godavari (flows through Maharashtra, Telangana, Andhra Pradesh, Chhattisgarh, Odisha).
  • Final Award (1980): Maharashtra's share = 1,350 TMC (Thousand Million Cubic feet) for consumptive use.
  • Key allocation: Upper Godavari basin (MH) → mainly Jayakwadi, and Nashik irrigation.

2. Krishna Water Disputes Tribunal I (KWDT-I) — 1969

  • Award (1976): Divided Krishna water among Maharashtra, Karnataka, Andhra Pradesh.
  • Maharashtra's share (KWDT-I): 560 TMC (basic allocation) + 585 TMC surplus = ~1,145 TMC total.
  • Key projects: Koyna, Ujani, Bhima basin projects.

3. Krishna Water Disputes Tribunal II (KWDT-II) — 2004

  • Constituted to redistribute surplus water and review KWDT-I.
  • Final Award (2010) partially modified Maharashtra's share. Pending Supreme Court review.

4. Tapi/Tapti River

  • No formal tribunal; disputes between Maharashtra, Madhya Pradesh, Gujarat on Tapi waters.
  • Hatnur Dam (Jalgaon): Key Tapi project in Maharashtra; irrigation in Khandesh.

Maharashtra Irrigation Scam (2012)

  • A CAG (Comptroller and Auditor General) report (2012) exposed massive cost escalation in 3,000+ irrigation projects (2001–2012).
  • Only ~0.1% additional irrigation potential created despite doubling of irrigation budget.
  • Associated with accusations against former Deputy CM/Water Resources Minister. Led to major political controversy.
  • White Paper (2012) acknowledged mismanagement; led to formation of Munde Commission.
  • This scam is a major MPSC Mains topic — governance, accountability, CAG role.

Current Irrigation Schemes

  • Baliraja Jal Sanjeevani Yojana: Reviving water bodies (bandharas — weirs) in drought-prone areas.
  • PMKSY (Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana): Central scheme; "Har Khet Ko Pani, More Crop Per Drop"; includes AIBP (Accelerated Irrigation Benefit Programme) for incomplete major irrigation projects.
  • Jalyukt Shivar Abhiyan: Drought-proofing by soil and water conservation + deepening of streams; flagship under Devendra Fadnavis government (2014–19); later questioned on effectiveness.

Maharashtra Connection

The irrigation imbalance within Maharashtra — well-irrigated western MH (sugarcane) vs rain-dependent Vidarbha-Marathwada — is a structural cause of agrarian distress and farmer suicides. The irrigation scam (2012) has become a case study in public accountability and the role of CAG. The Maharashtra Water Resources Regulatory Authority (MWRRA) was created to regulate water use but has been largely ineffective.
