Health schemes (Mahatma Phule Jan Arogya Yojana) PREMAINSINTMH

Overview

Maharashtra's flagship health scheme is Mahatma Phule Jan Arogya Yojana (MPJAY) — providing cashless health treatment up to ₹5 lakh (yellow ration card/BPL holders) at empanelled hospitals. It is integrated with the central Ayushman Bharat Pradhan Mantri Jan Arogya Yojana (PM-JAY) under the "Ayushman Bharat Maharashtra" umbrella. Other key schemes include Rajiv Gandhi Jeevandayee Arogya Yojana (RGJAY) (predecessor to MPJAY), and programmes targeting maternal health, COVID vaccination, and disease surveillance. MPSC tests scheme names, coverage, year, and the distinction between state and central health schemes.

Key Health Infrastructure

  • Public Health Department: Runs district hospitals (1 per district), sub-district hospitals, Primary Health Centres (PHCs), Sub-Centres; approximately 1,800 PHCs, 10,000+ Sub-Centres in Maharashtra.
  • Maharashtra Medical Education and Research (DMER): Governs medical colleges — Maharashtra has ~40+ government medical colleges; GMCs (Government Medical Colleges) at Nagpur, Pune, Mumbai (KEM, Nair, Sion hospitals — Grant Government Medical College + Sir JJ Group of Hospitals), Aurangabad, Nashik, Latur, etc.
  • Haffkine Institute, Mumbai (Parel): Historic public health research institute; established 1899 by Waldemar Haffkine (developed cholera and plague vaccines); produces biological reagents and medicines; plague vaccine for bubonic plague (Pune 1994 scare).
  • National Institute of Virology (NIV), Pune: India's premier virology institute; ICMR institute; identified first COVID-19 case in India (January 2020, from a traveller returning from Wuhan); key role in COVID surveillance; develops diagnostic kits.
  • Serum Institute of India (SII), Pune: World's largest vaccine manufacturer by number of doses; Cyrus Poonawalla; produces Covishield (AstraZeneca's Oxford COVID vaccine), Covi-Shield, Pentavalent, MMR, Varicella vaccines; exports to 170+ countries.

Disease Burden and Maharashtra-Specific Health Issues

  • TB (Tuberculosis): Maharashtra among top 5 states for TB cases; Mumbai (Dharavi) historically a TB hotspot; Nikshay Poshan Yojana (₹500/month nutrition support for TB patients).
  • Malaria: Coastal Maharashtra (Raigad, Ratnagiri, tribal belts); National Vector Borne Disease Control Programme (NVBDCP).
  • COVID-19 (2020–22): Maharashtra was the most affected state in India — Mumbai, Pune among earliest epicentres; Pune NIV confirmed India's first case; Serum Institute (Covishield) critical for India's vaccination drive.
  • Farmer Suicide and Mental Health: Vidarbha-Marathwada; SNEHI programme (mental health support); NIMHANS Collaborations.
  • Sickle Cell Disease: Predominantly tribal belt (Vidarbha, Nashik, Nandurbar); Sickle Cell Anaemia Mukt Bharat Mission (2023, central) — Maharashtra a target state.

National Health Mission (NHM) in Maharashtra

  • NHM = NRHM (Rural) + NUHM (Urban).
  • Maharashtra NHM: Covers maternal health, child health, immunisation, disease control.
  • ASHA workers (Accredited Social Health Activists): ~70,000+ ASHAs in Maharashtra; link between community and health system; key for home deliveries, immunization, TB DOT.
  • Janani Suraksha Yojana (JSY): Conditional cash transfer for institutional delivery; ₹700 (rural) to ₹600 (urban) per delivery in Maharashtra.

Maharashtra Connection

The Serum Institute of India made Pune globally significant during COVID-19 — producing Covishield that vaccinated billions. The National Institute of Virology (NIV) in Pune confirmed India's first COVID case. Maharashtra's MPJAY (renamed from Jyotirao Phule's name) combines the legacy of social reformers with modern health policy. The Haffkine Institute (1899) is the oldest public health institution in India.
