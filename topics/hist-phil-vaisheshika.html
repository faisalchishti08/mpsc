Vaisheshika PREMAINS

Overview

Vaisheshika is one of India's six orthodox philosophical schools, founded by Kanada (Kashyapa) and systematised in the Vaisheshikasutras (~3rd–2nd c. BCE). It is primarily a metaphysical and atomist school — arguing that the universe is composed of eternal, indestructible atoms (paramanus) and categorising all reality into seven padarthas (categories). Paired with Nyaya in the Shad Darshanas tradition. MPSC tests it primarily in the context of all six orthodox schools.

In-Depth

Founder and Texts

  • Kanada (literally "atom-eater" or "grain-eater"): the name reflects his philosophical preoccupation with the smallest particles of matter.
  • Vaisheshikasutras: 10 chapters; defines the seven categories of existence.
  • The name Vaisheshika comes from vishesha (particularity/distinction) — the school emphasises the particular qualities that distinguish one thing from another.

(Original Vaisheshikasutras had 6 padarthas; abhava was added later.)

Atomic Theory

Vaisheshika's most celebrated contribution: Indian atomism (Paramanu theory):

  • Ultimate constituents of matter are paramanus (atoms) — eternal, indivisible, indestructible.
  • Dvyanuka (dyad): two atoms combine to form the smallest visible particle.
  • Tryanuka (triad): three dyads form a more complex particle, and so on.
  • The combination of atoms is caused by divine will (God as the efficient cause) — making Vaisheshika theistic.
  • This atomism was developed independently of Greek atomism (Democritus) and is conceptually comparable though philosophically distinct.

Relationship with Nyaya

Vaisheshika and Nyaya are closely allied — the two schools accept each other's foundational principles and are often treated as one school (Nyaya-Vaisheshika):

  • Nyaya accepts Vaisheshika's metaphysical categories.
  • Vaisheshika accepts Nyaya's epistemological framework (pramanas).
  • Both are theistic and pluralistic (accept multiple individual souls).

Maharashtra Connection

Not directly Maharashtra-specific. The Vaisheshika atomic theory was debated in great medieval academic centres. Bhandarkar Oriental Research Institute (BORI), Pune has significant manuscript collections including Vaisheshika commentaries, though this is an institutional rather than philosophical connection.

Key Facts

  • Founder: Kanada (Kashyapa).
  • Text: Vaisheshikasutras.
  • Core: Seven padarthas (categories of reality); atomic theory (paramanu).
  • Nine substances: earth, water, fire, air, ether, time, space, mind, soul.
  • Atoms: eternal, indestructible paramanus.
  • School is theistic.
  • Paired with: Nyaya (both = Nyaya-Vaisheshika).

  • 7 padarthas: "D-G-K-S-V-S-A" (Dravya, Guna, Karma, Samanya, Vishesha, Samavaya, Abhava).
  • 9 substances: "Earth Water Fire Air Ether Time Space Mind Soul" (5 elements + 4).
  • Kanada = atom theory (kan = grain/atom in Sanskrit).

Exam Traps

  • Vaisheshika ≠ Vaishnava — Vaisheshika is a philosophical school; Vaishnava = devotee of Vishnu; don't confuse.
  • Original Vaisheshikasutras had 6 padarthas, not 7 — Abhava was added later.
  • Paramanu = atoms; Dvyanuka = dyad — these technical terms appear in MCQs.
