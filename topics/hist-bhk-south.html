South Indian Bhakti — Alvars and Nayanars PREMAINS

Overview

South Indian Bhakti (6th–10th c. CE) was the earliest phase of the pan-Indian Bhakti movement, produced by the Alvars (Vaishnava poet-saints) and Nayanars (Shaiva poet-saints) of Tamil Nadu. Their devotional hymns — composed in Tamil — formed the Nalayira Divya Prabandham (4000 verses) and Tevaram (Shiva hymns) respectively. Their emphasis on direct devotion, vernacular poetry, and egalitarian access to God laid the foundation for the entire subsequent Bhakti tradition across India. MPSC tests the number of Alvars/Nayanars, their texts, and key saint names.

Key Alvars:

  • Andal: only female Alvar; 8th c.; from Srivilliputhur; composed Thiruppavai (30 stanzas) and Nachiar Thirumozhi; considered an incarnation of Bhudevi (earth goddess).
  • Nammalvar: greatest Alvar; Tiruvaymoli (his 1102-verse masterpiece) = "Tamil Veda"; from Tirunelveli.
  • Periyalvar: Andal's adoptive father; composed Periyalvar Thirumozhi.
  • Kulashekhara Alvar: king of Travancore who renounced kingdom.

Key Nayanars:

  • Appar (Thirunavukkarasar): 7th c.; converted from Jainism to Shaivism.
  • Sambandar (Thirugnana Sambandar): 7th c.; child prodigy; composed Tevaram; credited with converting Jains + Buddhists back to Shaivism.
  • Sundarar: 8th c.; composed Tiruthonda Thogai (listing of 63 Nayanars).
  • Manikkavasagar: 9th c.; Tiruvacagam ("Holy utterances"); profound mystical poetry; NOT in Tevaram.

Social Character

  • Alvars and Nayanars crossed caste lines: saints from Brahmin, Vellala, Paraiyan (Dalit), fisherfolk, and weaver communities.
  • Nandanar: Dalit Nayanar (from Paraiyan/untouchable community); his story of wanting to enter Chidambaram temple (and ultimately achieving it miraculously) is iconic.
  • Andal (woman): female Alvar — notable gender inclusivity.
  • Appar: converted from Jainism = religious crossing.

Impact on Bhakti Tradition

  • Ramanuja (11th–12th c.) built Vishishtadvaita philosophy on the foundation of Alvar devotion; systematised their bhakti.
  • Chola temples (Brihadeeswara) and the tradition of Agamic Shaivism were influenced by Nayanar devotion.
  • Inspired the later Varkari tradition of Maharashtra (Namdev was influenced by Alvars through Pandharpur's South Indian connections).
  • Sri Vaishnava sampradaya (founded by Ramanuja, based in Srirangam): direct theological heir of Alvar tradition.

Maharashtra Connection

While the Alvars and Nayanars are primarily Tamil Nadu traditions, their influence on Maharashtra is significant:

  • Pandharpur Vitthal: scholars trace the Vitthal/Vithoba cult to a South Indian (possibly Karnataka-Tamil) origin, connected to Vishnu worship traditions that the Alvars promoted.
  • Namdev's South India journey: Namdev (14th c. Maharashtra) reportedly visited South Indian temples and encountered Vaishnava traditions that may have influenced his compositions.
  • Varkari tradition's Saguna Bhakti to Vitthal parallels Alvar's Saguna devotion to Vishnu — scholars see structural continuity.
  • Kala Bhairava temple (in Nashik) and Shaiva traditions in Maharashtra share theological features with Nayanar Shaivism.

Exam Traps

  • 12 Alvars, 63 Nayanars — numbers reversed in common confusion.
  • Tevaram ≠ Nalayira Divya Prabandham: Tevaram = Shaiva (Nayanars); NDPrabandham = Vaishnava (Alvars).
  • Andal = Alvar (Vaishnava), NOT Nayanar (Shaiva) — female saint, but wrong tradition if attributed to Nayanars.
  • Ramanuja's tradition = Alvar-based (Vaishnava), NOT Nayanar-based.
