Overview

Maharashtra has 6 national parks, including India's only urban national park (Sanjay Gandhi NP, Mumbai) and the famous tiger reserve (Tadoba-Andhari, Chandrapur). National park names, locations, key species, and river/geography associations are direct MPSC Prelims targets. Tiger reserves, area, and UNESCO linkages are also tested.

Tadoba-Andhari Tiger Reserve (TATR)

  • Location: Chandrapur district, Vidarbha (eastern Maharashtra).
  • Area: National Park ~625 sq km; total Tiger Reserve (with buffer) ~1,727 sq km — Maharashtra's largest tiger reserve.
  • Tiger population: One of India's highest densities; Maharashtra's premier wildlife tourism destination.
  • Rivers: Tadoba Lake (natural) at the centre; Andhari river flows through (name = "Andhari" = dark forest river).
  • History: Tadoba Tiger Reserve declared 1955 (one of India's first protected areas under Project Tiger); NP status 1955; included in Project Tiger network 1973 (although formally notified TR in 1993).
  • Species: Bengal Tiger, Leopard, Wild Dog (Dhole), Gaur (Indian Bison), Nilgai, Sambar, Chital, Sloth Bear, Indian Python, Mugger Crocodile.
  • Chandrapur district also houses Bramhapuri Wildlife Division — known for high tiger-human conflict.

Melghat Tiger Reserve (Gugamal NP)

  • Location: Amravati district, Satpura-Balaghat ranges.
  • Established: 1974 (one of the original nine Project Tiger reserves).
  • Core: Gugamal National Park (~361 sq km); total TR area ~2,768 sq km.
  • River: Tapti (Tapi) river flows through; also known for Chikhaldara hill station nearby.
  • Species: Tiger, Leopard, Dhole, Gaur, Sambar; also significant for rare Wolf and Four-horned Antelope (Chowsingha).
  • Tribal: Korku tribal communities live in and around Melghat; major conservation-development conflict.

Sanjay Gandhi National Park (SGNP)

  • Location: Borivali, Mumbai Suburban district (extends into Thane).
  • Unique: Only national park within a metropolitan city — Mumbai; one of the most visited national parks in the world by visitor count.
  • Leopards: ~35+ leopards (2020 estimates) coexist with urban Mumbai — leopard-human conflict a major concern.
  • Kanheri Caves: Inside SGNP — 109 Buddhist rock-cut caves (2nd–10th century AD); largest Buddhist cave complex in India; potential UNESCO nomination.
  • Lakes: Vihar Lake (1860) and Tulsi Lake (1879) inside SGNP — supply ~10% of Mumbai's water.

Pench Tiger Reserve

  • Spans Maharashtra–Madhya Pradesh border; Nagpur district (MH side) + Seoni (MP side).
  • Kipling Connection: Rudyard Kipling's The Jungle Book (1894) — setting for Mowgli's jungle — is widely believed to be inspired by Pench/Seoni forests.
  • Pench river flows through; deciduous teak/sal forest.
  • MH portion: Pench National Park (NP) + buffer = Pench Tiger Reserve (MH).

Sahyadri Tiger Reserve (Chandoli NP)

  • Location: Multi-district: Sangli, Satara, Kolhapur (Sahyadri foothills).
  • Includes Chandoli National Park and Koyna Wildlife Sanctuary as core zones.
  • Part of the Western Ghats UNESCO World Heritage landscape.
  • Corridors: Important for leopard, wild dog, and small cat movement between Goa (Bhagwan Mahavir WLS) and Maharashtra.

Maharashtra Connection

Tadoba is Maharashtra's flagship wildlife destination — it regularly appears in MPSC questions for its tiger population, Project Tiger status, and Chandrapur district association. SGNP's Kanheri Caves link wildlife conservation with Buddhist heritage — a geography + history combination. The Melghat Tiger Reserve (Korku tribals) and its project tiger status (one of original 9 in 1974) is a standard date-question.

Exam Traps

  • Tadoba-Andhari NP is in Chandrapur, NOT Nagpur — common confusion between Vidarbha's two biggest districts.
  • Melghat (not Gugamal alone) is the Project Tiger reserve; Gugamal NP is the core zone within it.
  • Pench spans two states (MH + MP) — do NOT say it's only in Maharashtra.
