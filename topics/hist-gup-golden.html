Golden age — science, literature, art PREMAINS

Overview

The Gupta Golden Age (c. 319–543 CE) is the period of India's greatest classical achievements in Sanskrit literature, mathematics, astronomy, medicine, sculpture, and philosophy. Under Chandragupta II and his successors, patronised scholars produced works that defined Indian civilisation for centuries. MPSC tests specific names, texts, and discoveries — "who wrote what" and "what was discovered first" are classic Prelims traps; Mains asks about the significance of the period as a whole.

In-Depth

Sanskrit Literature

Kalidasa (c. late 4th–early 5th century CE) — the greatest Sanskrit poet and dramatist; associated with Chandragupta II's court:

  • Plays: Abhijnanashakuntalam (Shakuntala), Vikramorvasiyam, Malavikagnimitram.
  • Epics/Kavyas: Raghuvamsham, Kumarasambhavam.
  • Lyric poems: Meghadutam (Cloud Messenger), Ritusamharam.
  • Abhijnanashakuntalam was the first Indian text translated into a European language (German, by Georg Forster, 1791; praised by Goethe).

Other literary figures:

  • Vishakhadatta: Mudrarakshasa (play on Chandragupta Maurya's rise — historical drama).
  • Shudraka: Mricchakatika (The Little Clay Cart) — realistic social drama.
  • Vatsyayana: compiled Kamasutra (treatise on social conduct and love).
  • Vishnu Sharma: Panchatantra (fable collection; translated into Arabic as Kalila wa Dimna, then across the world).
  • Amarasimha: Amarakosha — first systematic Sanskrit thesaurus/lexicon (one of Navaratna).

Mathematics and Astronomy

Aryabhata (476–550 CE) — born at Kusumapura (Pataliputra); his Aryabhatiya (499 CE) is a landmark:

  • Declared the Earth is a sphere that rotates on its axis (not the Sun moving around Earth).
  • Calculated π (pi) ≈ 3.1416 (correct to 4 decimal places).
  • Gave the correct explanation of solar and lunar eclipses as shadows (not a demon Rahu/Ketu).
  • Introduced the concept of zero (as a positional placeholder) and the decimal place-value system — foundational for modern mathematics.
  • Calculated the sidereal year as 365.3586... days (very close to modern value).
  • Developed algebra and trigonometric functions (jya = sine).

Varahamihira (505–587 CE) — Brihatsamhita (encyclopaedia of astronomy, astrology, natural science) and Panchasiddhantika (summary of five astronomical schools). Identified with Ujjain.

Brahmagupta (598–668 CE) — later than Guptas but in the tradition; Brahmasphutasiddhanta — formalised rules for arithmetic with zero (including division rules, though 0/0 he described incorrectly).

Medicine

Charaka (Charaka Samhita) — originally compiled in the Kushana period (Kanishka's court), but the text was revised and canonised during the Gupta era. Covers internal medicine (kaya chikitsa), prognosis, drug preparation. One of the foundational texts of Ayurveda.

Sushruta (Sushruta Samhita) — surgical text; describes 120 surgical instruments (shastrakarman), plastic surgery procedures (rhinoplasty — nose reconstruction), cataract surgery, setting of fractures. The Gupta period saw both texts receive final form and wide circulation.

Nagarjuna — alchemist-physician; texts on metallurgy and rasa shastra (mercury-based medicine).

Sculpture and Art

Sarnath School (Gupta sculpture): the synthesis of Gandhara (Greco-Roman) and Mathura (indigenous) styles produces the canonical Gupta Buddha — serene, spiritualised face; clinging transparent robe; ushnisha as smooth dome; elongated earlobes; mudras perfectly defined. Key examples:

  • Sarnath Buddha (c. 475 CE, Mathura sandstone) — considered the finest stone Buddha; teaching pose (dharmachakra mudra).
  • Sultanganj Buddha (copper, Bihar) — 2.3 m tall; Bihar Museum, now Birmingham.

Temple architecture: Guptas built the first free-standing stone temples (earlier temples were wood or rock-cut):

  • Dashavatara Temple, Deogarh (UP) — Panchayatana style; elaborate Vishnu panels; c. 500 CE.
  • Bhitargaon Temple (UP) — early terracotta brick temple.
  • Vishnu Temple, Tigawa (MP) — early Nagara-style tower (shikhara).
  • These mark the origin of the Nagara (north Indian) temple style: curvilinear shikhara, garbhagriha, porch (mandapa).

Philosophy and Religion

  • Bhagavatism/Vaishnavism was the state religion under Guptas (title Paramabhagavata — "devoted to Vishnu"). Temple construction for Vishnu and Shiva proliferates.
  • Buddhism continued — Nalanda monastery founded; Faxian documents thriving Buddhist monasteries.
  • Puranas received their canonical form — 18 Mahapuranas compiled, including Vishnu Purana, Matsya Purana. These spread Hindu mythology and cosmology.
  • Nyaya, Vaisheshika, Samkhya schools of philosophy were systematised in this period.

Maharashtra Connection

Not directly Maharashtra-specific. However, the Vakataka dynasty (Maharashtra) — closely allied with the Guptas — patronised the Ajanta cave paintings (phases II–V of painting, c. 460–480 CE), which are the greatest surviving examples of Gupta-era art in the world. Ajanta's Buddhist murals (Padmapani Bodhisattva, Mahajanaka Jataka) embody Gupta aesthetic ideals. The Vakataka prime minister Varahadeva and Queen Prabhavati Gupta (daughter of Chandragupta II) are named in Ajanta inscriptions.

Key Facts

  • Kalidasa's masterpiece play: Abhijnanashakuntalam — first Indian text translated into German (1791).
  • Aryabhata's text: Aryabhatiya (499 CE); born at Kusumapura.
  • Aryabhata's key claims: Earth rotates; eclipses = shadows; π ≈ 3.1416.
  • Dashavatara Temple (Deogarh): early Nagara-style free-standing stone temple; c. 500 CE.
  • Sarnath Buddha (c. 475 CE): finest Gupta sculpture.
  • Panchatantra translated to Arabic as Kalila wa Dimna.
  • Sushruta: 120 surgical instruments; rhinoplasty; cataract surgery.
  • Amarakosha: by Amarasimha — first Sanskrit thesaurus.

Memory Aids

  • "KAV MAS" — Kalidasa, Aryabhata, Varahamihira, Munisuvrata/Vishnu-Sharma, Amarasimha, Sushruta.
  • Kalidasa's three plays: "SAM" — Shakuntala, Abhijnanashakuntalam (same), Malavikagnimitram, (Vikramorvasiyam) → "SVM" for three plays.
  • "Aryabhata 499 CE, Pataliputra, π = 3.1416, Earth spins" — cluster of facts.
  • Deogarh = "God's fort = first stone Vishnu temple."

Exam Traps

  • Aryabhata (Gupta period, 476–550 CE) vs Brahmagupta (later, 598–668 CE) — two different mathematicians, often confused.
  • Aryabhata proposed Earth rotates (daily rotation) but did NOT fully propose the heliocentric model — don't overclaim.
  • Kalidasa is associated with Chandragupta II — NOT with Chandragupta I or Ashoka.
  • The Navaratna (nine gems) concept is legendary/traditional — its exact membership varies by source; don't memorise a single fixed list as a verified fact.
