Bahmani kingdom and Deccan sultanates PREMAINSMH

Overview

The Bahmani Sultanate (1347–1527) was the first major independent Muslim kingdom of the Deccan, founded by Alauddin Bahman Shah (Hasan Gangu) at Gulbarga after a revolt against Muhammad bin Tughlaq. On its disintegration it gave rise to five successor Deccan Sultanates directly relevant to Maharashtra's medieval history. MPSC (especially MH tag) asks heavily on Bahmani administration, its breakup, and the resulting Deccan Sultanates.

In-Depth

Foundation and Extent

In 1347, Hasan Gangu — an Amir under the Tughlaqs — rebelled and declared independence, taking the title Alauddin Bahman Shah. The kingdom covered the Deccan plateau between the Vindhyas and the Krishna river, with the capital initially at Gulbarga (Ahsanabad) and later shifted to Bidar (1424) under Ahmad Shah I.

Mahmud Gawan — The Great Minister

Mahmud Gawan (Khwaja Mahmud Gawan) was the most distinguished minister of the Bahmani kingdom under Muhammad Shah III (1463–1482). Originally a Persian merchant from Gilan, he became the chief minister (Wazir) and was the real power behind the kingdom:

  • Military: conquered Goa from Vijayanagara (1469); captured Konkan coast; expanded Bahmani power westward.
  • Administrative reforms: divided the kingdom into 8 provinces (tarafs) instead of 4; reduced powers of provincial governors; centralised royal control.
  • Education: built the famous Madrasa of Mahmud Gawan at Bidar (1472) — a landmark of Bahmani architecture with Persian tile-work.
  • Executed in 1481 by Muhammad Shah III after a forged letter was used against him; his death accelerated Bahmani's disintegration.

Bahmani Decline and the Five Successor States

Rivalries between Deccanis (Indian Muslims/converts) and Afaqis (foreigners from Persia, Arabia, Turkey) weakened the court. Provincial governors declared independence between 1490–1527:

The last Bahmani sultan Kalim-Ullah died in 1527, ending the dynasty. The five Sultanates together are known as the Deccan Sultanates.

Significance for Maharashtra

  • Ahmednagar Nizam Shahi and Berar Imad Shahi were directly within what is now Maharashtra.
  • Chand Bibi (regent of Ahmednagar, c. 1590s) famously defended the Ahmednagar fort against Mughal forces under Akbar's son Murad.
  • Shivaji Maharaj served under the Bijapur Adil Shahi initially (his father Shahaji was a Bijapur noble); Shivaji's early fort-taking was seen as defiance of Bijapur.
  • The Deccan Sultanates collectively participated in the Battle of Talikota (1565) against Vijayanagara.

Culture and Architecture

  • Bahmani rulers were patrons of Persian culture; their courts attracted scholars and artists.
  • Gulbarga style (early): arched prayer hall, flat roof; Gulbarga Jama Masjid inspired by Cordoba mosque.
  • Bidar style (later): Persian blue tile-work (kashi tiles); Madrasa of Mahmud Gawan (minaret-based Persian style).
  • Bidri metalwork — distinctive black metal inlaid with silver; originated in Bidar under Bahmani rule.
  • Urdu language traces its origins partly to the Deccani linguistic fusion of the Bahmani period.

Maharashtra Connection

Directly central: Ahmednagar (Nizam Shahi) and Berar (Imad Shahi) are entirely in today's Maharashtra. Ahmednagar Fort, where Nehru was imprisoned by the British (1942), has roots in Nizam Shahi construction. Chand Bibi, the warrior-queen who defended Ahmednagar, is a major figure in Maharashtra's history. The Adil Shahi conflict with Shivaji forms the basis of the early Maratha story. Daulatabad (Maharashtra) was earlier the Bahmani administrative centre in the Deccan.

Key Facts

  • Bahmani founded: 1347, by Alauddin Bahman Shah (Hasan Gangu).
  • First capital: Gulbarga; shifted to Bidar in 1424.
  • Greatest minister: Mahmud Gawan (executed 1481).
  • Mahmud Gawan built Madrasa at Bidar (1472).
  • Five successor states: Ahmednagar, Bijapur, Golkonda, Bidar, Berar.
  • Berar capital: Ellichpur (Achalpur) in Vidarbha.
  • Bidri metalwork originates at Bidar.
  • Bahmani dynasty ended: 1527.

Exam Traps

  • Bijapur (Adil Shahi) ≠ Ahmednagar (Nizam Shahi) — MPSC often asks which Sultanate Shivaji initially served under (Bijapur/Adil Shahi through his father Shahaji).
  • Chand Bibi = Ahmednagar, not Bijapur — she later became regent of Bijapur too, but her famous defence is Ahmednagar.
  • Golkonda is in Telangana (Hyderabad) — not Maharashtra; don't conflate with the Deccan Sultanates that are in Maharashtra.
  • Bahmani founded in 1347 (not 1336 — that's Vijayanagara's date).
