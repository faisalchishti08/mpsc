36 districts and recent district changes PREMAINSINTMH

Overview

Maharashtra has 36 districts as of 2014, when Palghar was carved out of Thane district to become the 36th. Districts are the primary unit of administration — each headed by a District Collector (Jilhadhikari) who is a key MPSC-recruited/IAS officer. District names, their divisions, and changes over time are high-frequency Prelims topics.

The 36th District — Palghar

  • Palghar was carved out of Thane district on 1 August 2014 to become Maharashtra's 36th district.
  • Rationale: Thane was one of the largest and most populous districts in India; better administration required bifurcation.
  • Palghar is a predominantly tribal district (Warli tribe, 36% ST population); significant for GI-tagged Warli painting.
  • HQ: Palghar town.

Recent Renamings (2023)

  • Aurangabad → Chhatrapati Sambhajinagar (June 2023): Named after Chhatrapati Sambhaji Maharaj, son of Shivaji Maharaj.
  • Osmanabad → Dharashiv (June 2023): Revived the old historical name.

Notable District Facts

  • Gadchiroli: Largest district by area; borders Chhattisgarh; highly forested; significant Left-Wing Extremism (LWE)/Naxal zone.
  • Nandurbar: Highest ST (Scheduled Tribe) population proportion among Maharashtra districts; tribal sub-division with Bhil community dominant.
  • Mumbai City + Mumbai Suburban: Smallest districts by area but highest population density.
  • Hingoli: Maharashtra's newest district before Palghar (carved 1999 from Nanded).
  • Washim: Carved from Akola in 1998.
  • Solapur: Known for the Pandharpur pilgrimage (Vitthal temple) and textile (towels/cotton).

District Administration

  • District Collector (DC): Head of revenue administration; also District Magistrate (DM) for law and order.
  • Collector is an IAS officer (State Civil Services officers also serve as Additional Collectors).
  • MPSC recruits Deputy Collectors (Naib Tahsildar → Tahsildar → Deputy Collector track).

Maharashtra Connection

The creation of Palghar (2014) and the renamings (2023) are Maharashtra current-affairs essentials. Gadchiroli's LWE situation is a recurring internal security topic. Nandurbar's tribal welfare programs (van adhikar pattas under Forest Rights Act 2006) are policy exam staples.

Key Facts

  • Total districts: 36 (as of 1 August 2014)
  • 36th district: Palghar (carved from Thane, 1 August 2014)
  • Largest district by area: Ahmednagar (~17,000 sq km) — wait, Gadchiroli is also large. Actually Ahmednagar district is largest by area in Maharashtra.
  • Renamed districts (2023): Aurangabad → Chhatrapati Sambhajinagar; Osmanabad → Dharashiv
  • Highest ST %: Nandurbar district
  • Most populous district: Thane (post-Palghar split, still extremely dense)
  • Largest by area: Ahmednagar (~17,413 sq km); Gadchiroli (~14,412 sq km) second

Memory Aids

Exam Traps

  • Before 2014, Maharashtra had 35 districts — MPSC questions from older syllabus materials still cite 35. Know both figures and the date of change.
  • Palghar was carved from Thane, NOT Nashik or Raigad.
  • The largest district by area is Ahmednagar, not Nagpur or Gadchiroli (common confusion).
