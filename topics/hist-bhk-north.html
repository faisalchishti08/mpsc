North Indian saints — Kabir, Nanak, Tulsidas, Surdas, Mirabai PREMAINS

Overview

North Indian Bhakti saints of the 15th–17th centuries created a profound devotional literature in vernacular languages (Hindi, Punjabi, Braj Bhasha) that challenged caste orthodoxy and Brahminical ritual. The key saints — Kabir, Raidas (Ravidas), Tulsidas, Surdas, Mirabai, Guru Nanak — represent both Saguna and Nirguna streams and are tested extensively in MPSC for their compositions, social positions, and specific facts.

Ramananda — The Bridge

  • Ramananda (14th–15th c., Varanasi): taught that devotion to Rama transcends caste; accepted disciples of all castes.
  • His disciples included both high-caste (Tulsidas) and low-caste (Kabir, Raidas, Sena) saints — making him the "bridge" between South Indian Bhakti (from Ramanuja's tradition) and North Indian Bhakti.

Kabir — Key Details

  • Raised by Muslim weavers (julaha community) but may have been Hindu-born; his identity was intentionally ambiguous.
  • Disciple of Ramananda (Hindu saint); also influenced by Sufis.
  • Dohas (couplets): pithy, satirical sayings in simple Hindi; attacked caste, idol worship, sectarianism.
  • Example Doha: "Pothi padhi padhi jag mua, pandit bhayo na koi; dhai akshar prem ka, padhe so pandit hoi" (Reading books the world has died, no one became a pandit; whoever reads the two-and-a-half letters of 'love' becomes a pandit).
  • Bijak: primary collection of Kabir's works (in Kashi dialect); used by the Kabir Panth sect.
  • Kabir's followers (Kabir Panthis) do not subscribe to any specific religion.
  • Sikandar Lodi reportedly persecuted Kabir for his iconoclasm.

Guru Nanak and the Sikh Connection

  • Guru Nanak founded the Sikh faith — not strictly a Bhakti movement but shares emphasis on nirguna devotion, equality, and community (langar = community meal transcending caste).
  • Udasis: Guru Nanak's four major journeys (east, south, north, west — covering much of Asia and the Middle East).
  • Japji Sahib: the foundational Sikh prayer; begins each reading of the Adi Granth.
  • Adi Granth (Guru Granth Sahib): compiled by Guru Arjan Dev (5th Guru, 1604); includes compositions of Bhakti saints including Kabir, Raidas, Namdev (Maharashtra).

Ashtachhap Poets (Vallabha Sampradaya)

  • Vallabhacharya (1479–1531): founded Pushtimarga (Path of Grace) — dedicated to Krishna; Saguna.
  • Ashtachhap = 8 poets associated with Vallabha's tradition including Surdas (greatest), Kumbhandas, Paramanandadas.
  • Based in Braj region (Vrindavan/Mathura).

Mirabai

  • Princess of Merta (Rajasthan); married Bhojraj (Rana Sanga's son, Mewar kingdom).
  • Defied royal convention; left the palace for Vrindavan devotion to Krishna.
  • Reportedly accepted Raidas (Ravidas) as her guru.
  • Bhajans in Braj Bhasha + Rajasthani; themes of longing, union with Krishna.

Maharashtra Connection

  • Namdev's padas are included in the Adi Granth (compiled in Punjab) — showing the Maharashtrian saints' pan-Indian reach (Namdev spent years in Punjab; his compositions in Punjabi are in the Granth).
  • Eknath translated and interpreted works that bridge Maharashtra and North India.
  • Warkari tradition and North Indian Bhakti are parallel streams that influenced each other — Namdev's journey to Punjab is the key link.
  • Kabir's Nirguna philosophy parallels that of Namdev's later compositions.
  • Raidas/Ravidas and Chokhamela (Maharashtra): both Dalit saint-poets of the same century; both wrote movingly of casteism and longing for a casteless divine order.

Key Facts

  • Kabir: Varanasi; weaver; disciple of Ramananda; Nirguna; Bijak; Dohas.
  • Tulsidas: Ramcharitmanas; Rama-devotion; Brahmin; Saguna.
  • Surdas: blind; Sursagar; Krishna; Ashtachhap; Saguna.
  • Mirabai: Rajput; Krishna-devotion; Raidas's disciple; Saguna.
  • Guru Nanak: Sikhism; Nirguna; Japji Sahib; 4 Udasis.
  • Raidas/Ravidas: cobbler; Nirguna; Adi Granth (40 padas); "Begampura."
  • Ramananda: teacher of Kabir + Raidas; bridge to South Indian Bhakti.

Memory Aids

  • Saints vs stream: "Nirguna = Kabir, Nanak, Raidas; Saguna = Tulsidas (Rama), Surdas (Krishna), Mirabai (Krishna)".
  • Kabir teacher: "Kabir's guru = Ramananda (Hindu)".
  • Ramcharitmanas = Tulsidas; Hindi; Awadhi dialect.

Exam Traps

  • Tulsidas wrote Ramcharitmanas in Awadhi Hindi, NOT Sanskrit (Sanskrit Ramayana = Valmiki).
  • Kabir was born to a Muslim weaver family but his own faith was syncretic — neither fully Hindu nor Muslim; avoid labelling him either.
  • Surdas was blind from birth (or became blind early); his visualisations of Krishna are considered miraculous.
  • Raidas ≠ Namdev — both low-caste saints but from different states (Raidas=UP cobbler; Namdev=Maharashtra tailor).
