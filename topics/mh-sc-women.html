Women & child schemes (Ladki Bahin, Manodhairya) PREMAINSINTMH

Overview

Maharashtra has a robust portfolio of women and child welfare schemes — ranging from direct benefit transfers to legal protection and skill development. The Mukhyamantri Majhi Ladki Bahin Yojana (2024, ₹1,500/month to women) and Manodhairya Yojana (compensation for rape/acid attack survivors) are recent key schemes. Maharashtra also administers the POSH Act (Prevention of Sexual Harassment) implementation and Women's Policy 2001 (revised). MPSC tests scheme names, amounts, eligibility, implementing agencies, and landmark acts.

Legal Framework for Women — Maharashtra Specific

  • Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005 (central): Implemented through Maharashtra Protection Officers; Maharashtra appointed Protection Officers in all districts.
  • Maharashtra Prevention of Eve-Teasing Act, 1998: Specific state law against street sexual harassment; one of India's first such state laws.
  • Protection of Children from Sexual Offences Act, 2012 (POCSO) (central): Maharashtra has Special POCSO Courts in major districts; fast-track courts.
  • Women's Policy, Maharashtra 2001 (and 2014 revision): Maharashtra State Women's Policy — guarantees 33% participation in governance, economic security, legal rights.
  • Maharashtra Commission for Women (Mahila Aayog): Quasi-judicial body; hears complaints from women; recommends policy; appointed by state.

Manodhairya — First in India

  • Manodhairya Yojana (2013) was India's first comprehensive state scheme providing immediate monetary relief + services to rape survivors.
  • Inspired by Verma Committee Report (January 2013) recommendations post-Nirbhaya case (December 2012).
  • Acid Attack: Compensation under Manodhairya covers acid attack victims separately — ₹3 lakh minimum (enhanced by SC/HC orders).
  • The scheme predates central schemes like Nirbhaya Fund utilization for survivors.

Maharashtra Connection

The Mukhyamantri Majhi Ladki Bahin Yojana (2024) was announced ahead of the November 2024 Maharashtra assembly elections and is credited with the ruling Mahayuti alliance's victory (winning ~230/288 seats). Karnataka's Gruha Lakshmi (₹2,000/month) was the direct inspiration. Maharashtra also has a strong network of Mahila Bachat Gats (Self-Help Groups) — ~4.5 lakh SHGs linked to Maharashtra State Rural Livelihoods Mission (MSRLM/Umed).

Exam Traps

  • Ladki Bahin ₹1,500 is the Maharashtra scheme; Karnataka's Gruha Lakshmi is ₹2,000 — different amounts, different states.
  • Manodhairya (2013) is a Maharashtra scheme — NOT a central government scheme.
  • POCSO Act (2012) is central law — not Maharashtra-specific, but Maharashtra's implementation (special courts) is distinctive.
