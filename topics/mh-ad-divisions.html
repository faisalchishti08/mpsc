Six revenue divisions (Konkan, Pune, Nashik, Aurangabad, Amravati, Nagpur) PREMAINSINTMH

Overview

Maharashtra is divided into six revenue divisions, each headed by a Divisional Commissioner (Vibhagiya Ayukta). The divisions aggregate multiple districts for administrative supervision and are the intermediate tier between the state government and districts. MPSC tests division names, their HQs, and the districts they contain.

Administrative Hierarchy

The divisional structure operates as: State → Division (Divisional Commissioner) → District (District Collector/DM) → Taluka (Tehsildar) → Village (Gram Panchayat/Patwari)

The Divisional Commissioner:

  • Supervises District Collectors in the division
  • Hears revenue appeals from district-level orders
  • Coordinates inter-district matters (disaster relief, law and order)
  • Cadre: IAS officers (senior level)

Regional Significance

  • Konkan Division includes Mumbai, Thane (most urbanised), Palghar (newest district), Ratnagiri, Sindhudurg (coastal tourism).
  • Aurangabad Division = entire Marathwada — most drought-prone, historically under-developed, integrated into Maharashtra in 1956 from the Nizam's Hyderabad.
  • Amravati + Nagpur Divisions = Vidarbha (11 districts total) — cotton belt; chronic farmer distress region.
  • Nashik Division includes Khandesh (Jalgaon, Dhule, Nandurbar) and Ahmednagar — tribal belt (Nandurbar: highest ST population district).

Maharashtra Connection

The division structure underpins MPSC's own cadre postings — MPSC-selected officers (Class I/II) are posted at the divisional and district level. The Aurangabad (now renamed Chhatrapati Sambhajinagar in 2023) and Osmanabad (renamed Dharashiv in 2023) renames are important for current-affairs MCQs. Vidarbha's 11 districts across two divisions regularly feature in discussions about regional imbalance and separate statehood demands.

Key Facts

  • Total divisions: 6
  • Konkan Division HQ: Mumbai (7 districts — most)
  • Aurangabad Division: 8 districts (most in a division) — all Marathwada
  • Amravati + Nagpur = Vidarbha (11 districts, 2 divisions)
  • Aurangabad renamed Chhatrapati Sambhajinagar (2023)
  • Osmanabad renamed Dharashiv (2023)
  • Divisional Commissioner rank: IAS (senior); also called Vibhagiya Ayukta

Memory Aids

  • K-N-P-A-A-N: Konkan, Nashik, Pune, Aurangabad, Amravati, Nagpur — clockwise from coast
  • "Aurangabad has 8 — Marathwada has all eight"
  • Vidarbha = A + N divisions (Amravati + Nagpur) = 11 districts

Exam Traps

  • Palghar is in Konkan Division (not Nashik) — it was carved from Thane in 2014.
  • Ahmednagar district is in Nashik Division, not Pune Division — a common mix-up.
  • "Aurangabad" in Mains/current papers may now refer to Chhatrapati Sambhajinagar — use the new name in recent context.
