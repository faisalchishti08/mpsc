Hindustani music — gharanas, instruments PREMAINS

Overview

Hindustani classical music is the north Indian classical music tradition, shaped by the synthesis of ancient Indian raga-tala system with Persian-Mughal influences from the 13th century onward. It is centred on improvisation within a raga framework and is organised into gharanas (schools). MPSC tests gharana names, key forms (Dhrupad, Khayal, Thumri), instruments, and celebrity musicians — especially those from Maharashtra.

In-Depth

Historical Development

  • Ancient roots: Sama Veda (musical recitation), Natyashastra (musical theory).
  • Medieval transformation: Amir Khusrau (1253–1325) — court musician of Alauddin Khilji; credited with creating the sitar and tabla (or at least reshaping them) and developing the khayal vocal form; merged Persian and Indian music.
  • Mughal patronage: Tansen at Akbar's court — greatest Dhrupad exponent; established the core raga tradition.
  • Post-Mughal dispersal: musicians moved to Rajput courts, later Lucknow Nawabs, developing distinct gharanas.

Key Instruments

String (Chordophone):

  • Sitar: 18–21 strings; most popular; Ravi Shankar (Padma Vibhushan; Grammy; performed at Woodstock 1969).
  • Sarod: no frets; fingertip pressing; Ali Akbar Khan (Padma Vibhushan).
  • Sarangi: bowed; accompaniment; Ram Narayan brought it to concert status.
  • Santoor: trapezoid dulcimer; Kashmiri; Shivkumar Sharma (Padma Vibhushan).
  • Surbahaar: bass version of sitar.

Percussion (Membranophone):

  • Tabla: paired drums; most common Hindustani percussion; Zakir Hussain (Padma Vibhushan; Grammy 2024 — first Indian classical musician to win a Grammy in a classical category).
  • Pakhawaj: barrel drum used in Dhrupad.

Wind (Aerophone):

  • Bansuri: bamboo transverse flute; Hariprasad Chaurasia (Padma Vibhushan).
  • Shehnai: double-reed oboe; Bismillah Khan (Bharat Ratna 2001).

Maharashtra Connection

Maharashtra is a major Hindustani music centre:

  • Kirana Gharana: Pandit Bhimsen Joshi (1924–2011) — Bharat Ratna 2008; lived in Pune; greatest 20th-c. khayal vocalist; his annual festival Sawai Gandharva Bhimsen Sangeet Mahotsav (December, Pune) is India's premier Hindustani festival.
  • Jaipur-Atrauli Gharana: Kishori Amonkar (1932–2017) — born Mumbai; "Queen of Khayal"; Padma Vibhushan; Sangeet Natak Akademi.
  • Vasantrao Deshpande (1920–1983): Agra gharana; iconic Marathi Natyasangeet singer.
  • Natyasangeet — Marathi musical drama songs — draws from Hindustani classical tradition; composers like Govindrao Tembe and performers like Bal Gandharva defined this uniquely Marathi form.
  • Kaivalyadhama, Lonavala (yoga/music institute): regular Hindustani music programmes.

Key Facts

  • Amir Khusrau (13th c.): sitar, tabla, khayal credited/developed.
  • Tansen: Akbar's court; Dhrupad; Raga Darbari Kanada.
  • Gwalior Gharana: oldest khayal gharana.
  • Bhimsen Joshi: Kirana Gharana; Bharat Ratna 2008; Pune.
  • Ravi Shankar: Sitar; Padma Vibhushan; Grammy.
  • Zakir Hussain: Tabla; Padma Vibhushan; Grammy 2024.
  • Bismillah Khan: Shehnai; Bharat Ratna 2001.
  • Hariprasad Chaurasia: Bansuri; Padma Vibhushan.

Exam Traps

  • Zakir Hussain ≠ Ustad Allah Rakha — father (Allah Rakha) and son (Zakir Hussain) are both tabla legends; questions may conflate them.
  • Bismillah Khan's Bharat Ratna = 2001 (not 1998 or 2008).
  • Kirana Gharana is from both Karnataka (origin, Abdul Karim Khan) and Maharashtra (Bhimsen Joshi, Gangubai Hangal) — state identification questions need care.
