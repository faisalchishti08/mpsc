Paleolithic age — tools and sites (Bhimbetka) PREMAINS

Overview

The Paleolithic Age (Old Stone Age, ~2.5 million – 10,000 BCE) represents the earliest phase of human cultural evolution in India, defined by the use of chipped and flaked stone tools, hunting-gathering subsistence, and nomadic lifestyle. The three sub-phases — Lower, Middle, and Upper Paleolithic — are distinguished by tool technology and increasing sophistication. Bhimbetka (Madhya Pradesh) is the iconic Paleolithic-Mesolithic site in India. MPSC tests specific site names, tool types, and the sequence of prehistoric periods.

Bhimbetka — The Iconic Site

Bhimbetka (Raisen district, Madhya Pradesh) is the most important Paleolithic site in India:

  • Rock shelters continuously occupied from Lower Paleolithic (~5,00,000 BCE) through Mesolithic and into early historical times.
  • Contains over 700 rock shelters; about 400 have paintings.
  • Rock paintings depict hunting scenes, animals (bison, deer, elephant), dancers, and geometric patterns.
  • Paintings date from Upper Paleolithic (~30,000 BCE) onwards through Mesolithic.
  • Discovered by V.S. Wakankar (1957–58) through systematic exploration.
  • Declared UNESCO World Heritage Site in 2003.

Narmada Valley — Hominid Evidence

The Narmada Valley (Madhya Pradesh, Gujarat) has yielded the only significant hominid fossil from India:

  • Narmada Man (Homo erectus? / archaic Homo sapiens): A skullcap found at Hathnora (Sehore district, MP) in 1982 by Arun Sonakia; the fossil dates to ~2,36,000–5,00,000 BP. Exact classification debated; shows features intermediate between Homo erectus and modern humans.
  • This find is important as it suggests human habitation in India predating the "Out of Africa" migrations that settled East/South Asia.

Distribution of Paleolithic Sites in India

  • South India: Attirampakkam (Tamil Nadu) — recently dated to 1.5 million BCE (Acheulean tools), pushing back the timeline.
  • Deccan Plateau: Maharashtra sites — Chirki-on-Pravara (Ahmednagar district), Nevasa, Patne (Jalgaon district, Upper Paleolithic blades).
  • Central India: Narmada Valley, Bhimbetka (MP).
  • East India: Singhbhum (Jharkhand), Mayurbhanj (Odisha).
  • Sites are found near river valleys where quartzite and flint raw materials were available.

Subsistence and Society

  • Paleolithic humans were hunter-gatherers — hunted large animals (wild cattle, elephant, rhinoceros) and gathered roots, berries.
  • No evidence of agriculture, pottery, or permanent settlement.
  • Groups were small, nomadic — moved following animal herds or seasonal food sources.
  • Use of fire — evidence from hearth ash at some sites (though early Indian evidence is sparse).
  • Burial practices: Not well documented in Lower/Middle Paleolithic; some evidence emerges in Upper Paleolithic.

Classification of Stone Ages (India vs. Global)

India's prehistoric terminology follows European models but timelines differ:

  • Paleolithic (Old Stone) = chipped stone, no agriculture.
  • Mesolithic (Middle Stone) = microlithic tools, rock art, early domestication.
  • Neolithic (New Stone) = polished tools, agriculture, pottery, settlements.
  • Note: These stages did not occur simultaneously across India — some regions had overlapping or delayed transitions.

Maharashtra Connection

  • Chirki-on-Pravara (Ahmednagar dist.): One of India's known Lower Paleolithic sites; cleavers and handaxes found in the Pravara river valley.
  • Nevasa (Ahmednagar dist.): Important for Middle Paleolithic flake tools; the site was also later a key Chalcolithic and historical site.
  • Patne (Jalgaon dist.): Upper Paleolithic blade industry; one of the few sites with datable Upper Paleolithic material from the Deccan.
  • These sites were excavated by Deccan College (Pune) researchers, making Maharashtra's role in Indian prehistory research significant.

Key Facts

  • Earliest sub-phase: Lower Paleolithic — handaxe and cleaver.
  • Bhimbetka: Raisen dist., MP; discovered by V.S. Wakankar (1957–58); UNESCO WHS 2003.
  • Narmada Man (Hathnora): found 1982 by Arun Sonakia; ~2.5 lakh years old.
  • Attirampakkam (TN): Acheulean tools dated to ~1.5 million years — oldest confirmed Paleolithic site in India.
  • Maharashtra Paleolithic sites: Chirki (Lower), Nevasa (Middle), Patne (Upper).
  • Key tool types: Handaxe/cleaver (Lower), Flakes/scrapers (Middle), Blades/burins (Upper).
  • Paleolithic people: nomadic, hunter-gatherer; no agriculture, no pottery.

Exam Traps

  • Bhimbetka is in Madhya Pradesh (Raisen district), not Maharashtra — commonly confused because Maharashtra Chalcolithic sites are also known.
  • Bhimbetka rock paintings are from Upper Paleolithic and Mesolithic, NOT the entire 5-lakh-year occupation — the site was occupied earlier but paintings came later.
  • Narmada Man fossil is India's only significant hominid find; do not confuse with hominid fossils from Africa (Lucy, etc.).
  • Paleolithic tools are chipped/flaked, NOT polished — polished tools = Neolithic.
