16 Mahajanapadas and rise of Magadha PREMAINS

Overview

The 16 Mahajanapadas (Great Kingdoms/Republics, ~6th–4th c. BCE) were the large territorial states that emerged across north India during the Later Vedic period's aftermath. They represent the transition from tribal Janapadas to true state systems. From among them, Magadha emerged as the dominant power and eventually became the base for the Mauryan Empire. MPSC asks on names, locations, and the reasons for Magadha's rise to supremacy.

Sanghas and Republics

Not all Mahajanapadas were monarchies — several were oligarchic republics (Ganas/Sanghas):

  • Vajji (Licchavi): Vaishali — most famous republic; assembly (Sangha) of 7,707 Licchavis met to deliberate; Mahavira was born here.
  • Malla: Kushinara and Pava — two separate Malla republics.
  • Shakya: Kapilavastu — small republic; Buddha's clan.
  • Kola, Moriya (Pipphhalivana): Ashoka's ancestor clan.

These republics were eventually absorbed by expanding monarchies, especially Magadha.

Magadha's Dynasties

  1. Haryanka (~544–413 BCE): Bimbisara (543–492 BCE) — diplomatist; married Kosala princess; friendly with Buddha. Ajatashatru (492–460 BCE) — patricide; conquered Vaishali (using Mahasilakantaka/catapult and Rathamusala/chariot); started Buddhist 1st Council.
  2. Shishunaga (~413–345 BCE): Shifted capital to Vaishali (briefly); conquered Avanti — removed last major rival.
  3. Nanda (~345–321 BCE): Mahapadma Nanda ("great Nanda") founded dynasty; first non-Kshatriya (Shudra?) dynasty; massive army (~200,000 infantry, 20,000 cavalry, 3,000 elephants, 2,000 chariots per accounts); Alexander's men refused to fight them; overthrown by Chandragupta Maurya.

Maharashtra Connection

Assaka (Asmaka) Mahajanapada is the only Mahajanapada south of the Vindhyas, located in the Godavari valley — in modern Maharashtra (and parts of Telangana). Its capital was Potana (identified with Boden or Bodhan area near the Godavari). This makes Maharashtra directly connected to the Mahajanapada era. Later, the Satavahana dynasty emerged from this region.

Key Facts

  • 16 Mahajanapadas: listed in Anguttara Nikaya (Buddhist) and Bhagavati Sutra (Jain).
  • Only Mahajanapada south of Vindhyas: Assaka (Godavari valley, Maharashtra).
  • Magadha capital: Rajgriha (early) → Pataliputra (Ajatashatru built; Nanda/Maurya used).
  • Bimbisara: first important Magadha king; 543–492 BCE.
  • Nanda army: ~200,000 infantry — stopped Alexander's eastward march.
  • Vajji/Vaishali: earliest republic in Indian history.
  • Ajatashatru invented: Mahasilakantaka (catapult/stone-hurler) and Rathamusala (scythed chariot).

Exam Traps

  • Assaka/Asmaka is the only Mahajanapada in Maharashtra region — MCQs ask which is south of Vindhyas.
  • Bimbisara was NOT killed by Alexander — he died 50 years before Alexander (492 BCE vs ~326 BCE). Bimbisara was killed by his son Ajatashatru.
  • The Nanda dynasty was founded by Mahapadma Nanda — some accounts say he was of low birth (Shudra); historically significant as a possible first "non-Kshatriya" major dynasty.
  • Pataliputra was founded/fortified by Ajatashatru (Haryanka), not by Bimbisara.
