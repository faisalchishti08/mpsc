Akhil Bharatiya Marathi Sahitya Sammelan PREMAINSINTMH

Overview

The Akhil Bharatiya Marathi Sahitya Sammelan (ABMSS) is the world's largest literary conference in a regional language — held annually, rotating across Maharashtra and occasionally outside the state. It was first convened in 1878 (Pune) under Vishnu Krishna Chiplunkar's inspiration. The Sammelan elects a Sahitya Samrat (Emperor of Literature) as its president. The Maharashtra Sahitya Parishad (MSP), founded 1906 in Pune, coordinates the Sammelan and is the apex institution of Marathi literary life. MPSC tests the first Sammelan (1878), MSP (1906), and notable Sahitya Samrat presidents.

Maharashtra Sahitya Parishad (MSP)

  • Founded: 1906, Pune.
  • Oldest and apex Marathi literary institution.
  • HQ: Sadashiv Peth, Pune (close to Tilak's Kesari office area).
  • Functions: Organises Sammelan; conducts Marathi language examinations (Bhasha Ratna, Bhasha Visharad, Marathi Tyanskhyeti); publishes Marathi dictionaries and references; felicitations; promotes Marathi outside Maharashtra.
  • MSP Awards: Sahitya Samrat award at Sammelan; Chintamanrao Joshi Award; Lokmanya Tilak Sahitya Puraskar.
  • MSP coordinates the giant annual Sammelan — logistics, cultural events, book fair, debates.

Related Marathi Literary Institutions

  • Sahitya Akademi New Delhi: National-level; has a Marathi advisory board; annual Marathi Sahitya Akademi Award.
  • Maharashtra Rajya Marathi Vishwakosh Nirmiti Mandal (Vishwakosh Mandal): Pune; prepares Maharashtra's Marathi Encyclopedia (Vishwakosh) — 20 volumes; government-funded.
  • Bal Bharati (Maharashtra State Bureau of Textbook Production and Curriculum Research): Pune; prepares school textbooks in Marathi for Maharashtra schools.
  • Marathi Vidyapeeth, Wardha: Mahatma Gandhi-inspired institution; Marathi learning for non-native speakers.
  • Maharashtra Sahitya and Sanskrit Mandal: Academic study of classical Marathi + Sanskrit.

Marathi Outside Maharashtra — Sammelan's Role

The ABMSS has been held outside Maharashtra to reach Marathi-speaking populations in:

  • Karnataka (Belgaum/Belagavi + Udupi): Marathi-speaking border villages; ongoing dispute about 865 villages' transfer; Sammelan at Belgaum has political significance.
  • Goa: Large Marathi-speaking (Konkani-Marathi) population; Marathi vs Konkani status debate post-Goa statehood (1987).
  • Diaspora Marathi: ABMSS has had events for Marathi diaspora in USA, UK.

Marathi Border Dispute — Maharashtra-Karnataka

  • Belgaum (Belagavi), Nipani, Karwar and 865 villages: Marathi-speaking areas under Karnataka state; Maharashtra has been demanding transfer since 1956.
  • Mahajan Commission (1967): Bhagwan Sahay Mahajan; recommended some transfer but both states rejected.
  • Supreme Court case: Maharashtra v/s Karnataka; still pending (filed in 2004).
  • Maharashtra Ekikaran Samiti (MES): Organisation fighting for Belgaum merger with Maharashtra.
  • ABMSS held in Belgaum signals Maharashtra's symbolic assertion over these Marathi-speaking areas.

Maharashtra Connection

The Sammelan is not just literary — it's political. Questions of Marathi identity, border disputes with Karnataka, Classical Language status, and the future of Marathi literature in the digital/Hindi/English age are debated here. Kusumagraj's birthday (27 February) as Marathi Bhasha Din and the Sammelan together form the institutional backbone of Marathi linguistic nationalism.
