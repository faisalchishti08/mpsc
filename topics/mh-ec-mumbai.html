Mumbai — financial capital, BSE, RBI PREMAINSINTMH

Overview

Mumbai is India's financial capital and one of Asia's largest financial centres. The city is headquarters to the Reserve Bank of India (RBI, 1935), Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI, 1988), Bombay Stock Exchange (BSE, 1875), and National Stock Exchange (NSE, 1992). Mumbai contributes ~6–7% of India's national GDP, generates ~25% of India's income tax revenue, and ~40% of foreign trade by value. MPSC tests specific institutional facts about these bodies, their location, year of establishment, and powers.

Mumbai's Stock Market — Key Facts

  • BSE SENSEX: Bombay Stock Exchange Sensitive Index; 30 blue-chip stocks; base year 1978–79 = 100.
  • NSE NIFTY 50: National Stock Exchange Fifty; 50 stocks; base value 1000 from November 1995.
  • Dalal Street: Street in Fort, South Mumbai where BSE is located; colloquial term for India's stock market.
  • Bandra-Kurla Complex (BKC): Mumbai's second CBD (Central Business District); NSE, SEBI, LIC HQ, many multinational banks are located here; newer financial hub.

RBI — Key Functions

  1. Monetary policy: Sets Repo Rate, Reverse Repo Rate, CRR, SLR to control inflation and credit flow.
  2. Currency issuance: Issues bank notes (except ₹1 note, which is issued by Ministry of Finance); ₹1 coin/note bears Finance Secretary's signature, not RBI Governor's.
  3. Banker to the Government: Manages Central Govt + State Govt accounts; public debt management.
  4. Regulator: Supervises commercial banks, cooperative banks (apex), NBFCs, payment systems.
  5. Foreign Exchange: Maintains forex reserves; implements FEMA 1999; intervenes in currency markets.
  • RBI Governors from Maharashtra: Numerous distinguished governors; Governor appointed by Central Cabinet, not RBI board — a nuance often tested.

Mumbai — Other Economic Facts

  • Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj International Airport (CSMIA): 2nd busiest airport in India; Terminal 2 opened 2014.
  • Jawaharlal Nehru Port (JNPT/NSICT): Navi Mumbai; India's largest container port (~50–55% of India's container traffic); now also called Jawaharlal Nehru Port Authority (JNPA).
  • India's Diamond Trade: Bharat Diamond Bourse (BDB), BKC Mumbai — India's largest diamond trading complex; India processes ~80–90% of world's cut and polished diamonds (mostly in Surat, but Mumbai is the trading hub).
  • Bollywood: Mumbai is the headquarters of Hindi film industry; film production, distribution, music — major economic sector; Mumbai Film City (Goregaon).
  • GIFT City (Gujarat): Note — GIFT City is in Gujarat, NOT Mumbai; it is a rival financial hub. IFSC (International Financial Services Centre) is at GIFT City (Gandhinagar).

Maharashtra Connection

Mumbai's economic dominance within Maharashtra creates a structural tension — the city contributes massive revenue but also demands massive expenditure on infrastructure. The Brihanmumbai Municipal Corporation (BMC) has the largest municipal budget in India (~₹50,000–55,000 crore). The Mumbai Metropolitan Region Development Authority (MMRDA) coordinates infrastructure planning for Greater Mumbai. Key projects: Mumbai Trans Harbour Link (MTHL, Atal Setu — opened Jan 2024, ~21.8 km, India's longest sea bridge), Metro rail expansion, Coastal Road, Navi Mumbai International Airport.

Exam Traps

  • SEBI was established in 1988 but got statutory power only in 1992 (SEBI Act 1992) — both years tested.
  • IRDAI is in Hyderabad (moved from Delhi), NOT Mumbai.
  • The ₹1 note is issued by the Ministry of Finance (not RBI); the rest are issued by RBI.
  • GIFT City IFSC is in Gujarat — a separate International Financial Services Centre, rival to Mumbai's dominance.
