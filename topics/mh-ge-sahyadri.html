Sahyadri (Western Ghats) and ranges (Satmala, Ajanta, Balaghat) PREMAINSINTMH

Overview

The Sahyadri (Western Ghats) is a mountain range running approximately 1,600 km along India's western coast, of which Maharashtra's portion extends from the Satpura junction in the north (near Nashik) to the Karnataka border in the south. Within Maharashtra, the Sahyadri is complemented by the Satmala, Ajanta, and Balaghat ranges on the Deccan plateau. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site (2012) and one of the world's 8 biodiversity hotspots — making it central to both Maharashtra Geography and Environment questions.

In-Depth

The Main Sahyadri Range

  • The Sahyadri forms the western edge of the Deccan plateau in Maharashtra, acting as the watershed between west-flowing Konkan rivers (into the Arabian Sea) and east-flowing Deccan rivers (Godavari, Krishna, Bhima).
  • Orientation: Runs roughly North-South; steep western escarpment (Konkan side) vs. gentle eastern slope (Deccan side) — classic asymmetric ridge.
  • Passes (Ghats): Major passes connect the Konkan coast to the Deccan plateau: Thal Ghat (NH-61, Nashik), Bhor Ghat (Mumbai-Pune Expressway/railway), Kasara Ghat (Mumbai-Nashik railway), Amba Ghat, Amboli Ghat.

Ecological Significance

  • Biodiversity Hotspot: Western Ghats is one of 36 global biodiversity hotspots (UNESCO declaration). Maharashtra's Sahyadri hosts: Shekru (state animal), Malabar giant squirrel, Indian Wild Dog (Dhole), Leopard, Tiger (in fringe areas), King Cobra, Malabar pit viper.
  • World Heritage Sites in Sahyadri (Maharashtra): Kaas Plateau (Satara district) — "Valley of Flowers of Maharashtra," declared UNESCO World Heritage (2012, as part of Western Ghats serial nomination).
  • Tiger Reserves: Sahyadri Tiger Reserve (Radhanagari-Chandoli corridor, Kolhapur-Satara), Melghat Tiger Reserve (Amravati, Satpura foothills).
  • Important Bird Areas (IBAs): Bhimashankar, Koyna, Radhanagari.
  • Major WLS: Bhimashankar (Pune), Koyna (Satara), Radhanagari (Kolhapur), Nawegaon (Gondia — plateau fringe).

Hydrology

The Sahyadri is the water tower of Maharashtra:

  • West-facing rivers (Konkan): Ulhas, Savitri, Vashishti, Kalu — short, fast, seasonal.
  • East-facing rivers (Deccan): Godavari (Trimbakeshwar source, Nashik), Krishna (Mahabaleshwar, Satara), Bhima (Pune district), Koyna (major Sahyadri river — Koyna Dam, India's largest hydroelectric project).

Maharashtra Connection

The Sahyadri's Kaas Plateau (Satara) draws lakhs of tourists during monsoon (September) for its wildflower bloom. Mahabaleshwar (Satara, ~1,372 m) is Maharashtra's premier hill station — source of Krishna, Venna, Koyna, Gayatri, and Savitri rivers at Panchganga temple (five rivers in one place). Matheran (Raigad) and Lonavala-Khandala (Pune) are other Sahyadri hill stations. The Koyna Dam (Satara) generates ~1,960 MW — Maharashtra's largest power source.

Exam Traps

  • Kalsubai is in Ahmednagar (Akole taluka), NOT Nashik or Pune — common location error.
  • Ajanta Range (geological/structural feature) ≠ Ajanta Caves (UNESCO heritage, Buddhist, in Aurangabad) — totally different things.
  • Sahyadri was declared UNESCO WH in 2012 (not 2010 or 2014).
