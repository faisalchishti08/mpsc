Marathi theatre and cinema PREMAINSINTMH

Overview

Maharashtra has India's oldest film industry (Bollywood/Hindi cinema in Mumbai) and a distinct Marathi cinema and theatre tradition. Dadasaheb Phalke — the "Father of Indian Cinema" — made India's first full-length film Raja Harishchandra (1913) in Nashik/Mumbai; the Dadasaheb Phalke Award (India's highest film award) is named after him. Marathi theatre — Natya Sangeet tradition — predates Bollywood and gave India legends like Bal Gandharva and Dinanath Mangeshkar (father of Lata Mangeshkar). MPSC tests Phalke's film year (1913), Phalke Award, and key Marathi cinema/theatre milestones.

Marathi Theatre — Natya Sangeet Tradition

  • Bal Gandharva (Narayan Shripad Rajhans, 1888–1967): Maharashtra's greatest theatre artist; known for playing female roles with extraordinary femininity; Gandharva Natak Mandali (founded 1913 with Kirloskar Company); songs ("Natya Sangeet") from his plays defined Marathi melodic tradition; Gandhijii called him "Voice of the Nightingale."
  • Dinanath Mangeshkar (1900–1942): Marathi Natya Sangeet singer; father of Lata, Meena, Usha, Hridayanath, Asha Mangeshkar; composer and singer in Marathi theatre; died age 42 (heart failure, poverty).
  • Bhaurao Kolhatkar: Early 20th century; female impersonator in Marathi theatre (Sangeet Manohar).
  • P.L. Deshpande (Pu La or Purushottam Lakerao Deshpande, 1919–2000): "PuLa" — Maharashtra's most beloved humorist; Batatyachi Chawl (play), Vyakti ani Valli (character sketches), Asami Asami; also actor, director, poet; received Maharashtra Bhushan (1999), Sahitya Akademi Fellowship; his death (12 June 2000) was a state mourning moment.

Bollywood's Maharashtra Connection

  • Bollywood = Mumbai: Hindi film industry; centered in Andheri, Juhu, Bandra (Mumbai); Film City at Goregaon.
  • Kolhapur School: V. Shantaram, Baburao Painter — Kolhapur gave India its first generation of filmmakers.
  • National Film Archive of India (NFAI): Pune — India's national film archive; preserves India's film heritage.
  • Film and Television Institute of India (FTII): Pune — India's premier film/TV training institute (on site of former Prabhat Film Company studio).

Maharashtra Connection

FTII (Pune) and NFAI (Pune) make Pune the intellectual capital of Indian cinema even as Mumbai hosts the industry. Nagraj Manjule's Sairat (2016) — a Marathi film about inter-caste romance — grossed over ₹100 crore and sparked a social conversation about caste across India. PuLa Deshpande remains the most-read Marathi prose writer; his characters (Namya, Nana Phadnavis etc.) are household names in Maharashtra.
