Kuchipudi (Andhra Pradesh) PREMAINS

Overview

Kuchipudi is the classical dance-drama tradition of Andhra Pradesh, named after the village of Kuchipudi (Krishna district) where Brahmin families have practised it for centuries. It is unique for combining dance, music, speech, and mime — making it closer to total theatre than pure dance. The distinctive feature of dancing on the rim of a brass plate (tarangam) is its most famous element. MPSC tests state, village origin, brass plate feature, and key exponents.

In-Depth

Origin and History

  • The Kuchipudi village in NTR (Krishna) district, Andhra Pradesh gave the form its name.
  • Traditionally performed by Brahmin men of the village; women were not performers (now they participate fully).
  • Founded in the 17th century by Siddhendra Yogi (a Vaishnava saint) who is credited with composing Bhama Kalapam (a dance-drama on the story of Satyabhama, wife of Krishna) and training the Kuchipudi village Brahmins to perform it.
  • Originally performed as a village theatrical event — Yakshagana-like in its total theatre approach; dialogue in Sanskrit and Telugu.

Bhama Kalapam — Central Composition

The Bhama Kalapam (story of Satyabhama) by Siddhendra Yogi is the core composition of Kuchipudi tradition:

  • The veshagadha (character role): the main character Satyabhama pleads with Krishna not to favour Rukmini; rich in shringara and viraha (separation love).
  • Traditionally only one performer would play Satyabhama in the entire village, passing it down generations.

Contemporary Kuchipudi

  • Vempati Chinna Satyam (1929–2012): most significant figure in modernising Kuchipudi as solo concert art; founded the Kuchipudi Art Academy in Hyderabad; Padma Bhushan; brought it to urban stages.
  • Yamini Krishnamurthy: trained in both Bharatanatyam and Kuchipudi; brought it wider national attention.
  • Raja and Radha Reddy: husband-wife duo; made Kuchipudi internationally famous.

Maharashtra Connection

No direct origin. Andhra-Maharashtra cultural exchange is significant — the Deccan region historically shares Telugu-Marathi cultural boundaries. Kuchipudi is performed in Marathi-Telugu border districts (Marathwada/Vidarbha belt).

Key Facts

  • State: Andhra Pradesh.
  • Named after: Kuchipudi village, NTR (Krishna) district.
  • Founded: 17th century, by Siddhendra Yogi (Bhama Kalapam composition).
  • Traditional performers: Brahmin men of Kuchipudi village.
  • Unique feature: Tarangam — dancing on brass plate rim.
  • Language: Telugu (primarily).
  • Music: Carnatic.
  • Modern consolidator: Vempati Chinna Satyam (Padma Bhushan).

Memory Aids

  • Kuchipudi = Andhra Pradesh + brass plate (tarangam) + Telugu + Siddhendra Yogi.
  • Bhama Kalapam = Satyabhama story + Siddhendra Yogi + the core composition.
  • Tarangam = Ta = toes, Ra = brass, Ngam = balance (aide-mémoire).

Exam Traps

  • Kuchipudi village is in NTR (Krishna) district, NOT Guntur or Visakhapatnam.
  • Siddhendra Yogi founded Kuchipudi — not to be confused with Nandikeshwara (author of Abhinaya Darpana, relevant to Bharatanatyam).
  • Tarangam (brass plate) is Kuchipudi's unique feature — not Bharatanatyam or Odissi.
