Jainism — Mahavira, doctrines, councils PREMAINS

Overview

Jainism is one of India's oldest surviving religions, founded (or more accurately, revived and given final form) by Vardhamana Mahavira (599–527 BCE), the 24th and last Tirthankara. Jainism rejects Vedic authority, caste hierarchy, and sacrifice; its core doctrines of Ahimsa (non-violence), Anekantavada (non-absolutism), and Aparigraha (non-possession) remain influential globally. MPSC tests on Mahavira's life, the five vows, the four noble truths of Jainism, the three jewels, Jain councils, and the split into Digambara/Svetambara sects.

In-Depth

Vardhamana Mahavira — Life

  • Born: ~599 BCE at Vaishali (modern Bihar) — but date is debated; Jain tradition says 599 BCE, some scholars say 540–468 BCE.
  • Family: Kshatriya clan — Licchavi republican family; father Siddhartha (Kshatriya chief), mother Trishala (sister of Licchavi chief Chetaka).
  • Left home at 30 (after parents' death) for 12 years of intense austerity.
  • Attained Kevala Jnana (omniscience/enlightenment) at 42, under a Shal tree, on the bank of the Rijupalika river near Vaishali (or Jrimbhikagrama).
  • Called Mahavira (Great Hero) and Jina (Conqueror — source of "Jainism").
  • Also known as Nirgrantha (free from bonds).
  • Died: 527 BCE at Pavapuri (Bihar) — attained Nirvana (liberation).

Mahavira and the 23rd Tirthankara — Parshvanatha

Mahavira was not the founder but the 24th Tirthankara (ford-maker/teacher). The 23rd Tirthankara, Parshvanatha, lived ~250 years before Mahavira (~8th–7th c. BCE) and had already established a monastic order. Parshvanatha prescribed four vows (Chaturyama): non-violence, non-lying, non-stealing, non-possession. Mahavira added a fifth vow: Brahmacharya (celibacy) — making it Panchashila (five vows).

For lay Jains, there are Anuvratas (lesser vows) — modified, more practical versions of the same five.

Three Jewels (Triratna) of Jainism

  1. Samyak Darshana (Right Faith/Vision) — correct perception of reality.
  2. Samyak Jnana (Right Knowledge) — correct knowledge of Jain metaphysics.
  3. Samyak Charitra (Right Conduct) — right behaviour, especially five vows.

Together, the Triratna lead to moksha (liberation from the cycle of birth and death).

Key Doctrines

  • Anekantavada ("many-sidedness"): Reality is complex and can only be fully known from multiple perspectives; no single viewpoint captures the whole truth. Expressed through Syadvada (conditional predication — "maybe/in some sense") and Saptabhangi (sevenfold predication).
  • Karma in Jainism: Karma is a subtle physical matter that adheres to the soul due to mental, verbal, and physical activities. Liberation = shedding all karma. This is different from Hindu/Buddhist karma concepts.
  • Transmigration: Soul (Jiva) is trapped in Samsara by karma; liberation = Moksha (Mukti/Nirvana).
  • Ahimsa as supreme: Jains are strict vegetarians; many avoid root vegetables (carrot, onion, etc.) as uprooting kills the plant; monks walk carefully to avoid stepping on insects; cover mouth to avoid inhaling micro-organisms.

Maharashtra Connection

Jainism has a deep and long history in Maharashtra:

  • Ellora Caves: Of the 34 caves, caves 30–34 are Jain (9th–11th c. CE) — featuring large Jain Tirthankara sculptures.
  • Shravanabelagola is in Karnataka, but the Deccan route passed through Maharashtra; Jain merchants (Sheth community) controlled much of Maharashtra's trade historically.
  • Akola, Osmanabad (Dharashiv): Jain temples and historical presence.
  • The Jain community is concentrated in Mumbai, Pune, and trade towns — significant economic influence; Maharashtra's Jain population (~1.3% of state) has outsized economic weight.
  • Mahavira Jayanti is an official state holiday in Maharashtra.

Key Facts

  • Mahavira: born ~599 BCE, Vaishali; attained Kevala Jnana at 42; died at Pavapuri (~527 BCE).
  • 24th Tirthankara; 23rd = Parshvanatha.
  • Added Brahmacharya (5th vow) to Parshvanatha's four.
  • Three Jewels: Samyak Darshana, Samyak Jnana, Samyak Charitra.
  • 1st Jain Council: Pataliputra, ~300 BCE (Sthulabhadra).
  • 2nd Jain Council: Vallabhi, ~512 CE (Devardhi Gani).
  • Jain Ellora Caves: 30–34 (out of 34 total caves).

Exam Traps

  • Mahavira was NOT the founder of Jainism — he was the 24th Tirthankara; Parshvanatha preceded him.
  • The 5th vow (Brahmacharya) was added by Mahavira, not present in Parshvanatha's teaching.
  • Jain Councils: 1st at Pataliputra (~300 BCE), 2nd at Vallabhi (~512 CE) — don't confuse with Buddhist councils.
  • Jain Nirvana/Moksha ≠ Buddhist Nirvana — in Jainism it means the soul is freed from karma and exists eternally in perfect bliss (not the "blowing out" Buddhist concept).
