MPSC — composition and functions PREMAINSINTMH

Overview

The Maharashtra Public Service Commission (MPSC) is a constitutional body under Article 315 of the Constitution, established to recruit officers for various Maharashtra state services through competitive examinations. It is the institution that MPSC aspirants are directly engaging with — knowing its composition, functions, independence, and the exams it conducts is essential for every aspirant.

Composition of MPSC

  • Chairman (Adhyaksha): 1 person; appointed by Governor.
  • Members: Currently 4–6 members (the number is not fixed constitutionally; varies by state act/rules).
  • Tenure: 6 years or till 62 years of age, whichever is earlier.
  • Removal: Only by the President (of India) on reference to Supreme Court inquiry; Governor can suspend pending inquiry; can also be removed by Governor for specific misconduct (insolvency, paid employment outside, unfit for office).
  • Independence: Salary charged to Consolidated Fund of State (not subject to annual vote of legislature — ensures independence).

Functions of MPSC (Article 320)

  1. Recruitment: Conducting competitive examinations for state civil services (Class I, II, III posts).
  2. Direct recruitment: When state wants to recruit on merit basis for specific posts.
  3. Promotion/transfer: Advisory role on promotions/transfers of officers in certain grades.
  4. Disciplinary matters: Consultation required before an officer is dismissed, reduced in rank, etc.
  5. Constitution of services: If there is doubt about method of recruitment.
  6. Matters referred by Governor: Any matter the Governor chooses to refer.

Note: MPSC functions are advisory — the state government may accept or reject recommendations, but must report to the legislature if it rejects MPSC advice (Article 323).

Maharashtra Rajyaseva Pariksha — Structure

  • Preliminary Examination: Objective type (100 marks General Studies + 100 marks CSAT = 200 marks); qualifying nature.
  • Mains Examination: 6 papers (General Studies 1-4, Marathi/English language papers, optional); each 100-150 marks; total ~800 marks.
  • Interview: 100 marks (for shortlisted candidates from Mains).
  • Posts: Deputy Collector, Deputy SP (Police), BDO, CEO, Tahsildar, Sales Tax Inspector, Assistant Commissioner (various depts.), State Excise Officer, etc.

MPSC Headquarters

  • Location: MPSC Headquarters, 68, MSDM Road (formerly CST Road), Bandra (East), Mumbai.

Maharashtra Connection

MPSC is the institution aspirants interact with — its composition, constitutional basis, independence (SC inquiry for removal, salary from Consolidated Fund), and advisory nature are tested in the exam conducted by MPSC itself. A functional irony. The annual MPSC report tabled in the legislature is a transparency mechanism; it often reveals vacancy positions and fill-rates.
