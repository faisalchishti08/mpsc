Mughal art, architecture, painting PREMAINS

Overview

Mughal art, architecture and painting represent the high point of Indo-Islamic cultural synthesis, combining Persian, Central Asian, and indigenous Indian traditions under imperial patronage from Babur to Aurangzeb. The architectural zenith came under Shah Jahan (Taj Mahal, Red Fort) while miniature painting flourished under Akbar and Jahangir. MPSC asks about key monuments, artistic styles, and their patrons.

In-Depth

Evolution of Mughal Architecture

Phase 1 — Akbar's Red Sandstone Era (1556–1605): Red sandstone with white marble accents; synthesis of Hindu and Islamic styles; massive scale:

  • Agra Fort (1565–73): red sandstone; Akbar's military fort palace.
  • Fatehpur Sikri (1571–85): new capital near Agra; masterpiece of Akbar's court — includes:
  • Buland Darwaza (1602): 54 m high; built to commemorate Gujarat victory.
  • Diwan-i-Khas (Hall of Private Audience): central pillar with radiating brackets.
  • Panch Mahal: 5-storey wind tower; Indian chatri (kiosk) + Islamic screens.
  • Jama Masjid: contains the Dargah of Sufi saint Salim Chishti (white marble tomb amid red sandstone).
  • Humayun's Tomb (1570, Delhi) — technically completed under Akbar; first garden tomb (charbagh) in India; red sandstone + white marble double dome; UNESCO WHS.

Pietra Dura (Parchin-kari): precious and semi-precious stone (lapis lazuli, cornelian, jade) inlaid in white marble — Shah Jahan's signature decorative innovation, perfected at the Taj Mahal.

Charbagh garden design: four-part formal garden with central water channel (Persian tradition); used at Humayun's Tomb and Taj Mahal.

Mughal Miniature Painting

Mughal painting fused Persian Safavid (Tabriz school) and Indian (Jain manuscript) traditions:

EmperorCharacteristicsKey Artists / Works
HumayunIntroduced Persian artists; founding schoolMir Sayyid Ali, Abdus Samad (Persian masters)
AkbarLarge-scale workshop; narrative scenes; Indian elementsDaswant, Basawan; Hamzanama (1400 folios, 14 years)
JahangirFinest naturalism; portraiture; birds/flowers; European influenceUstad Mansur (birds/animals), Abul Hasan, Bishandas
Shah JahanFormal, stiff; calligraphy as artMuhammad Faqirullah Khan
AurangzebCourt painting declined; regional schools dispersedDispersal to Rajput/Pahari schools

Hamzanama (Story of Hamza): largest illustrated manuscript commission; 12 volumes, ~1,400 paintings on cloth; commissioned by Akbar; painted by ~100 artists over 14 years (c. 1562–77).

Jahangir's naturalism: his court painter Ustad Mansur painted the Siberian Crane, Bengal florican, zebra, turkey with scientific accuracy — sometimes called the "Mughal Audubon."

Music

  • Tansen (court of Akbar, one of the Navaratnas) — master of Dhrupad style; credited with creating Ragas Darbari Kanada, Miyan ki Todi, Miyan ki Malhar.
  • Akbar's court was an active centre for Dhrupad.
  • Aurangzeb: banned court music (sama); his personal musician Khushhal Khan reportedly buried his instruments in protest — but Aurangzeb allegedly played the veena privately.

Literature

  • Baburnama (Babur): Chaghatai Turkic; autobiography.
  • Akbarnama + Ain-i-Akbari (Abul Fazl): Persian; official chronicle + administrative gazetteer of Akbar's empire.
  • Tuzuk-i-Jahangiri (Jahangir): Persian memoirs.
  • Akbar's court: translations of Sanskrit texts into Persian — Mahabharata (as Razmnama), Ramayana, Atharva Veda.

Maharashtra Connection

Bibi Ka Maqbara, Aurangabad (1660) — built by Aurangzeb's son Azam Shah for his mother Dilras Banu Begum (Aurangzeb's wife); often called the "Taj of the Deccan" or "Taj of the Poor" — a deliberate imitation of the Taj Mahal but in lime plaster over brick with only a small marble area. Located in present Maharashtra. Also Panchakki (watermill mosque complex) in Aurangabad.

Key Facts

  • Fatehpur Sikri: 1571–85, near Agra; Akbar's capital.
  • Buland Darwaza (1602): 54 m; commemorates Gujarat conquest.
  • Humayun's Tomb (1570): first charbagh garden tomb; double dome.
  • Taj Mahal: 1632–1653; Ustad Ahmad Lahori (architect); white marble from Makrana.
  • Pietra dura (parchin-kari): Shah Jahan's architectural signature.
  • Hamzanama: ~1,400 paintings, 14 years, under Akbar.
  • Tansen: Dhrupad master, Akbar's court; Raga Darbari Kanada.
  • Ustad Mansur: Jahangir's nature painter.
  • Bibi Ka Maqbara: Aurangabad (Maharashtra), 1660.

Memory Aids

  • Akbar = Red stone + Fatehpur Sikri + Hamzanama + Tansen.
  • Jahangir = Miniature naturalism + Ustad Mansur + Tuzuk.
  • Shah Jahan = White marble + Pietra dura + Taj (1632–53).
  • Humayun's Tomb = "First charbagh" → Taj Mahal = "perfected charbagh".

Exam Traps

  • Buland Darwaza is at Fatehpur Sikri, not Agra Fort — common confusion.
  • Taj Mahal architect: Ustad Ahmad Lahori (not Isa Khan or Ustad Isa which are popular internet myths).
  • Tansen ≠ Baiju Bawra — both are famous musicians but only Tansen was in Akbar's court.
  • Bibi Ka Maqbara is in Aurangabad (Maharashtra), not Agra — different from Taj Mahal but inspired by it.
