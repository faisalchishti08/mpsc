Musical instruments — string, wind, percussion PREMAINS

Overview

Musical instruments of India are classified by the ancient Natyashastra system into four categories (Chaturvidha Vadya): Tata (string), Sushira (wind), Avanaddha (membrane/drum), and Ghana (solid/idiophone). MPSC tests: instrument–performer linkages (Ravi Shankar/sitar, Zakir Hussain/tabla), instrument–tradition linkages (Mridangam=Carnatic, Tabla=Hindustani), and the classification system. Maharashtra instruments (Dholak, Tuntune, Ghumat, Tasha) also appear.

Maharashtra-Specific Instruments

  • Tuntune: single-string; Shahir tradition; used in Powada and Lavani accompaniment.
  • Tasha (Tashe): kettledrum used in Maharashtra dhol-tasha bands; Ganeshotsav processions.
  • Dhol-Tasha: iconic combination in Maharashtra festivals; associated with Shivajayanti, Ganeshotsav.
  • Lezim: not an instrument per se — a rhythmic exercise using a short rope with bells; Ganeshotsav cultural activity.
  • Ghumat: clay drum with lizard-skin membrane; traditional in Goa (Goan folk music); used in Shigmo festival.

Idiophones (Ghana Vadya)

  • Manjira/Taal: small brass cymbals; Bhajans, Kirtan.
  • Kartal: wooden clappers with metal discs; Rajasthani bhakti (Meera Bai's instrument).
  • Ghungroo: ankle bells; worn by classical dancers (not an instrument per se but classified Ghana).
  • Morchang/Morsing: jaw harp; placed against teeth; vibrated; Rajasthan + Carnatic.

Maharashtra Connection

  • Dhol-Tasha: signature percussion ensemble of Maharashtra festivals; Ganeshotsav (Tilak 1893) transformed it into a public cultural event.
  • Tuntune: the Marathi Shahir's instrument; associated with Powada and Lavani tradition; single-string folk instrument.
  • Dholak: used in Tamasha performances (Lavani dance-music).
  • Maharashtra's instrument tradition spans classical (Pandit Bhimsen Joshi used Tanpura accompaniment), folk (Tasha, Dholak), and ritual (Dhol at temple processions).
  • Hariprasad Chaurasia (Bansuri/flute): though from UP, spent formative years in Odisha and later Mumbai; concerts regularly held in Pune/Mumbai.

Key Facts

  • 4 Natyashastra categories: Tata (string), Sushira (wind), Avanaddha (membrane), Ghana (solid).
  • Sitar: Ravi Shankar; Bharat Ratna 1999; Grammy.

Memory Aids

  • 4 Vadya types: "T-S-A-G" = Tata (string), Sushira (wind), Avanaddha (drum), Ghana (solid)".
  • Celebrity instrument map: "Sitar=Ravi Shankar, Sarod=Ali Akbar, Tabla=Zakir, Bansuri=Chaurasia, Shehnai=Bismillah, Santoor=Shivkumar, Sarangi=Ram Narayan".

Exam Traps

  • Santoor is a string instrument (struck hammered dulcimer) — NOT percussion.
  • Ghatam (clay pot) is technically Georgia Vadya but often classified under Avanaddha in modern exams.
  • Violin is Western origin but is a major Carnatic accompaniment instrument.
  • Morchang = jaw harp = Ghana Vadya (solid idiophone) — not a wind instrument.
