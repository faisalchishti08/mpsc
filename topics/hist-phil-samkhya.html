Samkhya PREMAINS

Overview

Samkhya is considered the oldest of India's six orthodox philosophical schools, attributed to the sage Kapila and systematised in Ishvarakrishna's Samkhyakarika (~4th c. CE). It is a dualistic, atheistic system holding that reality consists of two eternal, independent principles: Purusha (pure consciousness/spirit) and Prakriti (primordial matter). Liberation comes from the discrimination (viveka) between these two. Samkhya forms the philosophical basis for the Yoga school. MPSC tests it primarily in the context of all six schools.

In-Depth

Core Dualism: Purusha and Prakriti

PrincipleNatureCharacteristics
PurushaConsciousness/SpiritEternal, inactive, unchanging, multiple (each being has its own Purusha); pure witness; never evolves
PrakritiMatter/NatureEternal, active, evolving, unconscious; single universal substance; source of all material existence

Prakriti's Gunas (Three Qualities): All matter (Prakriti) is made of three qualities (gunas) in varying proportions:

  • Sattva (purity, clarity, lightness) — responsible for intelligence, pleasure.
  • Rajas (passion, activity, restlessness) — responsible for action, pain.
  • Tamas (inertia, heaviness, darkness) — responsible for dullness, ignorance.

In the state of equilibrium, the three gunas are balanced = Prakriti in rest. When disturbed (by Purusha's proximity), evolution begins.

Evolution (Parinama) of Prakriti — 24 tattvas (principles) evolve from Prakriti:

Prakriti → Mahat/Buddhi (cosmic intelligence) → Ahamkara (ego) → Manas (mind) + 5 Jnanendriyas (sense organs: eyes, ears, nose, tongue, skin) + 5 Karmendriyas (action organs: hands, feet, voice, reproductive, excretory) + 5 Tanmatras (subtle elements: sound, touch, form, taste, smell) + 5 Mahabhutas (gross elements: ether, air, fire, water, earth).

Total: 24 evolutes + Prakriti (the unevolved) + Purusha = 25 tattvas in Samkhya.

Atheism in Samkhya

Samkhya does not accept God (Ishvara) as a necessary entity:

  • Prakriti is itself self-evolving (no external creator needed).
  • Purusha is passive (cannot be a creator).
  • However, Samkhya accepts the authority of the Vedas (making it astika/orthodox), distinguishing it from Buddhism/Jainism.

This makes Samkhya unique among orthodox schools for being atheistic while still accepting Vedic authority.

Liberation (Moksha)

  • Suffering arises from the false identification of Purusha with Prakriti (the soul thinking "I am the body/mind").
  • Liberation = vivekakhyati (discriminative knowledge) — realising Purusha is distinct from and unaffected by Prakriti.
  • On liberation, Prakriti ceases to evolve for that Purusha; the soul rests in its pure consciousness.

Relation to Yoga School

Yoga (Patanjali) accepts Samkhya's metaphysical framework (Purusha-Prakriti dualism, 25 tattvas) but adds:

  • God (Ishvara) as a special Purusha (unlike ordinary Purushas — never entangled in Prakriti).
  • Ashtanga Yoga (eightfold path) as the practical method for achieving viveka.

Maharashtra Connection

Not directly Maharashtra-specific. Samkhya philosophy had influence on the Bhagavad Gita (which integrates Samkhya dualism with devotional theism) — and the Gita's teaching is central to Maharashtra's Warkari bhakti tradition (Dnyaneshwari is Marathi commentary on the Gita, engaging with Samkhya-Yoga concepts).

Key Facts

  • Founder: Kapila (attributed; semi-legendary).
  • Text: Samkhyakarika by Ishvarakrishna (~4th c. CE).
  • Dualism: Purusha (consciousness) + Prakriti (matter).
  • Three gunas: Sattva, Rajas, Tamas.
  • Total tattvas: 25 (Prakriti + 23 evolutes + Purusha).
  • Samkhya is atheistic (no God) but orthodox (accepts Vedas).
  • Liberation = vivekakhyati (discriminative knowledge).
  • Paired with: Yoga.

Memory Aids

Exam Traps

  • Samkhya is atheistic but still orthodox (accepts Vedas) — unique position; Buddhists/Jains are also atheistic but nastika (reject Vedas).
  • Purusha is MULTIPLE in Samkhya (each being has its own Purusha) — unlike Vedanta where Brahman is single/universal.
  • 25 tattvas = 24 evolutes + Purusha (Prakriti is the unevolved mother-substance, not counted separately in some lists; check question context).
