Bordering states and geographical location PREMAINSINTMH

Overview

Maharashtra is located in west-central India between latitudes 15°35'N and 22°2'N and longitudes 72°36'E and 80°54'E. It shares borders with six states and one union territory, plus the Arabian Sea on its western coast. Knowing Maharashtra's bordering entities — states, UTs, and coastline — is a standard MPSC map-based Prelims question.

Geographical Location

  • Maharashtra lies on the western coast of India (Konkan) and extends inland across the Deccan plateau.
  • Northern boundary: roughly follows the Satpura and Ajanta ranges.
  • Eastern boundary: heavily forested (Vidarbha, bordering Chhattisgarh and Telangana).
  • Southern boundary: Karnataka border dispute (Belgaum/Belagavi, Karwar, Nipani, Bidar districts claimed by Maharashtra on linguistic grounds — unresolved; Supreme Court case pending).
  • Coastal length: approximately 720 km along the Arabian Sea.

Border Disputes

  • Maharashtra–Karnataka Border Dispute: Maharashtra claims parts of Karnataka (Belgaum/Belagavi, Karwar, Bidar, Nipani, Khanapur) on the basis of Marathi-speaking population. This stems from the 1960 linguistic reorganisation. The Mahajan Commission (1967) awarded these areas to Karnataka; Maharashtra rejected the award. The case remains in the Supreme Court (filed 2004).

Maharashtra Connection

The Karnataka border dispute is a live political issue and a favourite MPSC Mains topic. The unresolved nature — Mahajan Commission (1967) rejected by Maharashtra — is a classic "commission recommended X but state rejected" question. The UT of Dadra & Nagar Haveli is tucked within Maharashtra's coastal belt, a map-based trick question.

Key Facts

  • Maharashtra borders 6 states: Gujarat, MP, Chhattisgarh, Telangana, Andhra Pradesh (small), Karnataka, Goa
  • 1 Union Territory: Dadra & Nagar Haveli and Daman & Diu (DD)
  • Coastline: ~720 km (Arabian Sea, western coast)
  • Latitude: 15°35'N – 22°2'N; Longitude: 72°36'E – 80°54'E
  • Border dispute with Karnataka over Belgaum (Belagavi), Karwar, Nipani areas
  • Mahajan Commission (1967): awarded disputed areas to Karnataka; Maharashtra rejected
  • Gadchiroli borders both Chhattisgarh and Andhra Pradesh (small segment)

Exam Traps

  • Maharashtra does NOT border Rajasthan — a common distractor.
  • Andhra Pradesh (not just Telangana) also has a very small border with Maharashtra (via Gadchiroli) — both may appear as options.
  • The UT Dadra & Nagar Haveli is ENCLOSED within Maharashtra's northern coastal region, not a separate coastal entity.
