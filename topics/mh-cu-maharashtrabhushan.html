Maharashtra Bhushan and state awards PREMAINSINTMH

Overview

Maharashtra Bhushan is the State of Maharashtra's highest civilian award — given annually since 1996 to individuals who have made exceptional contributions in any field. The award carries a citation and a cash prize of ₹25 lakh (enhanced from time to time). Maharashtra also gives annual state awards for literature (Maharashtra Sahitya Puraskars), folk arts (Vithabai Award), sports (Shiv Chhatrapati Sports Award), and social service. MPSC tests the Maharashtra Bhushan recipients, year, and the fields for which the award was given.

Maharashtra Shiv Chhatrapati Sports Award

  • Maharashtra's highest sports award; given annually since 1974 (one of India's oldest state sports awards).
  • Named after Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj.
  • Given in multiple categories (cricket, kabaddi, wrestling, athletics, hockey, weightlifting, etc.).
  • Recipients receive certificate + trophy + cash prize from state government.
  • Notable recipients: Sunil Gavaskar, Sachin Tendulkar, Milind Soman (early career), P.V. Sindhu was not from MH but received national counterpart.

Vithabai Narayangaonkar Award

  • Maharashtra state award for Lavani / folk arts performers.
  • Named after Vithabai Narayangaonkar — legendary Lavani performer from Narayangaon (Pune).
  • Recognises lifelong contributions in Tamasha, Lavani, Powada, Kirtan, and allied folk arts.

Maharashtra Sahitya Purskar and Kabir Puraskar

  • Maharashtra Foundation Awards (USA-based; for literature + social work by Maharashtra figures).
  • Kabir Puraskar: State award for writers working to promote social harmony and communal amity.

Anna Hazare and Ralegan Siddhi

  • Anna Hazare (Kisan Baburao Hazare): Received Maharashtra Bhushan (2011).
  • Ralegan Siddhi (Ahmednagar): Model village transformation; watershed development; banning liquor; community work from 1970s.
  • Jan Lokpal Movement (2011): Fast at Jantar Mantar, Delhi; mobilized anti-corruption movement; inspired RTI movement (1997 MH).
  • Padma Bhushan 1992 (earlier award from central govt).

Sindhutai Sapkal — Special Note

  • Sindhutai Sapkal (1948–2022): Called "Mai" (Mother) by thousands; raised 1,400+ orphaned children from across India; despite her own poverty, ran several rehabilitation homes; Maharashtra Bhushan 2020; Padma Shri 2022 (died 4 January 2022; award announced posthumously).

Maharashtra Connection

The Maharashtra Bhushan captures the state's social ecology — from Pandit Bhimsen Joshi (classical music) to Anna Hazare (village development) to Sindhutai Sapkal (child welfare). The award's breadth across music, cricket, social reform, and medicine reflects Maharashtra's pluralistic excellence. Maharashtra's state sports award (Shiv Chhatrapati) predates the national Rajiv Gandhi Khel Ratna (now Dhyan Chand Khel Ratna) by several decades.
