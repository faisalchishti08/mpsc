Maratha forts and naval power (Kanhoji Angre) PREMAINSMH

Overview

Maratha forts and naval power were central to Shivaji's military strategy and the survival of the Maratha state against both the Mughals and European sea-powers. Shivaji controlled ~300 forts along the Sahyadri (Western Ghats) and the Konkan coast and built India's first organised naval force. Kanhoji Angre (Kanhoji Angria) was the most powerful Maratha admiral who dominated the western sea until his death (1729). Very high MPSC MH frequency.

In-Depth

Strategic Importance of Forts

Shivaji's military doctrine rested on forts as the backbone of the kingdom — "the fort is the treasury of the country". Key functions:

  • Supply bases and refuges during guerrilla warfare.
  • Revenue collection points; stored grain and treasure.
  • Controlled mountain passes (ghats) connecting the Deccan plateau to the Konkan coast.
  • Psychological centres of Swarajya (each recaptured or built fort symbolised sovereignty).

Key Forts

Raigad (Sahyadri, Raigad district): Shivaji's capital from 1674 (after his coronation); located on a 820 m plateau; seat of Maratha sovereignty; captured by Aurangzeb in 1689.

Torna (Pune district): First fort captured by Shivaji in 1646 (age ~16); the starting point of Swarajya.

Pratapgad (Satara district): Site of the Afzal Khan episode (1659); Bhavani temple here; now a major heritage site.

Sinhagad (Pune district): Originally Kondana; captured from Mughals by Tanaji Malusare in 1670 in a night assault (Tanaji died in the battle — Shivaji's famous lament: "Gad aala, pan sinha gela" — "the fort came, but the lion is gone"). Rajaram died here (1700). Now a popular tourist fort near Pune.

Sindhudurg (coastal fort, Malvan): Built by Shivaji in 1664–67 on a sea island; key naval base; now a heritage monument in coastal Maharashtra.

Vijaydurg: Oldest fort on the Konkan coast (originally Gheria); Maratha naval headquarters under Kanhoji Angre.

Janjira (Murud-Janjira): Held by the Siddi (Habshi) community — Abyssinian sea-power; Shivaji never captured this fort despite multiple attempts; remains a famous unconquered fort.

Naval Power — Shivaji's Innovation

Shivaji recognised the importance of sea power for the Konkan coast:

  • Established a navy in 1657–58 using ships built at the Kalyan and Bhiwandi shipyards.
  • Fleet included grabs (large warships), gallivats (smaller), and mazuas (rowing boats).
  • First Indian ruler to build an organised navy as a state institution (not just pirates or trading escorts).
  • Appointed Daryasaranga (Admiral) of the navy; later Mayanakkar as naval chief.
  • The Siddi of Janjira and the Portuguese remained the main challengers; the English (EIC) and Dutch also operated from Bombay.

Kanhoji Angre (Kanhoji Angria, 1669–1729)

Kanhoji Angre was the greatest Maratha admiral and the most powerful sea captain on the western Indian coast in the early 18th century:

  • Appointed Sarkhel (Admiral) by Shahu (c. 1698); headquarters at Vijaydurg (Gheria).
  • Dominated the sea from Bombay to Vengurla; all European ships paid him tribute or faced seizure.
  • Captured English ships repeatedly (including EIC ships and those of the Siddis of Janjira); British attacks on Vijaydurg failed (1710, 1718, 1721, 1722).
  • Portuguese and British launched multiple joint naval expeditions against him — all failed; Angre remained undefeated until his death in 1729.
  • Europens called him a "pirate" but he was exercising legitimate Maratha sovereignty over coastal waters.
  • After his death, his sons fought over succession; British and Portuguese united and destroyed the Angre base at Vijaydurg in 1756 (under Admiral Watson and Clive) — ending Maratha sea power.

Maharashtra Connection

Entirely Maharashtra-focused:

  • Nearly all key Maratha forts are in Maharashtra — Pune, Satara, Kolhapur, Raigad, Sindhudurg, Ratnagiri districts.
  • Sinhagad (Pune) — popular heritage + tourism site; Tanaji Malusare's heroic capture.
  • Murud-Janjira (Raigad) — the famous unconquered sea fort; tourist destination.
  • Sindhudurg (Malvan) — Shivaji's sea fort; UNESCO tentative list.
  • Vijaydurg (Sindhudurg district) — Kanhoji Angre's headquarters.
  • Maharashtra has ~350+ forts; the Maharashtra Forts Conservation Society campaigns for their protection.

Key Facts

  • Shivaji's capital fort: Raigad (coronation 1674).
  • Afzal Khan episode fort: Pratapgad (1659).
  • Tanaji Malusare captured: Sinhagad (Kondana), 1670.
  • Coastal fort built by Shivaji: Sindhudurg (Malvan, 1664–67).
  • Shivaji never captured Janjira (held by the Siddis).
  • Kanhoji Angre: Sarkhel (Admiral); HQ Vijaydurg; dominated 1698–1729.

Exam Traps

  • Janjira was NOT a Maratha fort — it belonged to the Siddis (Habshis); Shivaji failed to capture it.
  • Kanhoji Angre was NOT a pirate (in the Maratha context) — European label; he was the legitimate Maratha Sarkhel.
  • Sinhagad's original name was Kondana — "Sinhagad" name given after Tanaji's conquest.
  • Tanaji Malusare died at Sinhagad — Shivaji's lament: "Gad aala pan sinha gela" — test of translation.
