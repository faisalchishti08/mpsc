Major sites — Harappa, Mohenjo-daro, Lothal, Dholavira, Kalibangan PREMAINS

Overview

The major Harappan sites — Harappa, Mohenjo-daro, Lothal, Dholavira, Kalibangan, Rakhigarhi — are individually distinctive despite sharing a common Harappan urban grammar. MPSC regularly asks: which feature is unique to which site, in which state, and who excavated it. This is a high-yield factual topic where the district/state matters and unique features are the standard MCQ trap.

Famous Artefacts by Site

  • Mohenjo-daro: "Dancing Girl" (bronze, ~10.5 cm, depicts dancing female figure with bangles); "Priest-King" (soapstone bust with cloak and headband); Pashupati Seal; Unicorn seals.
  • Harappa: Terracotta female figurine; red jasper male torso (possible dancing figure).
  • Lothal: Terracotta ship model; bead workshop kiln.
  • Dholavira: Large script signboard (10 large signs on wood, discovered 1990); stadium/ground.
  • Rakhigarhi: DNA analysed ancient remains (2019 Nature study — suggests ancestors did NOT have Steppe-related ancestry, supporting "Ancient Ancestral South Indians" [AASI] theory).

Rakhigarhi DNA Study (2019)

A landmark 2019 Nature paper (Shinde, Reich et al.) analysed DNA from Rakhigarhi skeletons (Mature Harappan period):

  • Found NO evidence of Steppe Aryan gene flow in the Harappan-period skeletons.
  • Suggests the IVC population were largely descended from early South Asian hunter-gatherers and Iranian farmers — not related to later Indo-Aryan migrants.
  • Supports the view that Indo-Aryan migration came after the Harappan urban decline (post-1900 BCE).
  • This finding is contested politically but scientifically significant for understanding India's genetic and cultural history.

Maharashtra Connection

Daimabad (Ahmednagar district) is the southernmost significant Harappan-phase site — represents the southernmost reach of Harappan material culture into the Deccan. The famous Daimabad bronzes (now at Mumbai's Prince of Wales Museum) are the most spectacular artefacts from this interface zone.

Key Facts

  • Harappa: 1921, D.R. Sahni; coffin burials.
  • Mohenjo-daro: 1922, R.D. Banerji; Great Bath; Dancing Girl; Priest-King.
  • Kalibangan: fire altars; ploughed field (earliest in India).
  • Lothal: dockyard (debated); S.R. Rao, 1955–62.
  • Dholavira: three-part city; 16 reservoirs; UNESCO WHS 2021; R.S. Bisht.
  • Rakhigarhi: largest site (~350 ha); Haryana; 2019 DNA study.
  • Chanhu-daro: no citadel; bead-making capital.
  • Surkotada: horse bones (earliest Harappan horse evidence, debated).

Exam Traps

  • Rakhigarhi (Haryana) is the largest site, NOT Mohenjo-daro.
  • Chanhu-daro has no citadel — the only major Harappan city without one.
  • Dholavira received UNESCO WHS status in 2021, not earlier (Ajanta/Ellora were 1983; confuse risk).
  • Surkotada horse bones are debated — don't present as established fact.
