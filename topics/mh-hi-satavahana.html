Satavahanas, Vakatakas, Rashtrakutas, Yadavas PREMAINSINTMH

Overview

The Satavahana dynasty (c. 230 BCE – 220 CE) was the first great indigenous empire of the Deccan, with its capital at Pratishthana (Paithan, Aurangabad). They were followed by the Vakatakas (3rd–5th century CE), Rashtrakutas (8th–10th century), and Yadavas (Devagiri) (9th–14th century) — all ruling from Maharashtra. Together, these four dynasties span over 1,500 years of Maharashtra's ancient-medieval history and are a standard MPSC Maharashtra History section.

Gautamiputra Satakarni (also Gautamiputra Satkarni) is the most celebrated Satavahana ruler:

  • Defeated the Western Satraps (Sakas) under Nahapana — recaptured western territories.
  • His mother Gautami Balashri's inscription at Nasik describes his achievements.
  • He was called ekabamhana (supporter of Brahmana order) and controlled territory from Malwa to Deccan.

Nanaghat Pass and Inscription:

  • The Nanaghat cave inscription (Junnar, Pune district) is Maharashtra's earliest historical inscription — Queen Nayanika (Nagnika), wife of Satavahana king Satakarni I, commissioned this inscription (~1st century BCE) recording donations after Vedic sacrifices (yajnas).
  • Nanaghat was the main trade pass between Kalyan (Konkan port) and the Deccan.

Vakatakas (c. 250 – 500 CE)

  • Succeeded the Satavahanas in parts of the Deccan.
  • Capital: Nandivardhana (Nagpur region) and Vatsagulma (Washim district).
  • Key ruler: Prabhavati Gupta — daughter of Chandragupta II (Gupta emperor), married into Vakataka dynasty; effective regent; major Gupta-Vakataka alliance.
  • Contribution: Ajanta Caves (Phase II): The most spectacular cave paintings at Ajanta (Aurangabad) were executed during the Vakataka period, especially under Harishena (~460–480 CE). The caves are UNESCO World Heritage.
  • Vakatakas also patronised Sanskrit literature.

Rashtrakutas (c. 753 – 982 CE)

  • Capital: Manyakheta (Malkhed, Gulbarga area, now Karnataka).
  • Founder: Dantidurga (c. 753 CE) — defeated the Chalukyas of Badami.
  • Key rulers: Amoghavarsha I (814–878 CE) — longest-reigning; author of Kavirajamarga (Kannada); built Manyakheta; known for tolerance.
  • Ellora Caves: The Kailasa Temple (Cave 16) at Ellora — carved under Krishna I (Rashtrakuta, c. 756–773 CE); largest rock-cut monolithic structure in the world; dedicated to Shiva.
  • Controlled most of the Deccan, Gujarat, and pushed into north India; fought with Palas, Gurjara-Pratiharas.

Yadavas of Devagiri (c. 850 – 1317 CE)

  • Capital: Devagiri (Daulatabad, Aurangabad district) — a hill fort controlling the Deccan.
  • Key rulers: Bhillama V (founded the Yadava kingdom in Deccan, ~1185); Singhana (Singhanadeva) (1210–1246) — greatest Yadava ruler; extended to Gujarat, Karnataka.
  • Dnyaneshwar: Sant Dnyaneshwar wrote the Dnyaneshwari in 1290 CE during the Yadava period at Nevase, Ahmednagar — foundational text of Marathi literature and Warkari movement.
  • Fall (1317 CE): Malik Kafur (general of Alauddin Khilji) raided Devagiri in 1296; Alauddin finally defeated and executed the last Yadava king Ramachandra and later Harapala in 1317 CE — end of Yadava rule; Deccan came under Delhi Sultanate.

Maharashtra Connection

All four dynasties have capital cities or major sites in Maharashtra: Paithan (Satavahana), Washim/Nagpur area (Vakataka), Ellora-Aurangabad area (Rashtrakuta), Devagiri-Daulatabad (Yadava). The Ajanta Caves (Vakataka) and Ellora's Kailasa Temple (Rashtrakuta) are Maharashtra's crown UNESCO World Heritage monuments. Yadava rule connects to the rise of Marathi and the Warkari movement.

Key Facts

  • Satavahana capital: Pratishthana (Paithan, Aurangabad); founder: Simuka (~230 BCE)
  • Greatest Satavahana ruler: Gautamiputra Satakarni (defeated Saka Nahapana)
  • Nanaghat inscription: Queen Nayanika, Junnar (Pune) — earliest MH inscription
  • Vakataka: Ajanta Caves Phase II painted under Harishena (~460–480 CE)
  • Rashtrakuta: Kailasa Temple (Cave 16, Ellora) — built by Krishna I (~756–773 CE)
  • Rashtrakuta greatest king: Amoghavarsha I (814–878 CE)
  • Yadava capital: Devagiri (Daulatabad, Aurangabad); Dnyaneshwari written 1290 during Yadava period
  • Yadava defeat: 1317 CE by Alauddin Khilji's forces

Exam Traps

  • Ajanta Caves Phase I (Hinayana) predates the Satavahanas slightly or is early Satavahana; Phase II (Mahayana) with famous paintings is Vakataka — not Satavahana or Gupta (though Gupta era overlap).
  • Kailasa Temple builder is Krishna I (Rashtrakuta), NOT the Yadavas or later rulers.
  • Daulatabad (Devagiri) is in Aurangabad district — NOT in Nashik or Pune.
