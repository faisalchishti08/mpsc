Tourist places, hill stations, pilgrimage centres PREMAINSINTMH

Overview

Maharashtra is one of India's richest tourism states, offering hill stations in the Sahyadri/Western Ghats, pilgrimage centres linked to Varkari and other traditions, cave heritage (Ajanta-Ellora), coastal beaches, and wildlife sanctuaries. Tourism geography appears frequently in MPSC Prelims as match-the-following or district-to-place questions and in Mains as regional development and heritage-conservation angles.

Pilgrimage Centres

Maharashtra hosts some of Hinduism's most important shrines:

  • Pandharpur (Solapur): Shrine of Vitthal/Vithoba — spiritual heart of the Varkari movement; annual Wari (pilgrimage) on Ashadhi and Kartiki Ekadashi draws millions.
  • Shirdi (Ahmednagar/Shri Sai Nagar): Shrine of Sai Baba; one of India's highest-earning temples.
  • Trimbakeshwar (Nashik): One of the 12 Jyotirlingas of Shiva; source of Godavari river.
  • Bhimashankar (Pune/Raigad): Another Jyotirlinga; also a Wildlife Sanctuary.
  • Aundha Nagnath (Hingoli): Another Jyotirlinga; said to be the oldest.
  • Grishneshwar (Aurangabad/Chhatrapati Sambhajinagar): 12th and last Jyotirlinga; near Ellora.
  • Nanded (Hazur Sahib): One of the 5 Takhts of Sikhism — Sachkhand Sri Hazur Abchalnagar Sahib.
  • Ganpatipule (Ratnagiri): Famous Ganapati shrine on the coast.
  • Kolhapur — Mahalakshmi Temple: One of the Shaktipeethas.
  • Tuljapur (Osmanabad/Dharashiv): Tuljabhavani — Kuldevi of Marathas; another Shaktipeeth.
  • Vani — Saptashringi (Nashik): Major Devi shrine.
  • Jejuri (Pune): Khandoba temple — deity of Marathas and many communities.

Heritage and UNESCO Sites

  • Ajanta Caves (Aurangabad dist.): 30 rock-cut Buddhist caves (2nd c. BCE – 6th c. CE); UNESCO World Heritage Site (1983). Famous for murals.
  • Ellora Caves (Aurangabad dist.): 34 caves — Buddhist, Hindu, Jain; UNESCO WHS (1983). Kailasa temple (Cave 16) is the world's largest monolithic rock-cut structure.
  • Elephanta Caves (Mumbai Harbour): UNESCO WHS (1987); Shiva sculptures (Trimurti).
  • Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Terminus (CSMT) (Mumbai): UNESCO WHS (2004); Victorian Gothic architecture.
  • Victorian & Art Deco Ensemble of Mumbai: UNESCO WHS (2018).

Beaches and Coastal Tourism

Konkan coast: Alibag, Ganpatipule, Tarkarli (coral reef snorkelling), Vengurla, Murud-Janjira (sea fort). Murud-Janjira fort — the only fort in Maharashtra never conquered.

Wildlife / Eco-tourism

Tadoba-Andhari (Chandrapur) — Maharashtra's premier tiger reserve; Melghat (Amravati) — first Project Tiger reserve in Maharashtra (1974); Pench (Nagpur); Navegaon-Nagzira (Gondia-Bhandara); Radhanagari (Kolhapur) — bison sanctuary.

Maharashtra Connection

Tourism is integral to Maharashtra's economy and identity. The state has 3 UNESCO World Heritage Sites (Ajanta, Ellora, Elephanta) plus 2 urban-heritage sites in Mumbai. The Varkari pilgrimage is the largest periodic religious gathering in the Deccan. Hill stations such as Matheran and Mahabaleshwar drew British-era significance and continue as major revenue earners.

Key Facts

  • Mahabaleshwar: highest hill station, source of 5 rivers (Krishna, Venna, Savitri, Koyna, Gayatri).
  • Matheran: automobile-free; toy train.
  • Chikhaldara: only coffee hill station in Vidarbha.
  • Maharashtra Jyotirlingas: Trimbakeshwar, Bhimashankar, Aundha Nagnath, Grishneshwar (4 of 12).
  • Ajanta & Ellora: UNESCO 1983; Elephanta: UNESCO 1987.
  • Kailasa Temple (Ellora Cave 16): world's largest monolithic rock-cut structure.
  • Murud-Janjira: only undefeated fort on Maharashtra coast.
  • Nanded Hazur Sahib: one of 5 Sikh Takhts.
  • Tadoba: leading tiger reserve; Melghat: first Project Tiger reserve (1974) in Maharashtra.

Memory Aids

  • Jyotirlingas in MH (4): "T-B-A-G" — Trimbakeshwar (Nashik), Bhimashankar (Pune), Aundha Nagnath (Hingoli), Grishneshwar (Aurangabad).
  • Hill stations altitude order: Mahabaleshwar (1438m) > Chikhaldara > Matheran — or "Maha-Chi-Math".
  • UNESCO trio: Ajanta-Ellora (1983), Elephanta (1987), CSMT (2004), Art Deco (2018).

Exam Traps

  • Trimbakeshwar is source of Godavari; Mahabaleshwar is source of Krishna — don't swap rivers.
  • Bhimashankar is both a Jyotirlinga AND a Wildlife Sanctuary — MPSC pairs these as MCQs.
  • Matheran is automobile-free; do NOT confuse with "pedestrian-only" or claim UNESCO WHS status — it is only on the Tentative List.
  • Nanded is a Sikh Takht, not merely a gurudwara.
