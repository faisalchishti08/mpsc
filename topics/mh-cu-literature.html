Marathi literature and Jnanpith awardees PREMAINSINTMH

Overview

Marathi literature is one of India's oldest continuous literary traditions, dating to the 11th–12th century CE (Jnandev's era, with Dnyaneshwari at 1290 CE). Maharashtra has produced 5 Jnanpith Award (India's highest literary honour) winners in Marathi — the most of any language after Hindi/Kannada. Key eras: Bhakti (1200s–1700s, Varkari saints), Modern Reform era (1800s, Phule-Agarkar), and Contemporary (post-Independence). The Marathi language received Classical Language status from the Central Government in October 2024. MPSC tests Jnanpith winners, works, years, and key literary figures.

Marathi Bhasha Din and Classical Status

  • Marathi Bhasha Din: 27 February — birth anniversary of Kusumagraj (V.V. Shirwadkar); celebrated as Marathi language day across Maharashtra.
  • Marathi Classical Language: Government of India granted Marathi Classical Language status in October 2024 — 8th classical language in India (after Tamil, Sanskrit, Kannada, Telugu, Malayalam, Odia, and Pali).
  • Classical status criteria: Antiquity (1,500–2,000 years), distinct literary tradition, literature distinct from borrowed from another language, large body of ancient literature.
  • Key evidence for Marathi's Classical status: Dnyaneshwari (1290), earlier Mahanubhav texts (1270s), Satavahana-era Prakrit inscriptions.

Maharashtra Sahitya Parishad + Akademi

  • Maharashtra Sahitya Parishad (MSP): Founded 1906 (Pune); oldest Marathi literary institution; conducts examinations (Bhasha Ratna, etc.); Pune HQ.
  • Sahitya Akademi: National body; publishes Marathi translations; awards.

Maharashtra Connection

Marathi literature is a living civic tradition — Kusumagraj's birthday is a state holiday (Marathi Bhasha Din). V.S. Khandekar's Yayati is studied in universities across India and translated into 14 languages. Nemade's Kosala (1963) is considered the Marathi equivalent of Catcher in the Rye. Marathi's Classical status in 2024 was a landmark political-cultural moment after decades of advocacy.
