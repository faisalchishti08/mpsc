Agriculture — sugarcane, cotton, jowar, grapes, oranges PREMAINSINTMH

Overview

Agriculture employs approximately 50–55% of Maharashtra's workforce but contributes only ~10–12% to GSDP — highlighting the structural challenge of agrarian distress. Maharashtra is simultaneously a major commercial crop producer (sugarcane, cotton, grapes, onions, turmeric, oranges) and a state with severe farmer suicide crises (Vidarbha/Marathwada). MPSC tests crop geography (district-wise), key GI tags, irrigation coverage, and flagship agri-schemes.

Kharif vs Rabi Pattern in Maharashtra

  • Kharif (June–November): Cotton, soybean, jowar, bajra, rice (Konkan), groundnut, sugarcane (planted).
  • Rabi (November–March): Jowar, wheat (limited), chickpea/gram (Marathwada), sunflower.
  • Maharashtra is predominantly kharif-dependent — 60–65% of cultivated area under kharif.

Irrigation Challenge

  • Only ~20–22% of Maharashtra's net sown area is under irrigation (national average ~50%) — a key vulnerability.
  • Sugarcane (water-intensive) uses disproportionate irrigation share in western Maharashtra while Vidarbha remains rain-fed.
  • Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana (PMKSY) and Jalyukt Shivar Abhiyan address this gap.

Agrarian Distress — Vidarbha/Marathwada

  • Vidarbha cotton farmers have been in crisis since early 2000s — documented by P. Sainath (journalist) in "Everybody Loves a Good Drought" and extensive reporting.
  • Farmer suicides: Maharashtra consistently among top 3 states; Yavatmal, Amravati, Nanded hotspots.
  • Causes: input cost rise (Bt cotton seeds + pesticides), erratic rainfall, low MSP, indebtedness.
  • Loan waivers: Maharashtra has declared farm loan waivers multiple times (2001, 2008, 2017 Chhatrapati Shivaji Shetkari Sanman Yojana — ₹34,000 crore).

Key GI Tags in Maharashtra Agriculture

Alphonso Mango (Ratnagiri + Devgad), Nashik Grapes, Nagpur Orange, Sangli Turmeric, Solapur Pomegranate, Jalgaon Banana, Kolhapur Jaggery, Waghad (Nashik) chilli, Marathwada Kesar Mango (Aurangabad).

Maharashtra Connection

Western Maharashtra's cooperative sugar belt is a major political force — sugar cooperative chairmen are often MLAs/MPs. Onion price crashes trigger political crises (2010, 2013, 2019 famously affected elections). The Nashik grape export to Europe via Mahagrapes is Maharashtra's agri-export success story. Vidarbha farmer distress is a perennial Mains topic and links to Vidarbha statehood demand.

  • Onion: Lasalgaon (Nashik) = Asia's largest onion APMC market

Exam Traps

  • Maharashtra is NOT the top sugar producer by volume (UP is); Maharashtra ranks 2nd in area and production.
  • Jowar: Maharashtra is historically top, but annual rankings fluctuate — "traditionally 1st" is safe.
  • Alphonso GI tag is for Ratnagiri and Devgad (two separate GI registrations) — not all Konkan.
