Jyotiba & Savitribai Phule, Satyashodhak Samaj PREMAINSINTMH

Overview

Mahatma Jyotiba Phule (1827–1890) and Savitribai Phule (1831–1897) are the founders of Maharashtra's social reform movement — pioneers of education for women, Dalits, and backward classes in 19th-century Pune. Jyotiba founded the Satyashodhak Samaj (1873) to challenge Brahmanical hegemony and caste hierarchy. This is perhaps the most important Maharashtra social reform topic for MPSC — tested every exam cycle.

Savitribai Phule — Pioneer Educator

  • Born: 3 January 1831, Naigaon (Satara district).
  • India's first woman teacher: Taught at the girls' school opened by Jyotiba in 1848 — she taught while Brahmin men pelted her with stones and dung (she carried a spare sari to change into); her perseverance is one of India's great acts of social courage.
  • Founded Mahila Seva Mandal (1852) for women's rights.
  • Widow remarriage: Savitribai supported and facilitated widow remarriages — actively subverted social norms.
  • 1897 Bubonic Plague: Savitribai opened a plague care centre near Pune; she personally carried patients on her back; contracted the plague herself and died 10 March 1897.
  • Her birthday (3 January) is observed as Balika Din / Teachers' Day in Maharashtra (separate from national Teacher's Day on 5 September).
  • Savitribai Phule Pune University (formerly University of Pune) was renamed in her honour in 2014.

Satyashodhak Samaj (1873)

  • Founded: 24 September 1873, Pune, by Jyotiba Phule.
  • Goal: Challenge Brahmin-led priesthood; promote self-respect; argue that God is the father of ALL humans regardless of caste; reject the authority of Vedas/Brahmins as mediators between God and people.
  • Satyashodhak weddings: Non-Brahmin weddings without pandit (priest); bride and groom exchanged vows directly; a radical social practice.
  • Membership: Primarily from Shudra and Ati-Shudra (untouchable) communities; also some Muslims and Christians joined.
  • Literary wing: Phule used Marathi poetry, tamasha (folk theatre), and writings to spread messages.
  • Legacy: Inspired the non-Brahmin movement; direct ideological precursor to Ambedkar's work; shaped the later Bahishkrit Hitakarini Sabha (Ambedkar, 1924).

Key Writings of Jyotiba Phule

  • Gulamgiri (1873) — "Slavery"; dedicated to abolitionists of America; compared the condition of shudras/untouchables in India to that of Black slaves in America; foundational text.
  • Shetkaryacha Asud (1881) — "The Cultivator's Whipcord"; critiqued the exploitation of peasants by Brahmins, moneylenders, and the colonial revenue system.
  • Sarvajanik Satyadharma Pustak (1891) — published posthumously; universal religion of truth; challenged sectarian religion.

Maharashtra Connection

11 April (Jyotiba Phule's birthday) is observed as Samarasata Din / Jyotiba Phule Jayanti in Maharashtra. Savitribai Phule Pune University (SPPU) — renamed 2014 — is Maharashtra's largest university. The Phule couple is enshrined in Maharashtra's school textbooks and civic memory as the ultimate symbols of social transformation. Their work directly challenged the social order upheld by the Peshwai system that Ambedkar also criticised.
