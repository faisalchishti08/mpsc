State emblem, song and language PREMAINSINTMH

Overview

Maharashtra's state identity is expressed through its official emblem, the state song (Rajya Geet), and the state language (Marathi). These are recurring MPSC Prelims recall items and carry importance in the context of Maharashtra's cultural and administrative identity.

In-Depth

State Emblem

Maharashtra's official state emblem features an ancient gold coin (mohur) of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj as its central motif — a rising sun symbol with the Devanagari inscription "महाराष्ट्र शासन" (Maharashtra Shasana / Government of Maharashtra) below. The emblem is used on all official state documents, letterheads, and government seals.

The motif links Maharashtra's modern governance to the legacy of Chhatrapati Shivaji and the Maratha Empire, reinforcing cultural continuity.

State Song — Jai Jai Maharashtra Majha

  • Official Maharashtra Rajya Geet: "Jai Jai Maharashtra Majha"
  • Written by Raja Badhe (lyricist)
  • Set to music/composed — the song is sung on state occasions such as Maharashtra Din (1 May), government functions, and school assemblies.
  • It celebrates Maharashtra's geography (rivers, mountains), culture (saints, warriors), and people.

State Language — Marathi

  • Marathi is the official state language, spoken by ~83% of Maharashtra's population.
  • It is an Indo-Aryan language with a literary tradition dating to the 13th century Yadava period — the Dnyaneshwari (1290 AD) by Sant Dnyaneshwar (a commentary on the Bhagavad Gita in Marathi) is its foundational text.
  • Marathi literature is enriched by the Warkari tradition (Dnyaneshwar, Namdev, Eknath, Tukaram) and the Shahirs (poet-composers of the Samyukta Maharashtra Movement like Annabhau Sathe, Amar Sheikh).
  • Script: Devanagari (same as Hindi, but distinct orthographic conventions).
  • Marathi was central to the demand for a separate Maharashtra state — the Samyukta Maharashtra Movement was fundamentally a linguistic movement.

Marathi's Classical Status

There is a long-standing demand to accord Marathi the status of a Classical Language (alongside Tamil, Sanskrit, Kannada, Telugu, Malayalam, Odia). As of 2024, the Government of India declared Marathi a Classical Language — a significant development for MPSC aspirants to note.

Maharashtra Connection

The state emblem's Shivaji motif, the Marathi language, and the Rajya Geet together form the triad of Maharashtra's official cultural identity. The Dnyaneshwari, written at Nevase (Ahmednagar district) in 1290, is a UNESCO-recognized literary heritage. Marathi's classical status campaign was strongly supported by the Maharashtra government and literary organisations (Sahitya Mahamandal).

Exam Traps

  • The state song is "Jai Jai Maharashtra Majha" — NOT "Jai Jai Maharashtra Majhi" (gender of the word varies in usage but official title uses "Majha").
  • Dnyaneshwari is a commentary on the Bhagavad Gita, NOT the Ramayana or Vedas.
  • Marathi's Classical Language status was granted in 2024 — check for year accuracy; older exams may not have this data.
