Samyukta Maharashtra movement and martyrs PREMAINSINTMH

Overview

The Samyukta Maharashtra Movement (1946–1960) was the mass agitation demanding a unified Marathi-speaking state with Mumbai as its capital. It culminated in the formation of Maharashtra on 1 May 1960 after 105 protesters were killed at Hutatma Chowk (Flora Fountain, Mumbai) during police firing. This is one of Maharashtra's most emotionally and politically charged topics — tested heavily in MPSC for dates, leaders, martyrs, and the creation of Maharashtra.

In-Depth

Background — Linguistic States Demand

  • After Independence, the Indian National Congress had promised linguistic reorganisation of states — specifically relevant to Marathi-speakers scattered across Bombay Province, Berar (Central Provinces), and Hyderabad.
  • The Dhar Commission (1948) opposed purely linguistic states (would disrupt national unity, it argued).
  • JVP Committee (1949): Nehru, Vallabhbhai Patel, Pattabhi Sitaramayya — also opposed linguistic reorganisation.
  • Death of Potti Sriramulu (1952) after a 56-day fast forced the creation of Andhra (the first linguistic state, 1953) — galvanised similar demands across India.
  • States Reorganisation Commission (SRC, 1953): Chair: Fazl Ali; Members: H.N. Kunzru, K.M. Panikkar; Report: 1955.

SRC Report (1955) and the "Bombay Problem"

  • The SRC recommended a bilingual Bombay State rather than splitting it into Maharashtra and Gujarat.
  • Primary reason: Bombay city was cosmopolitan, inhabited by Marathi, Gujarati, and other speakers; both communities claimed it.
  • This recommendation triggered massive protests.

Police Firing and 105 Martyrs

  • Between 1955 and 1960, multiple rounds of protests and police firings occurred in Mumbai.
  • The most significant firing: 21 November 1955 and subsequent incidents.
  • 105 protesters were killed across various incidents.
  • The martyrdom site — Flora Fountain (now Hutatma Chowk = Martyrs' Square) — became Maharashtra's most powerful political symbol. The sculptures of protesters were erected at Hutatma Chowk.
  • "Hutatma" = martyr (Marathi); "Chowk" = square.

Parallel Gujarat Movement

  • Simultaneously, Gujarati-speakers demanded a separate Gujarat state — Mahagujarat Movement.
  • Both movements ran parallel; their demands were complementary (both wanted out of bilingual Bombay).

Bombay Reorganisation Act, 1960

  • Parliament passed the Bombay Reorganisation Act, 1960.
  • On 1 May 1960 ("Maharashtra Din"), Bombay State was bifurcated into:
  • Maharashtra (capital: Mumbai) — Marathi-speaking
  • Gujarat (capital: initially Ahmedabad, later Gandhinagar) — Gujarati-speaking
  • First Chief Minister of Maharashtra: Yashwantrao Balwantrao Chavan (Y.B. Chavan) — also called "Shilpkar of modern Maharashtra" (architect of modern Maharashtra); later became India's Defence Minister and Home Minister.
  • First Governor of Maharashtra: Sri Prakasa.

Aftermath and Legacy

  • 1 May = Maharashtra Din (Maharashtra Day) — state holiday; celebrated with cultural programmes, flag hoisting.
  • The movement highlighted that language was a legitimate basis for state formation — it solidified the principle of linguistic states in India.
  • The Maharashtra–Karnataka border dispute (Belgaum/Belagavi) traces directly to the 1960 reorganisation — areas with Marathi-speaking population that were awarded to Karnataka remain contested.
  • Acharya Atre's legacy: His satirical writings and speeches shaped Marathi public culture; his role is sometimes compared to Ireland's Eamon de Valera or India's Tilak.

Maharashtra Connection

Everything about this topic is directly Maharashtra. Hutatma Chowk (Flora Fountain, South Mumbai) is where the martyrs are commemorated — a pilgrimage site for Maharashtrians. The movement's leaders (Atre, S.M. Joshi, Jedhe) are enshrined in Maharashtra's historical consciousness. The Y.B. Chavan Centre (Mumbai) is named after the first CM. 1 May = school holiday, state celebrations, and political rallying every year.

Exam Traps

  • SRC recommended bilingual Bombay (NOT separation) — separation came via the 1960 Act, NOT SRC recommendation.
  • 105 martyrs (not 100 or 115) — exact number matters in MPSC MCQs.
  • Y.B. Chavan was first CM of Maharashtra (1960); he had also been CM of bilingual Bombay State — two different positions.
