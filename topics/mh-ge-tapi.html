Tapi-Purna system PREMAINSINTMH

Overview

The Tapi (Tapti) river is one of India's major west-flowing rivers, draining the northern Deccan plateau and flowing westward into the Gulf of Khambhat (Cambay) in Gujarat. The Purna is its most important Maharashtra tributary. The Tapi system drains the Khandesh region (Jalgaon, Dhule, Nandurbar) and parts of Amravati (Vidarbha). MPSC tests origin, direction of flow (west — unusual for Deccan rivers), the Hatnur Dam, and the Purna sub-system.

West-Flowing Nature of Tapi

  • The Tapi (like the Narmada) flows in a rift valley / graben created by the faulting of the Deccan Trap — hence it flows west despite being on the Deccan Plateau's north side.
  • This makes it distinct from the Godavari, Krishna, Bhima (which flow east → Bay of Bengal).
  • The Satpura range lies to its north; the Ajanta range lies to its south — Tapi is sandwiched in the Khandesh lowland.

Purna River — Key Tributary

  • Purna originates in the Ajanta-Balaghat range area (Amravati/Buldhana) and flows westward, joining the Tapi near Shendurni (Jalgaon).
  • NOTE: There is ALSO a Purna river in Marathwada (tributary of Godavari via Penganga); these are TWO DIFFERENT rivers sharing the same name — a classic MPSC confusion trap.
  • The Purna (Tapi tributary) drains Akola, Amravati, Buldhana, and Jalgaon.
  • Katepurna Dam (Akola district, on Katepurna tributary of Purna): Irrigation project in Vidarbha.

Girna River

  • Girna rises in the Sahyadri (Nashik) and flows northeast, joining the Tapi near Jalgaon.
  • Girna Dam (Jalgaon district): Major irrigation dam; supplies water to Nashik and Jalgaon districts.
  • Jalgaon (on Girna-Tapi plain) is the Banana Capital of India — bananas grown extensively on the Tapi-Girna alluvial plains.

Hatnur Dam

  • Hatnur Dam (also spelled Hathnur): Built across Tapi in Jalgaon district, near the Maharashtra-Maharashtra border... actually near Shahada (Nandurbar) and Jalgaon.
  • Used for irrigation and is a medium-sized dam.
  • The Tapi in Jalgaon is also flood-prone — devastating floods in Jalgaon have occurred periodically.

Aner Dam

  • Aner Dam (Dhule district): On the Aner tributary of Tapi; water supply to Dhule city and irrigation.

Religious Significance

  • Tapi is personified as the daughter of Surya (Sun god) in Hindu mythology — hence also called Tapti/Surya Putri.
  • Multai (origin, MP) has a sacred tank and temple to Tapti.
  • Surat (Gujarat, at the mouth): Historic port city at Tapi's mouth — major Mughal-era port.

Maharashtra Connection

The Tapi basin coincides with Khandesh — culturally distinct, the "Land of cotton and banana." Jalgaon district on the Tapi plain is India's banana capital (Maharashtra produces ~28–30% of India's bananas). The Tapi basin is in the rain shadow of the Sahyadri on its southern side and the Satpura on its north — it receives 600–800 mm of rain, making irrigation crucial. The Khandesh Anandvan (Baba Amte's social work base at Hemalkasa, Gadchiroli — not Khandesh but same Tapi-zone social ecology) and cotton history of the region are linked to MPSC questions.

Exam Traps

  • Tapi flows WEST (not east like Godavari/Krishna) — a frequent direction confusion.
  • Two rivers named Purna in Maharashtra: one joins Tapi (western), one joins Godavari via Penganga (eastern) — do NOT mix them.
  • Tapi originates in Madhya Pradesh (Betul), NOT in Maharashtra — it enters Maharashtra from MP.
